## CITATION AUDIT CORRECTIONS (v8.0)

The following corrections apply to citations flagged in the v7 citation audit:

**CIT-01 — §2.11 OFS: MCAS prevalence (REVISED)**
> Molderings, G.J., et al. (2011). Systemic mast cell activation disease: The German perspective. *Journal of Internal Medicine*, 271(1), 1–11. ⚠
> **Correction:** The 17% prevalence figure from Molderings (2011) was derived from a selected clinical cohort, not a population survey. Revise all references to this figure: *"MCAS prevalence in general population settings is uncertain; estimates range from <1% (population-based) to higher figures in selected clinical cohorts (Molderings et al., 2011). Treat as EMERGING evidence; do not use as primary justification for mainstream design priority."*

**CIT-02 — §1.4/§1.5: Levine et al. (2023) — INCOMPLETE CITATION (UNVERIFIED)**
> Levine, S. et al. (2023). [Grab bar positioning and functional outcomes in post-acute rehabilitation]. KITE Research Institute / University Health Network, Toronto. [Vol 2B: unverified — journal, volume, pages, DOI required before formal publication]
> **Note:** If grey literature, reclassify from Tier 1 to Tier 2 and revise confidence accordingly.

**CIT-03 — §8.3.3: ESG rent premium 25–50% — NO SOURCE (REMOVED)**
> ❌ This claim has no source. Removed from §8.3.3. Retained as: *"Buildings with high ESG performance ratings have in some markets demonstrated rental premium; the size and generalisability of this premium is not established in the current literature and should not be stated as a finding."*

**CIT-04 — §8.3.3: Hangzhou elevator retrofit (CONTEXTUALISED)**
> Buildings (MDPI). (2024). [Housing price effects of elevator installation in multi-storey residential buildings: A Hangzhou case study]. *Buildings*, 14. ✅
> **Correction:** Add caveat: *"This figure (5.53% mean price increase) is from a single Chinese city study (2024) and should be treated as jurisdiction-specific and context-dependent rather than as a general finding."*
