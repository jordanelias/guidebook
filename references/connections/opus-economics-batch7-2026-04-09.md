### CON-0162

**Status:** PENDING
**Confidence:** HIGH
**Opus-reviewed:** true
**Source BPC slug(s):** accessible-design-economics-cost-premium, government-grant-programmes-home-adaptation
**Target item(s):** Part 11 §11.x
**Target population(s):** ALL
**Evidence tier:** Tier 3–5
**Filed:** 2026-04-09
**Applied:** —

**Connection:** All government adaptation programmes fund retrofit exclusively — no programme funds design-stage construction. UK DFG spends £711m/year on retrofits averaging £12,000–15,000/dwelling. If those dwellings had been built accessibly, the cost would have been ~£2,000–4,000/dwelling (1% of median build cost). The grant budget would fund 3–7× more accessible homes if spent at construction. This policy misallocation is the strongest policy argument in the guidebook but is not calculated in any BPC.

**Evidence basis:** Economics BPC (0.94–3.92% new-build). Grant BPC (£711m/year DFG). KfW/Prognos (€19,100/dwelling retrofit, 700,000+ dwellings). Visitability ($100–600).

**Action required:** Part 11: calculate policy misallocation explicitly. Present as: redirect 20% of retrofit budget → funds [N] new accessible dwellings annually at 0.94% premium.

**Disposition notes:** — Strongest single policy argument. Arithmetic synthesis, ○ marker.
