## Key Findings (for BPC)

**F-D1 — Wet room / open-plan bathroom: alignment between accessible principle and dominant Global South typology:**
The zero-threshold open-plan wet room with floor drain IS the most accessible bathroom configuration (existing BPC confirmed). It is ALSO the dominant bathroom typology across South and Southeast Asia, MENA, and Sub-Saharan Africa. This means the Global South accessible bathroom challenge is not "adopt a foreign typology" — it is "specify the accessible elements within the existing open-plan typology." Key specifications needed within open-plan context: floor gradient toward drain (≥1:50, not steeper than 1:40 to allow wheelchair traction), non-slip ceramic or sealed concrete floor surface (wet COF ≥0.5), grab bar at WC, fold-down shower seat, accessible washbasin height. These are all achievable at low additional cost within standard local construction.

**F-D2 — Squat toilet: the primary unresolved accessible design problem:**
PubMed returns zero results for accessible squat toilet design. The Sejong Toilet (Indonesia, 2022) is the only documented participatory DPO-led accessible adaptation of a squat toilet. The RESNA 2012 India paper is the clearest statement of the mismatch: accessible standards specify seated WC but most Indians use squat latrines; most mobility-impaired Indians are polio survivors without wheelchairs. MIUSA confirms 4 billion people globally use squat toilets and documents informal adaptations (wheelchair over squat pan, curtain privacy, wheel removal). No design standards exist for accessible squat toilet provision anywhere.

**F-D3 — Japan split facility: accessible design model for culturally separate toilet and bathroom:**
Japan has the most comprehensive accessible split-facility design in Asia. Key elements: toilet near bedroom (heat shock prevention), sliding or outward-opening door, L-shaped grab rail, ≥750mm door width (≥800mm WC), toilet room length ≥1,300mm with ≥500mm assistance space, step-free pathway between bathroom and toilet. The 多目的トイレ (multipurpose toilet) is a gender-neutral, fully-equipped public accessible toilet with ostomate sink, change table, automatic flush, grab rails — now mandated by the Barrier-Free New Law 2006. This is the most advanced non-Western public accessible toilet standard.

**F-D4 — Shared/communal facility: design gap confirmed:**
Shared sanitation is the dominant mode for over 1 billion informal settlement residents. Systematic review (93 studies, 22 countries, 2025) confirms disability is a named vulnerability group but addresses only water access and maintenance, not accessible design of the sanitation fixture or space. No accessible design specifications exist for shared sanitation facilities in informal settlements anywhere.

**F-D5 — Design principle confirmed regardless of typology:**
Across all typologies, the core accessible bathroom principles remain constant: (1) zero/minimal threshold at entry; (2) non-slip wet floor surface; (3) adequate manoeuvre space (≥1500mm turning circle where wheelchair); (4) grab bar or stable support at WC or squat fixture; (5) reachable controls from seated/assisted position. The typology variance is in how these are achieved, not whether they apply.
