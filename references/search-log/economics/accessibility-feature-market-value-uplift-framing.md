## Provisional findings summary (session 2026-05-02)

The user's framing-effect hypothesis ("value uplift as consequence of accessible techniques being understood as something else") receives **mixed support across the evidence base examined this session**:

**Supported:**
- Industry universally relabels accessibility as universal design, aging-in-place, livable, life-course, lifecycle-proof, wellness, spa, open-concept, ranch, single-level. The relabeling IS the operating market norm.
- Conceptual literature (Carr et al. 2013 JAR; Mace; Steinfeld) explicitly states UD framing reduces stigma vs accessibility framing.

**Refuted at rigorous-WTP level:**
- SINTEF 2019 (Norway, commissioned by Husbanken): no measurable WTP for basic life-course housing qualities; some WTP for spaciousness features in larger units only; developers do not believe Husbanken loan generates higher selling price.
- UK 2022 DLUHC IA: M4(2) NPV spans −£4.66B to +£1.93B over 70 years; size-correlated price uplift exists but is not isolable from accessibility framing per se.
- AU LHA voluntary 2011→2020 universal-Silver target failed without regulatory mandate (states moved to NCC 2022 H8 mandate). If relabeling alone produced uplift, voluntary uptake would have happened.

**Most likely synthesis** (PROVISIONAL):
Accessibility's measurable market premium is captured through co-bundled features (size, daylight, openness, single-level) that have independent market value, while the relabeling operates as supply-side marketing consciousness rather than measurable demand-side WTP differential.
