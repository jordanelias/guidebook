## Key Findings (Session 1)

**F-B1 (confirmed):** Egypt (5/21 studies in Bertone 2021) and Malaysia (ASPECTSS applications) are the only non-Western jurisdictions with peer-reviewed autism built environment design research. All other Global South jurisdictions: zero published research on sensory space design.

**F-B2 (confirmed):** Japan has an active calm-down space research stream (Tsukuba 2021/2025). The concept is termed カームダウンスペース / クワイエットスペース. J-STAGE 2025 study uses biodata (heart rate) across Japan/USA/France — this is Tier 1 built-environment sensory space evidence outside UK/AU.

**F-B3 (CRITICAL):** Collectivist cultures (JP, KR, IN, MENA, Sub-Saharan Africa, most LAC) present a design transfer problem for the PAS 6463 model. The private individual retreat assumes individualist culture norms. In collectivist contexts: (a) stigma around withdrawal from group is higher; (b) autistic traits may be more heavily masked (PMC10954134); (c) the appropriate design response may be communal quiet zone rather than individual retreat room. No research addresses this directly.

**F-B4 (confirmed):** Chile Ley de Autismo N°21.545 (2023) is the first LAC statutory requirement for sensory accommodation in educational settings. No design specifications beyond mandate.

**F-B5 (confirmed):** Commercial sensory room provision is active in Mexico, Colombia, Chile, Spain — Snoezelen-derived model exported from Netherlands/Europe. No low-resource adaptation evidence. Cost of Snoezelen rooms (USD $10,000–$50,000+) makes them inaccessible in most Global South contexts.

**F-B6 (confirmed design gap):** No research exists on low-resource sensory space alternatives for Global South — e.g., natural materials, simple acoustic treatment, outdoor quiet spaces, ventilated shade structures. The PAS 6463 model assumes mechanically controlled indoor environments (AC, LED dimming, STC 50 partition). None of these are feasible as standard specification in most Global South educational or residential settings.
