## Conflict Domain: PREDICT
**Parameter:** Spatial predictability / reconfigurability
**Status:** MIXED — Opus synthesis complete [2026-03-30]
**Date created:** 2026-03-30
**Opus synthesis:** 2026-03-30
**Feeds:** Part 5 §5.2 conflict resolution table; D-series (wayfinding) and Part 7 matrices

### Opposition Summary

| Pop A need | Pop B need | Classification |
|---|---|---|
| NDV/AUT, DEM: fixed layout, no reconfiguration, consistent furniture arrangement | MOB: flexible, reconfigurable for equipment access (hoist, standing frame, wheelchair transfer) | Tension |

### Evidence Analysis

**NDV/AUT predictability evidence:**
- NDV-AUT BPC: "Predictability — consistent layouts; routine-supporting design; reduced visual complexity" (Tola et al. 2021, Tier 1 scoping review).
- Cognitive-wayfinding BPC: "consistent furniture arrangement within room types; no mirror-image pairs" (Tier 3 + Co-1: DSDC, Alzheimer Nederland).
- PAS 6463:2022: predictable spatial organisation as core NDV design principle.
- The NDV need is genuine and well-evidenced. Unexpected spatial change causes distress, disorientation, and meltdown in autistic users.

**DEM predictability evidence:**
- DEM BPC: "distinctive landmark objects at all decision points; personalised door identifiers for residential rooms" — predictability supports topographic memory.
- Cognitive-wayfinding BPC: "consistent furniture layout critical for DEM tactile memory navigation" (Tier 3 + Co-1).
- DEM users navigate by spatial memory. Layout changes eliminate stored routes and cause severe disorientation.

**MOB reconfigurability evidence:**
- MOB BPC (2026-03-29, Opus synthesis): the MOB need is NOT for reconfigurable layouts. MOB specifications are about ADEQUATE SPACE (turning circles, clear floor areas, door widths) and FIXED ACCESSIBLE FEATURES (grab bars, accessible controls, level surfaces).
- The apparent "flexibility" need in the domain table conflates two distinct requirements:
  1. **Adequate clear floor space** for equipment manoeuvring (hoist access, transfer space) — this is a FIXED spatial provision, not reconfiguration
  2. **Furniture adjustment** at Tier 2 (OT assessment repositions furniture for individual transfer patterns) — this is a ONE-TIME setup, not ongoing reconfiguration

Neither of these requires the kind of ongoing spatial change that disrupts NDV/AUT or DEM predictability.

### Opus Assessment

The apparent conflict is substantially overstated. The MOB need for "reconfigurability" dissolves under closer analysis:

- **Adequate space** (sufficient turning circles, clear zones for equipment) is a FIXED design provision that SUPPORTS predictability. A room with a 1800mm turning circle has predictable open floor space.
- **Tier 2 furniture placement** is a one-time OT-directed setup, not ongoing reconfiguration. Once positioned for the individual, furniture STAYS in place — supporting both MOB access and NDV/DEM predictability.
- **DAR (Design for Adaptable Readiness)** provisions (blocking for future grab bars, reinforced walls, removable cabinetry) are latent adaptability, not visible reconfiguration. They do not change the spatial experience until activated.

### Domain Classification: MIXED (largely compatible)

**Predictable fixed layout sub-parameter: CONVERGENT**
All three populations (NDV/AUT, DEM, MOB) benefit from consistent, predictable spatial arrangement. MOB specifically benefits from knowing where clear floor space is and that it will remain clear.

**Equipment access sub-parameter: COMPATIBLE**
Adequate space for equipment (hoist, standing frame) is achieved through spatial provision (larger rooms, adequate clear zones) — not by removing/rearranging existing layout elements.

**Tier 2 one-time adaptation sub-parameter: COMPATIBLE**
OT-directed furniture placement is a one-time intervention. After setup, the layout is FIXED for that occupant — supporting predictability.

**Multi-occupancy reconfiguration: TENSION**
The only genuine tension arises in spaces that must serve DIFFERENT individuals at different times (e.g., shared clinic rooms, multi-use community spaces) where MOB equipment needs vary per user. In these settings, NDV/AUT and DEM users may encounter different layouts on different visits.

### Resolution

**Resolution 1 — Adequate fixed space (design-out the conflict):**
Specify rooms large enough that equipment access does not require furniture rearrangement. If the turning circle, transfer zone, and hoist track are all achievable with furniture in its standard position, no reconfiguration is needed.

Status: **RESOLVED-CONSENSUS** (MOB BPC: adequate space is the provision; reconfiguration is a workaround for inadequate space)

**Resolution 2 — Consistent configuration protocol for multi-use spaces:**
Multi-use clinic/community rooms: establish a standard layout and reset to it after each use. Document the standard layout visually (wall-mounted room plan). NDV/AUT and DEM users can orient to the standard layout; MOB equipment needs are met within it.

Status: **RESOLUTION-PROPOSED** (clinical plausibility; no direct evidence)

**Resolution 3 — DAR as latent adaptability:**
DAR provisions (Part 10) provide future adaptability without altering current spatial experience. Reinforced walls, service voids, removable cabinetry are INVISIBLE to occupants until activated. They support MOB future adaptation needs without compromising NDV/DEM predictability.

Status: **RESOLVED-CONSENSUS** (DAR framework is a core guidebook principle)
