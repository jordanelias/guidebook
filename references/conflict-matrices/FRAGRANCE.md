## Conflict Domain: FRAGRANCE

### Opposition Map (corrected from skill domain table)

The original domain table describes the opposition as "NDV/SENS, OFS/MCAS: zero fragrance" vs "DEM (olfactory therapy): scented cues for orientation."

Opus assessment: the DEM olfactory therapy claim is overstated for built environment specification purposes.

**DEM olfactory therapy — evidence review:**
- The DEM BPC (2026-03-29, Opus synthesis) does NOT include olfactory therapy as a wayfinding or spatial design provision. DEM wayfinding provisions are: loop circulation, 3D object landmarks, colour coding, lighting, tactile cues.
- Olfactory therapy for dementia (aromatherapy — lavender, lemon balm) is a clinical intervention delivered by care staff, not a built environment specification. It uses topical application or diffusers in controlled clinical settings.
- No BPC, standard, or design guideline reviewed recommends embedding scented cues as a built environment wayfinding mechanism.
- The DEM BPC consensus findings include NO reference to olfactory wayfinding. The "scented cues for orientation" entry in the conflict domain table appears to derive from a general association between dementia care and aromatherapy, not from spatial design evidence.

**NDV/SENS + OFS/MCAS fragrance-free — evidence review:**
- Air-quality BPC (2026-03-30): fragrance-free policy is an operational overlay. All spaces serving MCAS/MCS/PCS populations require documented fragrance-free policy.
- Chemical sensitivity prevalence: ~12.8% US adults report sensitivity; ~3.9% MCS diagnosis (Steinemann 2018, Tier 3).
- NDV BPC: "controllable; avoid scented materials" (Tier 5, PAS 6463:2022).
- Air-quality BPC: "No fragrance diffusers (electronic, passive, or HVAC-integrated) in OFS-occupied environments."

### Domain Classification: MINIMAL CONFLICT (approaching convergent elimination)

The purported DEM olfactory wayfinding need lacks evidence as a BUILT ENVIRONMENT specification. Olfactory therapy is a clinical care intervention, not a spatial design parameter. No design guideline or standard recommends architectural scent installation for dementia wayfinding.

The only genuine tension is:
- **Biophilic planting in DEM care settings** (gardens, courtyards — established DEM therapeutic benefit) may include aromatic plants that trigger OFS/MCAS symptoms.
- Air-quality BPC already addresses this: "low-VOC, low-pollen species only in OFS-occupied spaces; no strongly scented flowering plants."

### Resolution

**Resolution 1 — Fragrance-free as near-universal:**
No population in the guidebook requires architectural fragrance. Fragrance-free specification (F-02 policy + F-04 physical) serves NDV/SENS, OFS/MCAS, and all other populations without harm.

Status: **CONVERGENT** (fragrance elimination serves all; no population harmed)

**Resolution 2 — Biophilic planting species selection:**
DEM garden therapy uses aromatic herbs (lavender, rosemary) as sensory stimulation. Where OFS/MCAS users share the facility:
- Outdoor gardens: aromatic planting acceptable (open-air dissipation)
- Indoor planting: low-scent, low-pollen species only (air-quality BPC specification)
- Controlled aromatherapy: clinical intervention by staff in designated rooms with enhanced ventilation, not architectural specification

Status: **RESOLVED-CONSENSUS** (indoor/outdoor distinction; architectural specification = fragrance-free; clinical aromatherapy = operational protocol)
