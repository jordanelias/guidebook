## Conflict Domain: TEMP-RANGE

### Opposition Map

| Population | Thermal need | Clinical basis | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| NEU/MS (Uhthoff's) | Ambient ≤18°C; avoid core temp rise ≥0.5°C | 60–80% of MS patients heat-sensitive | ● MSIF Atlas 2023; Tier 3 |
| OFS/POTS | Cool 18–21°C; heat triggers symptoms | Autonomic thermoregulation failure | ● CDC ME/CFS; Newcastle POTS 2018; Tier 4 |
| PAIN/fibromyalgia | Warmth ≥20°C; cold increases pain | Peripheral sensitisation | ● Staud 2011; Geisser 2021; Tier 3 |
| MOB (spasticity) | Warm preferred; cold increases spasticity | Peripheral nerve/muscle temperature effect | ○ Clinical reasoning; Tier 5 |
| DEM/older adults | 24.9°C neutral; comfort zone 18.3–26.3°C | Reduced thermoregulation capacity | ● Baquero 2023, n=1,065; Tier 3 |

### Domain Classification: DIVERGENT

No single ambient temperature serves all five populations simultaneously. NEU/MS and OFS need cool (≤18–21°C). PAIN and MOB/spasticity need warm (≥20–24°C). DEM/older adults have a higher neutral point (24.9°C) than any disability-specific thermal requirement. ISO 7730 (PMV/PPD) comfort model is unreliable for these populations — developed for healthy adults.

This is the clearest example of a mechanistically irreconcilable cross-population conflict in the guidebook. The pathophysiological mechanisms are opposite: Uhthoff's phenomenon causes neurological deterioration with heat; fibromyalgia causes pain escalation with cold. No compromise temperature exists.

### Resolution Evidence

The ms-thermal-temperature-conflict-resolution BPC (2026-03-30, Opus synthesis) already contains the canonical resolution:

**Resolution — Cool ambient + individual supplemental heating:**
Ambient temperature ≤18–21°C (serving NEU/MS and OFS as the safety floor) with individual supplemental heating (radiant panels, portable heaters, heated seating, zoned BMS) at each primary seating position.

Rationale (harm asymmetry):
- NEU/MS harm from heat: neurological symptom exacerbation, potentially irreversible within a session. Cannot be locally cooled — ambient must be cool.
- PAIN/MOB harm from cool: discomfort, increased pain, increased spasticity. CAN be locally warmed at the individual level.
- Harm is asymmetric: NEU/MS damage > PAIN/MOB discomfort. The population that cannot self-remedy takes priority in the ambient setting.

Status: **RESOLUTION-PROPOSED** (clinically sound; no POE data from facilities implementing this strategy)

**Resolution — High-performance envelope:**
Passivhaus-grade envelope (U ≤0.15 W/m²K wall; Uw ≤0.8 W/m²K glazing; thermal bridges eliminated) delivers passive thermal stability (≤3°C diurnal variation without active HVAC). This reduces the severity of temperature fluctuations that trigger NEU/MS and OFS symptoms.

Status: **RESOLUTION-PROPOSED** (engineering best practice; no disability-specific outcome data)

**Resolution — Individual zone control:**
Per-desk/seat thermostat or radiant panel. Minimum: accessible thermostat within reach of each primary seat. No forced air heating as sole source (acoustic + airborne trigger concern for NDV/OFS).

Status: **RESOLVED-CONSENSUS** (PAS 6463:2022 recommends individual control; no mandatory standard)

### Operability Audit (per project-standards)

"Adjustable" requires operability check:
- NEU/MS: can operate thermostat if mobility/dexterity preserved; may need accessible controls (large buttons, voice control)
- PAIN: can operate if not in acute pain episode; accessible location (750–1050 mm per PAIN-OFS BPC)
- DEM (late stage): CANNOT self-adjust — staff/carer operation required; default setting needed
- MOB (severe): may need accessible controls depending on upper limb function
- OFS (severe fatigue): may be unable to operate during crash — carer/staff protocol needed

Default setting when no individual is present: ≤21°C (harm asymmetry: NEU/MS deterioration > PAIN discomfort). Warming is additive; cooling below ambient is architecturally harder.
