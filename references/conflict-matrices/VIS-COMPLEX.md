### Evidence Analysis

The cognitive-wayfinding BPC (2026-03-29, Opus synthesis) already contains a resolution that the Opus note describes as "one of the strongest in the compendium — both populations are served without compromise." The resolution rests on three evidence-based distinctions:

**1. Modality distinction (3D object vs 2D graphic):**
DEM wayfinding research (Marquardt 2011; van Buuren & Mohammadi 2022, Tier 3) demonstrates that 3D object landmarks at decision points are MORE effective than wall-mounted signage or colour coding for dementia orientation. This is clinically meaningful: DEM users process three-dimensional, culturally familiar objects more reliably than flat images or text.

NDV/SENS distress is triggered by complex wall surfaces, high-density signage, and pattern — NOT by isolated 3D objects. A single distinctive object at a junction does not constitute "visual clutter" in the NDV sensory processing framework (Dunn model, per sensory-processing-model BPC).

Therefore: 3D objects as primary wayfinding serve DEM better than wall graphics AND avoid NDV/SENS triggers.

**2. Placement distinction (decision-point-only vs blanket coverage):**
DEM redundancy needs are at DECISION POINTS — junctions, room entries, floor transitions. Between decision points, redundant cues are unnecessary and may actually impair DEM orientation by creating indistinguishable corridors (cognitive-wayfinding BPC: "no repetitive corridor elements").

NDV/SENS needs clean, predictable circulation surfaces BETWEEN decision points.

Both populations are served by concentrating wayfinding information AT decision points and minimising it between them.

**3. Format distinction (pictogram + single word vs dense text):**
DEM and IntD benefit from pictograms with single-word labels (Tier 3–5, consensus across 10+ languages per cognitive-wayfinding BPC). NDV/SENS can process consistent, predictable sign formats — the distress trigger is unpredictable variety and density, not the presence of standardised signage per se.

Consistent format (same size, same font, same position, same background) satisfies NDV/SENS predictability while delivering DEM/IntD information. Inconsistent format (varied sign types, mixed media, bulletin boards, posters) fails both populations.

### Domain Classification: RESOLVED-CONSENSUS

The apparent opposition dissolves when wayfinding strategy shifts from blanket wall-mounted signage to targeted 3D objects at decision points with standardised supplementary signage. No population compromise required.

### Conflict with NDV/MH

One residual tension: NDV/MH (PTSD) environments benefit from artwork and homelike visual richness (Rodríguez-Labajos 2024; Faerden 2022 — "landscapes, wood flooring"). This conflicts with NDV/SENS clean-wall requirement. Resolution: artwork in designated social/lounge spaces only (not circulation); nature-imagery (low-complexity, biophilic) preferred over abstract or high-complexity art; no artwork in corridors or transition zones. This is a ZONING resolution, not a specification compromise. Route to MH BPC conflict note C-01.
