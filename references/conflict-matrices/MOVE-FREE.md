## Conflict Domain: MOVE-FREE
**Parameter:** Autonomous circulation freedom
**Status:** DIVERGENT — Opus synthesis complete [2026-03-30]
**Date created:** 2026-03-30
**Opus synthesis:** 2026-03-30
**Feeds:** Part 5 §5.2 conflict resolution table; Part 7 matrices (residential care)

### Opposition Summary

| Pop A need | Pop B need | Classification |
|---|---|---|
| DEM: freedom to walk/wander continuously; restricted circulation causes distress and agitation | MOB (fall risk, frail elderly): supervised paths; fall-prevention measures; contained circulation where fall risk is high | Partial |

### Evidence Analysis

**DEM wandering evidence:**
- DEM BPC: loop-path corridor systems associated with reduced agitation (DA, Tier 3; Nationalt Videnscenter for Demens / SBi-anvisning). Loop circulation is the primary DEM spatial provision — it enables continuous walking without dead ends, reducing frustration.
- Cognitive-wayfinding BPC: "continuous loop circulation with no dead ends" is the most-confirmed wayfinding finding across 10+ languages and jurisdictions.
- DEM ethical principle: freedom of movement is a CRPD right. Architectural containment (locked doors, barriers) is a restraint, not a design solution.

**MOB fall risk evidence:**
- MOB BPC: does not mention containment or restricted paths. MOB provisions focus on adequate width, smooth surfaces, grab rails, lighting at floor level, and elimination of trip hazards.
- Clemson et al. 2023 (Cochrane): OT-delivered home hazard reduction reduces falls 38%. The intervention modifies the ENVIRONMENT (removes hazards, adds supports), not the person's circulation freedom.
- Fall prevention evidence is about HAZARD ELIMINATION, not CIRCULATION RESTRICTION.

**Opus assessment:** This conflict is overstated in the domain table. The opposition frame assumes MOB fall prevention requires path restriction — it does not. Evidence-based fall prevention modifies the path (removes hazards, adds handrails, improves lighting, eliminates level changes) rather than restricting circulation.

The genuine tension is NOT between two populations but within DEM care practice: the historical model restricted DEM movement for safety (locked wards); the evidence-based model creates safe environments for free movement (loop paths, level floors, continuous handrails, no trip hazards). The latter is the guidebook's position per CRPD alignment.

### Domain Classification: DIVERGENT but ARCHITECTURALLY RESOLVABLE

The conflict is between an outdated care model (containment) and an evidence-based care model (safe free movement). The guidebook adopts the evidence-based position. The architectural resolution is to make circulation routes safe enough that restriction is unnecessary.

### Resolution

**Resolution 1 — Safe continuous circulation (loop design):**
Design circulation that eliminates fall hazards while permitting unlimited movement:
- Loop-path corridors (no dead ends — DEM provision)
- Level floors throughout (no steps, no ramps in primary circulation — MOB + DEM)
- Continuous handrails on both sides (MOB provision; also DEM steadying support)
- Even, non-slip, non-glare flooring (MOB + VIS + DEM)
- Adequate lighting (≥300 lux in circulation — VIS + DEM)
- Rest seating every 25–30 m (OFS/PAIN provision; benefits DEM fatigue)
- No barriers, locked doors, or restricted zones in primary circulation

This is a convergent resolution: every fall-prevention measure (except physical containment) is compatible with DEM free movement.

Status: **RESOLVED-CONSENSUS** (multiple sources: DSDC, Helsedirektoratet, KDA, cognitive-wayfinding BPC)

**Resolution 2 — Secure perimeter, free interior:**
In dementia residential care:
- Building perimeter is secure (door alarms, screened exits, garden enclosure)
- All interior circulation is unrestricted
- Garden access is free (secure garden perimeter, not locked garden door)

This is standard dementia village design (De Hogeweyk model, NL; Dagmarsminde, DK; Green House homes, US). The "containment" is at the site boundary, not at individual doors or corridors.

Status: **RESOLVED-CONSENSUS** (De Hogeweyk POE; Helsedirektoratet; DSDC)

### Operability Audit

- DEM residents: cannot self-adjust their environment — circulation design must be safe BY DEFAULT, not made safe by user action
- MOB with fall risk + DEM: co-occurring in the same person (very common in aged care). The resolution must serve both simultaneously — and it does, because safe loop circulation addresses both needs.
