## FDR-KIT-01 — d630 + seated wheelchair user → meal preparation

**Sources:** RCOT Living Made Easy (UK, Co-2); First in Architecture (UK, Co-2/Tier 5); Blue Copper Design (US, Co-2); RCOT / Approved Document M (UK, Tier 5–6)

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Worktop height — fixed seated | 760mm AFF | Manual wheelchair user meal prep | RCOT Living Made Easy; First in Architecture (UK) | Co-2/Tier 5 | CONFIRMS |
| Worktop height — adjustable range | 760–900mm | Dual-use household (seated + standing); adjustable provision recommended | RCOT Living Made Easy (UK) | Co-2 | CONFIRMS |
| Knee clearance — manual WC | 700mm H × 600mm D × 800mm W; insulation required below hob/sink | Roll-under access for manual WC | RCOT Living Made Easy; First in Architecture (UK) | Co-2/Tier 5 | CONFIRMS/REFINES |
| Knee clearance — power WC | 710–810mm H × 460mm D | Anti-tip castor and footplate clearance differs from manual WC; standard 700mm may be insufficient | Blue Copper Design (US) | Co-2 | REFINES — power WC-specific knee clearance absent from existing BPC |
| Toe recess at base units | 250mm H × 150mm D | Seated close approach | RCOT Living Made Easy (UK) | Co-2/Tier 5 | CONFIRMS |
| Preparation zone each side of appliance | ≥400mm each side of hob, sink, oven | Slide don't lift technique — prevents lifting hot/heavy items across distance | RCOT Living Made Easy; First in Architecture (UK) | Co-2/Tier 5 | CONFIRMS |
| Oven door opening height | Door opens ≥760mm AFF; side-opening preferred | Prevents reach over hot surface from seated position; lateral reach is biomechanically safer than reach over an open front-opening door | First in Architecture (UK) | Co-2/Tier 5 | CONFIRMS — side-opening rationale is the clinical reasoning addition |
| Minimum kitchen clear width | ≥1200mm between unit fronts and fixed obstruction | Single WC L/U-shaped layout | RCOT / Approved Document M (UK) | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS |

**Clinical reasoning (slide don't lift):** The 400mm preparation zone is not merely about clear surface — it is the operationalization of the OT "slide don't lift" technique for seated kitchen work. Items are slid from hob/sink to preparation surface without being lifted, eliminating the combined demand of grip strength, upper limb strength, and hot-surface reach. This is the clinical reasoning basis for why the zone cannot be reduced — it must accommodate the full item footprint during the slide.

## FDR-KIT-02 — d440 + unilateral UPL → food preparation

**Sources:** Arm Dynamics OT team (US, Co-2); ETAC product literature; AOTA / Neurolutions (US, Co-2); RCOT (UK, Co-2)

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Worktop surface material | Smooth, non-porous, continuous ≥450mm wide | Required for suction-cup AT anchoring (adaptive cutting board, dycem mat) — textured or jointed surfaces prevent suction seal | Arm Dynamics OT team (US) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — surface material spec as AT-enabler absent from BPC |
| Tap/faucet type | Single-lever or sensor/touch; long lever arm; no separate H/C controls | Unilateral UPL — simultaneous hold and twist is not possible; single lever operable with wrist or elbow | AOTA; Neurolutions; RCOT (UK) | Co-2 | CONFIRMS single-lever; REFINES by adding sensor/touch as best practice and elbow-operability rationale |
| Sink-wall AT mount zone | Inner sink wall or rim accessible from dominant-hand side; no projecting fittings within zone | One-handed scrubbing requires suction brush anchored to sink; projections prevent suction cup placement | Arm Dynamics OT team (US) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — spatial zone for AT mount within sink absent from BPC |

**Clinical reasoning:** Unilateral UPL kitchen design is primarily about AT anchoring — every technique an OT prescribes for one-handed food preparation requires a surface that can hold the AT in place. Suction cups, dycem mats, spike boards, and clamps all require smooth, firm, continuous surfaces. This clinical reasoning inverts the usual design logic: the surface material specification is driven by what AT the user will need, not by aesthetics or durability alone.

## FDR-KIT-03 — d640 + limited bilateral reach → laundry / household storage

**Sources:** RCOT Housing Adaptations Without Delay 2019 (UK, Co-2); AOTA Home Modification Practice Guidelines 2023 (US, Co-2); UNSW Home Mod Clearinghouse 2023 (AU, Co-2)

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Washer/dryer pedestal height | 300–400mm raise; drum opening at 700–800mm AFF | Seated user — drum opening at comfortable reach height without trunk lean; standard floor-level placement requires >30° forward lean | RCOT Housing Adaptations Without Delay (2019) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — pedestal height as spatial specification with seated-reach rationale absent from BPC |
| Accessible shelf height range | 380–1200mm AFF (seated); 380–1370mm (ambulant reduced reach) | Lower bound 380mm = 30° lean threshold; consistent with bedroom storage FDR-BAB-05 | AOTA Home Modification Practice Guidelines (2023) | Co-2 | REFINES — lower bound (380mm) not previously specified in kitchen BPC |
| Storage layout configuration | U-shape or L-shape where possible | Reduces travel distance between tasks; shelf edges at corners provide incidental bracing surface for trunk instability | AOTA Home Modification Practice Guidelines (2023) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — layout type as fall-risk mitigation specification |
| Fold-down ironing board (wall-mounted) | Surface height 760mm AFF; fold clearance ≥450mm when folded | WC user — ironing at seated height; fold clearance prevents obstruction of circulation path when board is not in use | UNSW Home Mod Clearinghouse (AU) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** |

**DAR provision note:** Laundry appliance pedestal provision (300–400mm raise, structural support for anti-vibration) should be specified as a DAR rough-in. Pedestal is retrofittable without structural work if space is allocated and floor is reinforced at design stage.

**Clinical reasoning (30° lean threshold cross-reference):** The 380mm lower-bound shelf height is consistent with the trunk instability lean threshold established in FDR-BAB-05. This is not coincidental — it is the same biomechanical constraint applying across rooms. The cross-room consistency of this parameter is a candidate for a universal design specification: accessible storage lower bound = 380mm AFF across all rooms.
