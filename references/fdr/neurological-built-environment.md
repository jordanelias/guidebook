## FDR-NEU-01 — d450 + MS fatigue → circulation and layout (energy conservation)

**Sources:** Rocky Mountain MS Center (Dittmar OTR/L); Solace.health MS home modifications; Live In Place Designs MS; MSAA energy conservation guidance

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Activity centre clustering | Frequently used functions (kitchen prep, coffee, work station) clustered within ≤2m of each other — eliminate traversal between related tasks | MS fatigue: energy is finite and non-recoverable mid-task; traversal between related task points is avoidable energy expenditure — "activity centres" reduce cumulative movement cost | Dittmar OTR/L (MS specialist, Rocky Mountain MS); Medium/LiveInPlace | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — task-cluster layout as energy-conservation spatial specification absent from BPC |
| Bedroom on ground floor / single-level layout | Sleeping area accessible without stair negotiation | MS fatigue + MS variability: fatigue is non-linear — a person able to climb stairs in the morning may be unable in the evening; single-level eliminates variable stair demand | Solace.health MS modifications | Co-2 | REFINES existing single-level living guidance; adds MS fatigue variability mechanism |
| Storage at counter height — priority items | Frequently used items stored at 800–1100mm AFF; eliminate overhead reach and floor-level crouch for daily-use items | MS fatigue: every additional reach or crouch is an energy expenditure — storage design that eliminates these movements conserves energy for functional tasks | Solace.health; Dittmar OTR/L | Co-2 | REFINES storage height guidance; adds MS-specific energy-conservation rationale |
| Pull-out storage — deep cabinets | Pull-out drawers/shelves in all base cabinets; eliminates forward lean into cabinet | MS fatigue + trunk instability: reaching into deep cabinets requires forward lean with sustained arm extension — both are fatiguing and destabilising | Solace.health | Co-2 | REFINES pull-out drawer guidance (in kitchen FDR); confirms cross-room |
| Seated work provision | Lowered counter section at 710–815mm (28–32 inch) for seated work; knee clearance ≥700mm; stool with back support at variable height for sit-stand alternation | MS fatigue: seated task performance eliminates standing energy cost; sit-stand alternation paces energy use | Solace.health; Dittmar OTR/L | Co-2 | REFINES seated worktop in kitchen BPC; adds MS-specific sit-stand alternation rationale and stool provision |

**Clinical reasoning (MS fatigue non-linearity):** MS fatigue is not proportional to effort — it is non-linear and non-recoverable within the same day. A person who feels well in the morning may be unable to perform the same task in the afternoon. OT energy conservation for MS operates on the Four Ps: Prioritise, Plan, Pace, Position. The architectural implementation is to eliminate all mandatory energy expenditures that can be designed out — stair negotiation, overhead reaches, traversal between related task points — so that available energy is spent on chosen activities rather than mandatory transitions.
