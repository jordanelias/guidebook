## FDR-DB-01 — d460 + deafblind (combined VI + HI) → wayfinding (tactile-only navigation)

**Sources:** ISO 23599:2019; Frontiers wayfinding tools review (Tier 2); Clearing Our Path TWSI; BigRentz VI design guide

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tactile wayfinding — mandatory independence from auditory and visual | All spatial information for deafblind navigation must be available through tactile channel alone — no auditory or visual supplement can be assumed | Deafblind: both visual and auditory channels unavailable; TWSI, braille signage, and tactile maps are the *only* available wayfinding channels | ISO 23599:2019; Frontiers review | Tier 2/Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — deafblind-specific independence of tactile channel as design constraint absent from BPC |
| Guidance TWSI — logical path from entry to all key destinations | Continuous guidance TWSI from building entry to all primary destinations (WC, reception, exit); no gap in guidance path | Deafblind: cannot scan across a space to locate the next path cue; the path must be continuous — any gap renders the subsequent path segment inaccessible | ISO 23599:2019; Clearing Our Path | Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — continuous guidance path without gap as deafblind-specific requirement absent from BPC |
| Attention TWSI at every decision point | Attention pattern at every turn, junction, stair approach, hazard, and destination — not only at hazards | Deafblind: cannot look up at signage to confirm decision point; the floor itself must communicate every choice point | ISO 23599:2019; OT O&M principles | Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — decision-point attention TWSI coverage (not only hazards) as deafblind-specific requirement |
| Tactile maps — provision at key entry points | Tactile maps at building entrance and at each floor level change; consistent orientation (north up or entry-facing) | Deafblind: mental map of building must be formed through tactile exploration before independent navigation; tactile map at entry enables pre-navigation orientation | BigRentz VI guide; Frontiers review | Co-2/Tier 2 | **NOVEL** — tactile map provision as built environment installation requirement absent from BPC |
| Braille signage — height and position | Braille signage at 1400–1500mm AFF on latch side of door; consistent position throughout building | Deafblind: must locate signage through hand sweeping; consistent position enables predictable location; latch-side position aligns with approach path | ISO 23599 principles; BS 8300 braille signage | Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — braille height and consistent latch-side position as deafblind-specific wayfinding provision absent from BPC |

**Clinical reasoning (channel independence):** The critical design distinction for deafblind users is that the built environment cannot rely on any multi-modal redundancy — for VI-only users, auditory supplements (announcements, staff) compensate for visual gaps; for HI-only users, visual supplements compensate for auditory gaps. For deafblind users, *neither* supplement is available. Every spatial information requirement must be resolved through tactile means alone. This means the TWSI system must be complete, the guidance paths unbroken, and signage in braille at consistent predictable positions — gaps that are tolerable for VI-only users become impassable barriers for deafblind users.
