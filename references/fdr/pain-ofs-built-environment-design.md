## FDR-PAIN-01 — d450 + chronic pain / OA → circulation (joint-protective gait, surface hardness, rest)

**Sources:** Physiopedia Joint Protection Principles; University Hospitals Cleveland (OT joint protection); AOTA Home Modification Guidelines 2023; OT clinical reasoning

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Floor surface hardness — joint loading | Cushioned/resilient flooring preferred over hard tile or concrete for extended circulation paths; vinyl, cork, rubber, or carpet tiles reduce impact loading | Chronic pain/OA: hard surfaces increase joint impact loading during each footfall — cumulative load across daily steps is significant for knee/hip OA | Joint protection OT principles; OT clinical reasoning | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — floor surface hardness as pain-specific joint-loading specification absent from BPC |
| Rest point provision — pain management | Position change every 15–20 minutes recommended in OT joint protection guidelines; circulation paths ≥15m should have adjacent rest seating | Chronic pain: prolonged same-position loading causes joint stiffness and pain; architectural rest points are the environmental implementation of OT pacing recommendation | University Hospitals Cleveland / Physiopedia joint protection | Co-2 | REFINES existing rest seating guidance; adds 15–20 minute pacing interval as OT basis for rest point spacing |
| Seating at key task locations | Fixed or adjacent seating at kitchen work zones, entry, bathroom vanity | Chronic pain/OA: brief seated rest during task reduces sustained joint load; OT consistently prescribes seated task performance as joint protection strategy | AOTA Home Modification Guidelines 2023; OT clinical reasoning | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — task-adjacent seating as joint protection architectural provision absent from BPC |
| Rise symmetry from seating | Seating at uniform height throughout environment; avoid asymmetric rises | OA/pain: OT joint protection specifies "rise from chair symmetrically, avoid leaning to either side" — asymmetric seating or uneven heights force asymmetric loading on one knee/hip | University Hospitals Cleveland joint protection | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — seating height consistency as joint-protection specification across environment absent from BPC |
| Wheeled transport of loads | Internal layout provides slide/roll paths between kitchen, dining, and laundry — reduces lift-and-carry | Chronic pain: OT joint protection specifies "use wheeled cart for transporting items" — internal layout must accommodate cart movement (clear 900mm paths, level transitions) | Physiopedia joint protection; AOTA | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — wheeled load transport path as joint-protection layout specification absent from BPC |

**Clinical reasoning (pacing and joint protection):** OT joint protection for chronic pain/OA operates on two principles: (1) distribute load — use more joints, lighter loads, shorter durations; (2) pace — alternate activity and rest before pain onset. The architectural implementation of these principles inverts usual design logic: the building must build in involuntary rest points and reduce the load required for every task, rather than minimising the number of steps to complete a task. A compact kitchen that minimises walking distance may actually be worse for OA if it eliminates the micro-rest intervals between work zones.
