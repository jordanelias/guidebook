## FDR-ID-01 — d460 + intellectual disability → wayfinding (signage, spatial legibility, colour zoning)

**Sources:** PMC12033658 (ID home environment scoping review, Tier 2); MDPI Down syndrome school design (2025); Mostafa 2010 M2 model; Pagethink wayfinding

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Colour zoning — room differentiation | Distinct colours for each primary room type; consistent colour-to-function mapping throughout environment | ID: abstract spatial reasoning and memory impaired; colour is a concrete, memorable identifier that substitutes for cognitive mapping ability | PMC12033658 (ID home scoping review, Tier 2) | Tier 2 | **NOVEL** — colour zoning as ID-specific wayfinding provision absent from BPC |
| Signage — pictogram with text | Room and directional signs use ISO 7001 pictograms paired with simple text (≤3 words); avoid text-only signs | ID: text literacy and reading fluency vary widely; pictograms are accessible to non-readers and supplement text for partial readers; single-word or paired sign-text is maximum complexity | PMC12033658; MDPI 2025; Pagethink wayfinding | Tier 2/Co-2 | **NOVEL** — pictogram+text combination as ID-specific signage specification absent from BPC |
| Decision points — minimised | Route from bedroom to bathroom to living area should present maximum 1–2 binary choices | ID: working memory constraints limit ability to hold multi-step route in mind; each additional decision point compounds error probability | PMC12033658; OT clinical reasoning | Tier 2/Co-2 | REFINES dementia finding (FDR-DEM-01); confirms cross-population application to ID |
| Spatial legibility — prospect and refuge | Open prospect areas (where person can survey the environment) adjacent to refuge areas (private, bounded spaces for withdrawal) | ID: anxiety in unfamiliar environments is reduced by ability to survey space before entering; refuge areas allow regulation breaks | PMC12033658 (Kaplan & Kaplan applied) | Tier 2 | **NOVEL** — prospect-and-refuge spatial pairing as ID-specific environmental design specification absent from BPC |
| Glazed view into key rooms | Glazed panel or window in key room doors (bathroom, activity room) allowing preview before entry | ID: transition anxiety is reduced by visual confirmation of room content before committing to entry; opaque doors require trust that room is safe without confirmation | PMC12033658; OT clinical reasoning | Tier 2/Co-2 | REFINES dementia finding (FDR-DEM-01); confirms for ID via independent mechanism (transition anxiety) |

**Clinical reasoning (spatial legibility for ID):** PMC12033658 identifies "legibility" — the degree to which a space can be understood, remembered, and navigated without becoming disoriented — as the central environmental quality for people with ID. Legibility is compromised by complex layouts, text-only signage, colour uniformity (all rooms look alike), and opaque doors. The architectural solution is systematic differentiation: every room has a unique colour, a pictogram, and a visible interior. This is not decoration — it is functional substitution for the cognitive mapping capacity that is impaired in ID.
