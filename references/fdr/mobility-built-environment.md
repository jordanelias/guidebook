## FDR-MOB-01 — d450 + festinating gait (Parkinson's disease) → circulation / corridors

**Sources:** Parkinson's UK / COT OT Best Practice Guidelines (Aragon & Kings, 2010) [Co-2]; Ramos et al. 2020 SR [Tier 2]

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Floor visual cue strip — length | ≈450mm; high-contrast to floor surface | Freezing of gait (FOG) / festination in corridor; placed parallel at approximately individual stride-length intervals in known trouble zones | Parkinson's UK / COT OT Best Practice Guidelines (Aragon & Kings, 2010) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — PD-specific floor cue strip specification absent from BPC |
| Visual cue strip — corner treatment | Fan pattern of strips around 90° turn; same ≈450mm strip length | FOG at direction changes; strips must be within visual field during use | Parkinson's UK / COT OT Best Practice Guidelines (2010) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — corner-specific strip layout absent from BPC |
| Doorway — FOG trigger status | Doorways identified as primary indoor FOG trigger; wider clear opening than statutory minimum is beneficial | PD with FOG; doorways, narrow openings | Jones et al. cited in Ramos et al. 2020 SR [PMC7683074] | Tier 2 | **NOVEL** — doorway as primary FOG trigger not documented in BPC; implies clear-width spec beyond statutory |
| Stair nosing lines — gait cueing effect | Stair nosings function as natural visual gait cues; stair negotiation is rarely problematic for PD users (unlike level corridors) | PD with FOG; stair vs. level-surface distinction | Parkinson's UK / COT OT Best Practice Guidelines (2010) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — stair/corridor distinction absent; informs why flat corridors need embedded cues but stairs typically do not |
| Furniture / obstacles in circulation | Furniture in circulation path triggers gait interruption; risk increases with disease severity | PD with FOG; indoor circulation | Jones et al. cited in Ramos et al. 2020 SR | Tier 2 | REFINES — BPC has corridor width; adds PD-specific rationale for unobstructed circulation zone beyond standard clear width |

**Methodology note:** Visual cue strip specification (450mm, stride-length intervals) is a design provision, not a fixed architectural dimension — it is applied as an overlay to existing circulation geometry, not as a substitute for clear-width compliance. Design teams must allow for strip installation in floor finish specification.
