## FDR-LRV-01 — d460/d450 + visual impairment → luminance contrast (universal thresholds by application)

**Sources:** BS 8300-2:2018; Approved Document M 2015; AS 1428.1:2009; AS 1428.4.1:2009; SATRA LRV guide; Reading University Project Rainbow

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| General adjacent surface contrast — minimum | ≥30 LRV point difference between two adjacent surfaces requiring differentiation (wall to floor, door to wall, step tread to nosing) | VI/partial sight: 30 LRV is the derived minimum discriminable contrast for partial sight under typical building lighting; below 30 LRV, contrast is unreliable for orientation | BS 8300-2:2018; Approved Document M 2015; DDA/Equality Act; Reading University Project Rainbow | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS ≥30 LRV; **NOVEL** — synthesis of UK + AU sources as cross-jurisdictional confirmation |
| Large adjacent surfaces — reduced threshold | ≥20 LRV acceptable for large areas where illuminance >200 lux | VI: large surfaces at high illuminance are easier to discriminate at lower contrast — 20 LRV is a reduced threshold acceptable in specific conditions, not a general relaxation | SATRA LRV guide (BS 8300 Annex B) | Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — conditional 20 LRV threshold for large surfaces at high illuminance absent from BPC |
| Small items (handles, switches, sockets) — higher contrast | ≥15 LRV for door handles vs door face; though small items can be acceptable with smaller differential due to 3D nature | VI: 3D small items (handles) are visible through shadow and shape as well as contrast — BS 8300 allows smaller differential than flat surfaces; however maximum contrast is always safer | SATRA LRV guide; Gradus guide | Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — small-item LRV threshold distinction absent from BPC |
| TGSI (integrated tile type) | ≥30% luminance contrast | VI: integrated tile TGSIs have larger contact area — lower threshold acceptable | AS 1428.4.1:2009; CAG; ESP Australia | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS (cross-reference FDR-VI-01); jurisdictional synthesis |
| TGSI (individual single colour) | ≥45% luminance contrast | VI: individual discrete TGSIs have smaller contact area — higher contrast required | AS 1428.4.1:2009; CAG; ESP Australia | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS (cross-reference FDR-VI-01) |
| TGSI (individual two colours / composite) | ≥60% luminance contrast | VI: composite TGSI with coloured dome insert requires highest contrast — each colour component must still be detectable | AS 1428.4.1:2009; ESP Australia | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS (cross-reference FDR-VI-01) |
| Stair nosing contrast | ≥30 LRV difference between nosing and tread; 50–75mm wide on tread | VI: nosing must be detectable as a line across the full stair width; width determines visual target size | AS 1428.1:2009; Quantum; Gradus; BS 8300 | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS (cross-reference FDR-VI-02, FDR-STAIR-01) |

## FDR-LRV-02 — d460 + dementia → luminance contrast (spatial orientation applications)

**Sources:** Quantum Flooring LRV guide; DSDC dementia LRV guidance; Realm Access SDA Design Standard; Danfloor LRV guide

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| NDIS SDA (Improved Liveability) — doorways | ≥30% luminance contrast between doorway and surrounding wall | Dementia + cognitive impairment: door must be identifiable as a distinct element; matching door and wall creates invisible entry point | NDIS SDA Design Standard; Realm Access | Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — SDA Improved Liveability doorway contrast as regulatory requirement absent from BPC |
| NDIS SDA (Improved Liveability) — toilet seat | ≥30% luminance contrast between toilet seat and bowl or surrounds | Dementia + VI: toilet must be identifiable against surrounding surfaces — white toilet on white floor is functionally invisible to VI and dementia users | NDIS SDA Design Standard; Realm Access | Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — toilet seat contrast as SDA regulatory requirement absent from BPC (cross-confirms FDR-DEM-02 WC contrast finding) |
| Floor to wall contrast — wayfinding | ≥30 LRV between floor and wall surfaces in circulation spaces | Dementia + VI: floor/wall junction defines spatial boundary — without contrast, edges of rooms and corridors are indistinguishable, creating disorientation | Quantum; DSDC; Danfloor | Tier 5–6/Co-2 | **NOVEL** — floor-to-wall contrast as wayfinding specification with dementia-specific rationale absent from BPC |
| Glare control — reflective floors contraindicated | Polished or high-gloss floors contraindicated in VI and dementia spaces regardless of LRV | VI + dementia: highly reflective floors create glare and visual ambiguity (puddle illusion, reflections misread as obstacles) — LRV compliance alone insufficient if surface is highly specular | Realm Access; FDR-VI-01 cross-reference | Co-2/Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — specular reflectance as contraindication additional to LRV absent from BPC |
