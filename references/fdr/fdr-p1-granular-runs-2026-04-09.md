## FDR-NEW-02: d530 + OFS/POTS → Toileting with Orthostatic Intolerance

**ICF:** d530 (Toileting) · **Constraint:** orthostatic hypotension/POTS · **Context:** bathroom

### Spatial Parameters Extracted

| Parameter | Value | Evidence | Classification |
|---|---|---|---|
| WC seat height | 460–480mm AFF (higher than standard 400–430mm) | OT clinical reasoning: reduces vertical displacement during sit-to-stand, decreasing orthostatic blood pressure drop. Dochod & Grapczynski (OJOT): staged rising protocol requires intermediate sitting position. | **REFINES** G-05 (currently 430–480mm range — POTS rationale elevates minimum to 460mm) |
| Bilateral grab bars | Required both sides (not unilateral) | POTS syncope risk is bilateral — cannot predict which side will be needed for support during presyncope. Awareness for POTSies: bedside commode with bilateral rails as adaptation. | **REFINES** G-03 (currently permits unilateral in some configurations) |
| WC-to-bedroom proximity | ≤5m door-to-door (or en-suite) | POTS symptoms worsen with ambulatory distance after position change. Urgency + presyncope = fall risk in corridor. | **NOVEL** — no current specification for WC-bedroom proximity in OFS context |
| Staged rising space | ≥500mm clear in front of WC for forward-lean recovery position | OT staged rising protocol: lean forward with elbows on knees for 30–60s before standing. Requires clear knee space. | **NOVEL** — no current specification for pre-standing recovery space |
| Grab bar height relative to seat | 200–300mm above seat surface | Must support pull-to-stand from forward-lean position, not just lateral transfer | **REFINES** G-03 positioning rationale |

**ISW brief:** Update G-05 OFS population note: minimum 460mm for POTS. Add bilateral grab bar requirement for OFS to G-03. Flag WC-bedroom proximity for Part 6 residential matrix (OFS row). Add staged rising space note.

## FDR-NEW-04: d630 + Visual Impairment → Meal Preparation Safety

**ICF:** d630 (Preparing meals) · **Constraint:** absent/reduced vision · **Context:** kitchen

### Spatial Parameters Extracted

| Parameter | Value | Evidence | Classification |
|---|---|---|---|
| Induction cooktop (mandatory for VIS) | Induction only — no gas, no radiant electric | Eliminates open flame and invisible hot-surface hazards. RNIB and Vision Australia guidance. No exposed heat source accessible to touch. | **REFINES** H-01 (currently recommends induction; elevate to mandatory for VIS) |
| Tactile differentiation of controls | Raised dots/ridges on all controls; distinct tactile profiles per function | BS 8300, DIN 18040-2 Annex (controls). Prevents mis-selection of burner/temperature. | **REFINES** H-01 (currently generic "accessible controls") |
| Contrast at counter edges | LRV ≥50 at counter edge for partial sight | Consistent with CON-0043 (≥50% best practice). Prevents hand/arm contact with hot surfaces. | **CONFIRMS** C-04 (≥50% already in guidebook as best practice) |
| Pan detection on induction hob | Auto-off when no pan detected | Safety feature preventing accidental activation. Standard on modern induction hobs. | **NOVEL** — specification note for H-01 |
| Knife storage | Enclosed magnetic strip or block with tactile indicator; not open rack | Open knife racks are a laceration hazard for VIS. Enclosed storage with tactile position indicators. | **NOVEL** — no current specification |
| Hot surface warning | Residual heat indicator (visual + tactile/audible for VIS) | Post-cooking hazard. Induction hobs with residual heat indicator beep until safe. | **NOVEL** — specification note for H-01 |

**ISW brief:** Elevate induction-only to mandatory for VIS kitchens in H-01. Add tactile control differentiation. Add kitchen safety notes (knife storage, pan detection, residual heat). Cross-ref C-04 for counter edge contrast.

## FDR-NEW-05: d630 + Dementia → Kitchen Safety and Task Completion

**ICF:** d630 (Preparing meals) · **Constraint:** cognitive decline · **Context:** kitchen

### Spatial Parameters Extracted

| Parameter | Value | Evidence | Classification |
|---|---|---|---|

| Parameter | Value | Evidence | Classification |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tactile evacuation signage | Raised characters + braille at every decision point on egress route; floor number + direction + exit floor (tactile star) | CAN/ASC 2.2 Emergency Egress Standard (2025): explicit specification for handrail-mounted raised characters. NFPA guidance. DbI guidelines. | **NOVEL** — current D-06/D-08 do not specify tactile evacuation signage separately from general tactile signage |
| Vibrotactile fire alarm | Bed shaker + body-worn vibration alert connected to building fire alarm system | Krown KA300 system: visual + auditory + vibrotactile (bed shaker). ## FDR-NEW-15: d440 + Tremor (PD/MS/Essential) → Fine Control Operation

**ICF:** d440 (Fine hand use) · **Constraint:** action tremor · **Context:** all rooms

### Spatial Parameters Extracted

| Parameter | Value | Evidence | Classification |
|---|---|---|---|
| Touchscreen inaccessibility | Capacitive touchscreens inaccessible with action tremor: mis-registration, unintended multi-touch, inability to maintain finger position | OT clinical evidence: tremor amplitude 2–10mm makes precision touch targets (<15mm) unusable. Parkinson's Foundation guidance. EAA accessibility requirements (EN 301 549). | **NOVEL** — H-02 does not currently flag touchscreen as inaccessible for tremor populations |
| Toggle/rocker switches | Large-surface rocker switches (≥25mm actuator surface) or toggle switches preferred | Positive haptic feedback; does not require precision positioning. DIN 18040-2: rocker switches at 900–1100mm AFF. | **REFINES** E-05 (add tremor rationale for rocker preference) |
| Operating force | ≤5N for all controls | ADA/Section 508: ≤5 lbf (22.2N) for operable parts. DIN 18040-2: ≤5N. Tremor populations may require lower force to avoid triggering intention tremor amplification. | **CONFIRMS** existing ≤5N specification |
| Voice control as primary | Voice-activated environmental control for severe tremor | H-02 already specifies voice control; for tremor populations it becomes the PRIMARY interface, not alternative. | **REFINES** H-02 (elevate voice control from alternative to primary for tremor) |
| Large-target interfaces | Minimum touch target 44×44mm for any digital interface used by tremor population | WCAG 2.2 Level AAA: 44×44 CSS pixels. For physical interfaces: equivalent physical target size. | **NOVEL** — no current guidebook specification for physical touch target minimum |

**ISW brief:** Add tremor population notes to H-02 (voice control primary), E-05 (rocker switches), and create touchscreen inaccessibility flag. Add minimum physical touch target size. Cross-ref EAA for digital interfaces in EU jurisdictions.

## FDR-NEW-16: d440 + Tetraplegia (C4–C5) → Environmental Control Unit Interface

**ICF:** d440 (Fine hand use) · **Constraint:** no functional hand use · **Context:** all rooms

### Spatial Parameters Extracted

| Parameter | Value | Evidence | Classification |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sip-and-puff interface mounting | Fixed mounting bracket at each primary position: bed (700mm AFF, ≤300mm from head), WC (seated height), primary seating (armrest level) | Must be reachable from fixed position without hand adjustment. RESNA/ARATA standards. Mounting bracket specified on structural drawings. | **NOVEL** — H-02 does not specify spatial mounting requirements for sip-and-puff |
| Eye-gaze screen position | Screen at 500–700mm from face, perpendicular to line of sight, adjustable arm mount | Eye-gaze tracking requires stable screen-to-eye geometry. Tobii/ARATA guidance. Screen must be repositionable when user transfers between positions. | **NOVEL** — no current specification |
| Voice control acoustic environment | RT60 ≤0.4s in rooms with voice-controlled ECU | Voice recognition accuracy degrades with reverberation. Already in A-02 for speech intelligibility; C4–C5 users have reduced vocal volume, increasing sensitivity to acoustic conditions. | **REFINES** A-02 (add voice control reliability rationale for C4–C5) |
| Switch access point per position | Accessible switch (head, chin, or proximity) at bed, WC, and primary seating — wired to ECU | Alternative to sip-and-puff. Must not require hand operation. | Backup power for ECU | UPS with ≥4hr battery for ECU system — life safety requirement | ECU controls door locks, lighting, call system, HVAC. Power failure = complete environmental lockout for C4–C5 user. DAR provision: conduit for UPS at construction. | **NOVEL** — H-02 does not specify backup power; this is life safety for C4–C5 |
| Cable routing from construction | Conduit from ECU mounting positions to electrical panel — DAR provision | Retrofit cable routing through finished walls: ×8–15 retrofit multiplier. Construction-stage conduit: ≤0.1% NC premium. | **NOVEL** — Part 10 DAR cross-reference |

**ISW brief:** Create ECU spatial specification for C4–C5: mounting positions, eye-gaze screen geometry, per-position switches, backup power (life safety), construction-stage conduit. This is the most specification-dense FDR scenario. Cross-ref Part 10 DAR for construction-stage provisions.
