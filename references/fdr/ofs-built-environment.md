**Clinical reasoning (post-exertional malaise and spatial design):** ME/CFS OT management is uniquely constrained by post-exertional malaise (PEM) — symptom worsening following activity that exceeds individual energy envelope, with delayed onset (typically 12–48 hours). PEM means that a home that required slightly more energy than the person's envelope today will produce a crash tomorrow. The spatial design implication is that the distance and effort cost of every mandatory daily journey must be minimised independently, not just as a general accessibility principle. Bedroom-to-bathroom distance is a priority because nocturnal toileting cannot be deferred; kitchen-to-rest adjacency matters because cooking is a trigger for PEM; stair elimination matters because stairs are mandatory-use barriers that cannot be avoided.
