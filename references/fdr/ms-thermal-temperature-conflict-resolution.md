## FDR-MST-01 — d570 + MS Uhthoff's phenomenon → thermal management (indoor temperature, ventilation, cooling)

**Sources:** Christogianni et al. 2022 (ScienceDirect, Co-2 survey); Davis & Wilson 2010 thermoregulation MS (Tier 2 review); MSAA; VA MS; Solace.health MS modifications; FDR-NEU-01 cross-reference

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Uhthoff's prevalence — design driver | 60–80% of pwMS experience heat sensitivity (Uhthoff's phenomenon); air conditioning is the primary management strategy cited by heat-sensitive pwMS | MS heat sensitivity: demyelinated axons block at elevated temperature (0.5°C above baseline can trigger block); this is not subjective discomfort but neurological conduction failure — design must treat cooling as a clinical requirement | Davis & Wilson 2010 (Tier 2); MSAA; Christogianni 2022 | Tier 2/Co-2 | **NOVEL** — 0.5°C threshold for conduction block as clinical basis for design requirement absent from BPC |
| Air conditioning — required not optional | Whole-home air conditioning treated as a clinical requirement for heat-sensitive pwMS; refrigerative AC preferred over evaporative | MS heat sensitivity: evaporative cooling (swamp cooler) adds humidity and provides limited cooling in humid climates; refrigerative AC reduces both temperature and humidity reliably | MSAA; VA MS; OT clinical reasoning | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — AC as clinical requirement vs. comfort amenity distinction absent from BPC |
| Indoor temperature target | ≤21°C (70°F) recommended for pwMS with heat sensitivity; individual threshold varies — some pwMS trigger below 22°C | Uhthoff's: individual temperature sensitivity threshold varies; 21°C is a conservative target that accommodates most heat-sensitive pwMS without over-cooling for non-MS occupants | MSAA; VA MS heat; MS temperature community data | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — 21°C indoor temperature target for MS absent from BPC |
| Cool zone — retreat space | One designated cool zone (bedroom preferred) maintained ≤2°C below rest of dwelling; accessible from primary activity areas | MS fatigue + heat: during an Uhthoff's episode, person must move to a cooler space to restore function; cool zone within self-rescue distance allows function restoration without carer assistance | Tampa neurologists; Solace.health MS | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — cool zone as spatial provision within dwelling absent from BPC |
| Kitchen — heat source management | Induction cooktop; enhanced extraction ventilation; split unit AC in kitchen or open-plan AC coverage includes kitchen | Uhthoff's: kitchen is highest heat-generating room in dwelling; cooking with conventional hob raises kitchen temperature by 3–6°C — this alone may trigger Uhthoff's episode in heat-sensitive pwMS | Solace.health MS; FDR-NEU-02 cross-reference | Co-2 | CONFIRMS FDR-NEU-02 induction + ventilation; adds temperature-rise quantification |

## FDR-MST-02 — d570 + MS cold sensitivity → thermal management (cold protection)

**Sources:** Christogianni et al. 2022; MS temperature community

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cold sensitivity — bilateral conflict | Some pwMS have cold sensitivity (muscle cramping, fatigue in cold) rather than heat sensitivity — thermal design must accommodate both profiles in the same dwelling or provide individual control | MS: cold sensitivity produces muscle cramping and fatigue through different mechanism than Uhthoff's (muscle function impaired by cold); design cannot assume all pwMS need cooling — individual thermal control is the resolution | Christogianni 2022; MS temperature patient data | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — cold sensitivity conflict with heat sensitivity as design problem requiring individual control absent from BPC |
| Individual zone control — HVAC | Each primary room independently controlled; avoid whole-dwelling single thermostat | MS + cold conflict: heat-sensitive occupant and cold-sensitive occupant in same dwelling require different zone temperatures; room-by-room control resolves conflict | OT clinical reasoning; Christogianni 2022 | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — individual zone HVAC control as MS-specific conflict resolution absent from BPC |
