## FDR-BAB-01 — d420 + hemiplegia → WC transfer

**Sources:** Nakamura et al. 2009 (JA, Tier 1); Yamashita OT guidelines (JA, Co-2); DIN 18040-1/-2 (DE, Tier 5-6); Kennedy et al. 2015 (CA, Tier 1)

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Paralysis-side vertical support bar position | 5cm from WC side edge, 15cm from front; 70–140cm height range | Hemiplegic WC user — bar on affected side supports trunk, frees unaffected hand for clothing management | Nakamura et al. 2009 (JA) | 1 | **NOVEL** |
| Min lateral clearance from WC centreline | 45–50cm each side | Both front and lateral transfer approaches; prioritise transfer side if constrained | Yamashita OT guidelines (JA) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** |
| Grab bar height seat-relative | 28cm above seat surface; bilateral spacing 65–70cm clear | Auto-adapts to seat height variation — seat-relative spec more robust than AFF spec | DIN 18040-1/-2 (DE) | 5–6 | REFINES — seat-relative approach absent from EN/AU/US BPC |
| Preferred grab bar orientation for ambulatory balance | Two vertical bars — produces smallest COP deviation across stroke, hip replacement, and non-disabled users | Ambulatory users with balance impairment using grab bar for steadying, not transfer | Kennedy et al. 2015 (CA) | 1 | REFINES |

**Clinical reasoning (Nakamura 2009):** Hemiplegic toilet transfer requires trunk support on the affected side AND free use of the unaffected hand for clothing. Standard bilateral horizontal grab bar configurations serve neither need simultaneously. Vertical bar on affected side is the correct intervention — it braces without requiring grip strength on the affected side.

## FDR-BAB-03 — d530 + bilateral UPL → toileting (bidet/wash-dry)

**Sources:** UNSW Home Modification Clearinghouse OT Guidelines for Bidet Prescription (AU, Co-2); Vasilakopoulou & Bridge 2019 (AU, Co-2) — prior GAP FLAG resolved

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Electrical outlet for bidet seat | ≤1500mm from WC; GFCI/RCD protected; side wall preferred | Electronic bidet seat with heater/dryer function; bilateral UPL — hands-free hygiene | UNSW Home Mod Clearinghouse (AU) | Co-2 | REFINES — corrects prior GAP FLAG (600mm was water supply spec, not electrical) |
| Water supply for bidet seat | T-valve on existing cold supply line; no additional plumbing envelope required | Bidet seat attachment (not standalone unit) | UNSW Home Mod Clearinghouse (AU) | Co-2 | REFINES — resolves prior GAP FLAG |
| Wall-mount remote zone | Side wall; 800–1100mm AFF; reachable from seated position | Users where body habitus or bilateral UPL impedes reach to side-panel controls on seat | UNSW Home Mod Clearinghouse (AU) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — spatial zone for wall-mounted remote absent from BPC |

**Clinical reasoning:** Bilateral UPL creates a hygiene independence problem — manual wiping is not possible. Bidet seat attachment solves this without structural modification if electrical outlet is provisioned. DAR rough-in: electrical outlet ≤1500mm from WC, GFCI/RCD, side wall, accessible height. The remote zone is essential for high-cord or shoulder-level impairment where seat-mounted controls cannot be reached.

## FDR-BAB-04 — d465 + powered wheelchair → entrance threshold

**Sources:** BS 8300-2:2018 Annex G (UK, Tier 5); AS 1428.1:2021 (AU, Tier 6)

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Powered WC turning space (90° turn) | ≥1800mm × 1800mm | Electrically propelled wheelchair at entrance/door approach — anti-tip castor and extended footplate geometry | BS 8300-2:2018 Annex G Tables G.7–G.8 | 5 | **NOVEL** — existing BPC ø1500mm calibrated for manual chair; insufficient for power WC |
| Threshold height — power WC | Zero preferred; ≤6mm absolute maximum without ramp; ramp ≤1:12 retro-fit | Anti-tip castor geometry; footplate ground clearance typically ≥75mm — even small thresholds create tip risk | BS 8300-2:2018; AS 1428.1:2021 | 5–6 | CONFIRMS zero-threshold; adds power WC castor mechanism as independent rationale |

**Clinical reasoning:** Power wheelchair geometry is fundamentally different from manual. Anti-tip castors project behind the rear axle and can catch raised thresholds that a manual chair rolls over. The turning envelope required for a powered chair (especially bariatric power chairs) routinely exceeds what standard accessible design specifications assume.

## FDR-BAB-05 — d540 + trunk instability → bedroom (dressing)

**Sources:** RCOT Housing Adaptations Without Delay 2019 (UK, Co-2); AOTA Home Modification Practice Guidelines 2023 (US, Co-2); CAOT Home Assessment and Modifications 2024 (CA, Co-2); UNSW Home Mod Clearinghouse 2023 (AU, Co-2)

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Closet rod height — forward reach, seated | ≤1050mm AFF | Wheelchair user with trunk instability — forward reach only; no lateral lean permitted | RCOT Housing Adaptations Without Delay (2019) | Co-2 | REFINES — tighter than AOTA ≤1200mm; RCOT constrains for forward-reach-only condition |
| Shelf depth with trunk instability | ≤380mm | Prevents forward trunk lean >30° — fall risk threshold for trunk-unstable users | AOTA Home Modification Practice Guidelines (2023) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** |
| Pull-out drawer depth | ≤600mm with full-extension hardware | Seated access to rear of storage without forward lean | AOTA Home Modification Practice Guidelines (2023) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** |
| Dual-rod closet configuration | Upper rod 1000–1100mm AFF; lower rod ≈600mm AFF | Seated user — lower rod for everyday items at comfortable reach; upper for long-hang items at maximum forward reach | AOTA Home Modification Practice Guidelines (2023) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** |
| Dressing area clear floor space | ≥1500×1500mm | WC user requiring turning during dressing sequence | CAOT Home Assessment and Modifications (2024) | Co-2 | REFINES — extends prior 760×1200mm transfer-only spec to full dressing turning circle |
| Wardrobe door type | Sliding or bi-fold preferred; swing door requires clear floor space ≥door width + manoeuvre | Eliminates door arc intrusion into limited dressing space; critical where room is constrained | UNSW Home Mod Clearinghouse (AU) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** |
| Low storage — contraindicated height | ≤500mm AFF: below this threshold requires >30° forward lean — fall risk for trunk-unstable users | Trunk instability; items should not be stored below 500mm | UNSW Home Mod Clearinghouse (AU) | Co-2 | REFINES — adds trunk-instability biomechanical rationale to reach-range lower bound |

**Clinical reasoning (30° lean threshold):** OT assessment consistently identifies 30° forward trunk lean from seated as the fall-risk threshold for trunk-unstable users. Items below 500mm AFF require this lean. This provides the biomechanical justification for the lower-bound reach specification — it is not simply a reach range, it is a stability limit.

## FDR-BAB-06 — d410 + lower limb weakness → bedroom (bed transfer / rising)

**Sources:** Threshold OT (OT home assessment blog, US, Co-2); Mattress Miracle accessibility guide (CA, Co-2); ADA Lodging Best Practices Bed Height Fact Sheet (US, Tier 5); Accora FloorBed product/clinical documentation (UK, Co-2)

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bed transfer height — wheelchair user | 430–585mm AFF (17–23 inch range); matched to WC seat height | Transfer from WC to bed requires surface height match; above or below this range makes independent transfer extremely difficult or impossible | ADA Lodging Best Practices / Corada (US) | Tier 5 | **NOVEL** — specific transfer-height range with WC-match rationale absent from BPC |
| Bed height — ambulant lower limb weakness | Top of sleeping surface at approximately knee height when standing | Sitting down becomes a controlled movement; rising requires less quadriceps/hip extensor demand when height is appropriate | Mattress Miracle accessibility guide (CA); Threshold OT (US) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — ambulant lower-limb-weakness bed height rationale distinct from WC transfer spec |
| Bed underclearance — wheelchair positioning | ≥150–200mm floor clearance under bed | Manual wheelchair footrests must clear under bed frame during transfer positioning | Mattress Miracle accessibility guide (CA) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — floor clearance for WC transfer positioning absent from BPC |
| Height-adjustable bed — DAR provision | Profiling/height-adjustable bed frame specified as DAR provision; height range typically 300–650mm | Lower limb weakness and WC users require different heights for sleeping (personal preference) and transfer (matched to WC seat) | Accora FloorBed clinical documentation; Vivid Care (UK) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — height-adjustable bed as DAR provision with clinical rationale absent from BPC |
| Mattress firmness — transfer surface | Firm/medium-firm preferred; avoid high-sink memory foam | Deep-sink mattresses make edge-of-bed repositioning and transfer initiation significantly harder; OT consistently recommends against high-sink surfaces for transfer-dependent users | Threshold OT (US); ADA Lodging Best Practices | Co-2/Tier 5 | **NOVEL** — mattress firmness as OT-specified transfer parameter absent from BPC |

**Clinical reasoning (OT home assessment):** Bed height is one of the top-priority modifications in home OT assessment. The intervention is bidirectional — too high risks fall off the edge; too low prevents STS. The correct target height is popliteal height (back of knee to floor when seated), which varies by individual. Height-adjustable beds resolve this by allowing the same bed to serve different users and different tasks (sleeping vs. transfer). This is the clinical reasoning basis for height-adjustable bed as a DAR provision.

## FDR-BAB-07 — d570 + emergency/medication → bedroom (overnight safety)

**Sources:** RCOT Housing Adaptations Without Delay 2019 (UK, Co-2); AOTA Fall Prevention in Bedroom 2023 (US, Co-2); Dementia Australia Built Environment Guidelines 2022 (AU, Tier 2)

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Emergency pull cord — secondary position | ≤500mm AFF from bed head (in addition to floor-level cord) | Users falling in bed-transfer zone; enables bed-head activation without reaching to floor | RCOT Housing Adaptations Without Delay (2019) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — prior BPC has floor-level cord only; bed-head secondary is additive |
| Bedside light switch position | ≤900mm from pillow position | Operated without stretching from lying; critical for users who cannot safely lean or rise to reach standard switch | RCOT Housing Adaptations Without Delay (2019) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** |
| Bed-exit sensor activated night lighting | Auto-on; covers full bed-to-bathroom route | Eliminates need to locate switch at night; fall prevention for disoriented or mobility-limited users | RCOT Housing Adaptations Without Delay (2019) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — sensor activation (not just presence of lighting) specified |
| Overnight wayfinding path lighting | Continuous strip 300–400mm AFF; ≥5 lux; 2700–3000K warm CCT | Bed-to-bathroom route; warm CCT avoids circadian disruption; low mounting avoids glare when lying down | Dementia Australia Built Environment Guidelines (2022) | Tier 2 | **NOVEL** |
| Bedroom door — room identification | Distinguishable from other doors by colour/texture; name/symbol plate ≥1200mm AFF | Orientation support; especially important in multi-room dwellings for cognitively impaired users or post-anaesthesia disorientation | Dementia Australia Built Environment Guidelines (2022) | Tier 2 | **NOVEL** — spatial/material spec, not just signage advisory |
| Bedside medication storage | Lockable; ≤1200mm AFF; ≤500mm horizontal reach from bed-seated position | Overnight medication access without standing; horizontal reach limit prevents bed-edge instability | AOTA Fall Prevention in Bedroom (2023) | Co-2 | **NOVEL** |
| Bed clearance — foot end | ≥1000mm at foot of bed | Emergency responder access path; paramedic equipment approach requires this clearance | AOTA Fall Prevention in Bedroom (2023) | Co-2 | REFINES — 760mm each side retained from BPC; 1000mm foot dimension is additive |

**Clinical reasoning:** Overnight safety is a distinct design problem from daytime transfer — it involves disorientation, reduced alertness, and urgency (fall, medication need, emergency). The design logic is: eliminate the need to search for anything in darkness or disorientation. Sensor-triggered lighting, bedside controls within lying reach, and bed-head emergency cord are all applications of the same principle.
