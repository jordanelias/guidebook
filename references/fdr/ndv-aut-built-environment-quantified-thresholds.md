## FDR-NDVT-01 — d710 + NDV/AUT → quantified acoustic thresholds

**Primary source:** Appl Sci 2021 — Indoor Acoustic Requirements for Autism-Friendly Spaces (Tier 2, applied acoustics journal). ANSI S12.60-2002 classroom acoustics standard.

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Background noise — activity space | ≤35 dB(A) for NDV-designed spaces; ANSI S12.60 specifies ≤35 dB(A) for classrooms accommodating hearing-impaired and learning-disabled students | NDV auditory hypersensitivity: standard office background noise is 40–50 dB(A); NDV users cannot selectively filter irrelevant sounds — every dB above threshold compounds cognitive load | Appl Sci 2021 (Tier 2); ANSI S12.60-2002 | Tier 2/Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — 35 dB(A) as quantified NDV-specific background noise target absent from BPC |
| RT60 — NDV activity space | ≤0.6s for NDV activity spaces (classrooms, common rooms); ≤0.4s for sensory relief space | NDV: RT60 >0.6s creates smeared auditory environment where individual sounds cannot be distinguished — particularly problematic for auditory processing differences | Appl Sci 2021 (Tier 2); Architizer; ANSI S12.60 | Tier 2/Co-2 | **NOVEL** — RT60 ≤0.6s as quantified NDV activity space target absent from BPC |
| HVAC noise — NDV | Mechanical ventilation noise ≤25 dB(A) in NDV-designed spaces; avoid units with tonal hum at 50/60Hz | NDV auditory hypersensitivity: HVAC hum at 50/60Hz is at same frequency as fluorescent flicker — dual-channel trigger; low hum that is tolerable for neurotypical users is a persistent irritant for hypersensitive users | Appl Sci 2021; Architizer | Tier 2/Co-2 | **NOVEL** — HVAC noise target for NDV spaces absent from BPC |

## FDR-NDVT-02 — d710 + NDV/AUT → quantified lighting thresholds

**Sources:** PMC9748440 (light/colour ASD case study, Tier 3); Architizer ASD sensory design; ScienceDirect multi-sensory ASD design

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Illuminance — NDV activity space | 200–300 lux for general activity; avoid >500 lux in NDV-designed spaces | NDV visual hypersensitivity: high illuminance (standard office 500 lux) creates visual overload; reduced illuminance reduces activation without compromising task performance for most NDV users | PMC9748440 (Tier 3); OT clinical reasoning | Tier 3/Co-2 | **NOVEL** — illuminance target for NDV activity spaces absent from BPC |
| Colour temperature — NDV | 2700–3500K (warm white); avoid cool-white >5000K | NDV: cool, blue-spectrum lighting is documented as an arousal trigger in ASD; warm-spectrum reduces activation and supports regulation | PMC9748440; Architizer | Tier 3/Co-2 | **NOVEL** — colour temperature range as NDV-specific lighting specification absent from BPC |
| Flicker — zero tolerance | Flicker-free LED mandatory (>3000Hz PWM or DC-driven); measured flicker percentage <5% | NDV visual hypersensitivity: any perceptible or sub-perceptible flicker above 0 contributes to arousal accumulation — zero-flicker specification is stronger than mere "LED" specification | Architizer; OT clinical reasoning | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — zero-flicker specification (flicker percentage target) as NDV lighting requirement absent from BPC |
| Individual dimming control — sensory room | Full dimming 0–100% in sensory relief space; user-operable without standing | NDV self-regulation: environmental control over light level is a self-regulation tool; dimmable user control shifts agency to the person | UniqueCS; DYNSEO; Autism Spectrum News | Co-2 | REFINES FDR-SRS-01; confirms from quantified thresholds angle |
