**Clinical reasoning (transfer exertion at 420mm) [GAP-SYNTH-01 RESOLVED 2026-05-06]:** Standard bench seating at 420mm AFF requires greater sit-to-stand (STS) exertion due to deeper hip flexion at transfer initiation. For OFS/POTS users, STS exertion is an orthostatic trigger and a PEM trigger — reducing the energy cost of transfer is clinically significant. ≥480mm AFF reduces STS exertion by decreasing hip flexion depth. This is the same ergonomic rationale used for raised toilet seats in OT practice (standard toilet ~400mm + 75–125mm riser). **CORRECTION:** The prior version attributed this to venous pooling from hip flexion angle (Roxburgh 2024). Citation mining (2026-05-06) confirmed Roxburgh 2024 (PMID:40337282) is about time diaries, not seat height. The venous pooling rationale is also contradicted by POTS management guidance (Johns Hopkins: some POTS patients prefer low chairs to reduce dependent pooling during sitting). The correct rationale is transfer ergonomics — reduced STS exertion. The 480mm value is derived from standard raised-seating practice (Co-2), not from a specific study. ○ [CONFIDENCE: moderate — mechanism sound (STS exertion triggers orthostatic symptoms); specific 480mm threshold is OT practice convention, not empirically derived]

**Universal Mode candidate:** Rest seating alcove recess geometry (≥200mm recess + ≥900mm width) — applies across fatigue, mobility, pain, dementia, and neurological populations with no cross-population conflicts. Route to connection-scout.
