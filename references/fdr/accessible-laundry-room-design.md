## FDR-LAU-01 — d465 + wheelchair/mobility → laundry room (appliance selection, turning space, approach)

**Sources:** US Access Board ADA §611; SDSU Extension; UD Project laundry; T-Square universal design laundry

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Appliance type — front-load mandatory | Front-loading washer and dryer; side-by-side not stacked; doors opening away from each other | WC users: top-loading requires forward lean into drum not achievable from seated position; stacked units place dryer controls out of reach range; side-by-side with outward-opening doors allows transfer between machines from one approach position | US Access Board §611; UDLL; UD Project | Tier 5–6/Co-2 | CONFIRMS front-load; **NOVEL** — door opening direction (away from each other) as spatial specification absent from BPC |
| Pedestal height — appliances | 300–400mm (12–16 inch) pedestal raises appliance opening to direct line-of-sight from WC seat level | WC: without pedestal, drum opening at floor level requires head-down reach into machine — inaccessible for many WC users; pedestal raises opening to ~500mm AFF, within comfortable reach and sightline | UDLL; T-Square; AgeInPlace | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — pedestal specification with sightline rationale absent from BPC |
| Clear turning space | ≥1524mm (60 inch / 5 foot) turning circle in front of washer and dryer combined | WC: approach must allow lateral repositioning between washer and dryer; standard 60-inch turning circle applies; additional 30×48 inch parallel approach zone per ADA §611.2 | US Access Board §611; T-Square; UD Project | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS turning space; REFINES with laundry-specific combined machine approach context |
| Counter height — sorting/folding | ≤864mm (34 inch) AFF; knee clearance ≥700mm height × ≥760mm depth | WC: front approach to sorting/folding surface requires knee clearance; 34-inch counter allows forward reach to full depth from seated position | SDSU Extension; UD Project | Tier 5–6/Co-2 | CONFIRMS counter height; adds laundry-specific context |
| Laundry room location — main floor | Main-floor location mandatory; basement or garage laundry is a barrier that elevator or dumbwaiter may address but not resolve | WC/mobility: stair access to laundry is a functional barrier; main-floor location is the primary specification — all other accessible laundry features are irrelevant if the room requires stairs | SDSU Extension; UD Project; UDLL | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — laundry room floor level as primary accessibility specification absent from BPC |

## FDR-LAU-02 — d445 + UPL → laundry room (controls, handling, one-hand operation)

**Sources:** ADA §611; UDLL; OT clinical reasoning

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Controls — rotary knobs excluded | Push-button, touch pad, or paddle controls; avoid rotary dials requiring grip and rotation | UPL: rotary dials require the grip-and-rotate motion that is inaccessible for many UPL conditions (hemiplegia, spasticity, reduced grip); large buttons operable with palm or fist | US Access Board §611.3; OT clinical reasoning | Tier 5–6/Co-2 | CONFIRMS operable parts; adds UPL-specific rotary knob exclusion rationale |
| Laundry product storage — reachable one-hand | All frequently used products within seated reach zone at counter height (380–1100mm AFF); unit-dose dispensing preferred over heavy jugs | UPL: lifting, pouring, and measuring from heavy liquid containers requires bilateral upper limb function; unit-dose pods eliminate this entirely | OT clinical reasoning; SDSU Extension | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — unit-dose product strategy as UPL-specific laundry room provision absent from BPC |
| D-handle on all storage | D-pull handles on all cabinet doors and drawers | UPL: D-pulls are operable with finger hook or palm — no tight grip required; knob handles require sustained grip inaccessible to many UPL users | AgeInPlace laundry; ADA §309 | Tier 5–6/Co-2 | CONFIRMS D-handle; adds UPL functional mechanism |
