## FDR-VI-01 — d450 + visual impairment → circulation (tactile guidance, surface contrast)

**Sources:** ISO 23599:2019 Tactile Walking Surface Indicators (international standard); Tactile paving Wikipedia (multi-jurisdiction survey); Veroniiiica.com (VI user perspective); RICS Journal (UK guidance)

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tactile attention indicator (warning) — pattern | Truncated domes/cones in grid pattern; raised 4–5mm above base surface; bevelled edges | VI users approaching hazard (stair top/bottom, ramp entry, platform edge, level change) — detectable underfoot and by long cane | ISO 23599:2019; AS 1428.4.1 | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS existing VI BPC |

| Reflective/shiny floor surfaces — contraindicated | Avoid highly polished or reflective floor finishes in VI circulation paths | Low vision: glare from reflective floors creates bright spots that obscure spatial information and cause pain for photosensitive users | Veroniiiica.com (VI user); OT clinical reasoning | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — reflective floor as VI-specific spatial barrier absent from BPC |
| Overhead obstacles — cane detection gap | All obstacles ≤2000mm AFF within circulation path must be detectable at floor level (e.g., by cane sweep or fixed element); overhead branches/objects above waist not detectable by cane | VI/blind users: long cane detects ground-level and leg-height obstacles; overhead or torso-height obstacles are invisible to cane — building design must eliminate or provide detectability at sweep height | Veroniiiica.com (VI user); O&M principles | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — cane detection gap height (above waist = undetectable) as design constraint absent from BPC |

**Clinical reasoning (cane detection gap):** The long white cane sweeps at approximately ground level and detects obstacles from floor up to approximately mid-shin. Objects above waist height (overhanging cabinets, projecting signs, trees, ATM surrounds) are in the cane detection gap — completely invisible to a blind user until they make contact. This is a fundamental orientation and mobility (O&M) principle that translates directly to built environment specification: no element should project into the circulation path above 300mm AFF unless it is detectable at 300mm AFF (i.e., has a base element at that height that the cane will contact first).

## FDR-VI-02 — d460 + visual impairment → level changes (stair nosing, tactile warning at approaches)

**Sources:** UK Approved Document M (Vol 2, §1 stepped access); Approved Document K (stair safety); RICS Journal (stair nosing LRV guidance); DDA/Equality Act 2010 (30 LRV difference); Safe Tread (BS 8493:2008+A1:2010 LRV testing)

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Stair nosing contrast material — width | 55mm wide on both tread and riser; permanently contrasting material | VI approaching stairs — nosing must be visible at approach and at each step during descent; 55mm provides sufficient visual target | UK Approved Document M Vol 2 §1 | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS nosing requirement; **NOVEL** — 55mm width specification and tread+riser requirement absent from BPC |
| Stair nosing contrast — LRV difference | ≥30 LRV difference between nosing and adjacent stair surface | VI with residual vision — ≥30 LRV is the minimum discriminable contrast for partial sight; consistent with DDA/Equality Act 2010 guidance | DDA guidance; BS 8493:2008+A1:2010; RICS Journal | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS LRV concept; **NOVEL** — 30 LRV as specific minimum for nosing absent from BPC |
| Nosing — full stair width | Nosing extends full width of stair | VI/blind users navigating with cane — cane sweep must contact nosing across full width regardless of approach angle | UK Approved Document M; RICS guidance | Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — full-width nosing rationale for cane detection absent from BPC |
| Open risers — prohibited for VI | Avoid open-riser stairs in VI circulation routes | VI/blind: open risers allow cane to pass through without detecting the step edge — critical fall hazard | UK Approved Document M | Tier 5–6 | **NOVEL** — open-riser prohibition with VI-specific cane-detection rationale absent from BPC |
| Corduroy hazard warning surface at stair head/foot | Installed at top and bottom landings of each flight; tactile material distinct from adjacent floor; extends full width of path | VI approaching stair top/bottom — corduroy pattern signals imminent level change before step edge is reached | UK Approved Document M | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS TWSI at stair; **NOVEL** — corduroy-specific (not truncated dome) as stair approach TWSI type, with full-width and landing position requirement, absent from BPC |

**Clinical reasoning (LRV 30 as functional minimum):** The 30 LRV difference threshold is not aesthetic guidance — it is derived from contrast sensitivity research showing that people with partial sight (the largest VI subgroup) require at minimum this level of luminance contrast to reliably distinguish two adjacent surfaces. Below 30 LRV, the edge of the step becomes indistinguishable from the riser under typical building lighting conditions. The 55mm nosing width ensures the contrasting band occupies enough visual angle to be perceived at approach speed.
