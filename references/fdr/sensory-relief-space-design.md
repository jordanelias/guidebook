## FDR-SRS-01 — d710 + sensory hypersensitivity → sensory relief space (geometry, acoustic, light)

**Sources:** Appl Sci 2021 (indoor acoustic requirements autism-friendly spaces, Tier 2); Vibe By Vision NDV acoustic design; UniqueCS sensory room; Architizer ASD sensory guide

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Minimum usable area | ≥4m² (approx. 2000×2000mm); ≥6m² preferred for paired use | NDV hypersensitivity: space too small creates claustrophobic response; space must accommodate user at rest with companion without forced proximity | OT clinical reasoning; DYNSEO | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — minimum floor area for sensory relief space absent from BPC |
| Acoustic isolation — background noise target | Background noise ≤35 dB(A) with door closed; acoustic panels/soft furnishings on minimum 2 walls | NDV auditory hypersensitivity: standard occupied building noise (40–55 dB(A)) is above threshold tolerable for severe hypersensitivity; ≤35 dB(A) provides meaningful reduction | Vibe By Vision; OT clinical reasoning; NAS noise research | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — background noise target for sensory relief space absent from BPC |
| Reverberation time — RT60 target | RT60 ≤0.4s for sensory relief space; achieve with carpet/soft furnishings on ≥50% of floor and ceiling absorption | NDV: reverberant spaces cause auditory smearing that is disproportionately distressing for hypersensitive users; ≤0.4s creates acoustically "dead" space analogous to a conference room | Appl Sci 2021 ASD acoustic review (Tier 2); Biamp RT60 targets | Tier 2/Co-2 | **NOVEL** — RT60 target for sensory relief space absent from BPC |
| Fluorescent lighting — excluded | No fluorescent tubes or CFLs; dimmable warm LED (2700–3000K) minimum; full dimming range 0–100% | NDV visual/auditory hypersensitivity: fluorescent flicker (50–120Hz) and audible hum are dual-trigger sensory stressors; LED eliminates both | Architizer; UniqueCS; DYNSEO | Co-2 | CONFIRMS FDR-NDV-02; cross-slug corroboration for lighting specification |
| Visual control — blackout capability | Full blackout blinds or equivalent; dimmable artificial light sole source during use | NDV visual hypersensitivity: uncontrolled external light variation (clouds, vehicles, glare) is a distraction trigger; full environmental control is required for regulation | UniqueCS; Architizer | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — blackout capability as sensory relief space specification absent from BPC |
| Tactile variety — floor zoning | Two or more distinct floor textures delineating zones (e.g., soft foam/mat area for movement, carpet tile area for seated rest) | NDV proprioceptive and tactile processing: distinct tactile zones allow user-selected input type; variety without visual instruction | UniqueCS; DYNSEO | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — floor texture zoning as sensory relief space specification absent from BPC |
| Direct access — no corridor dependence | Sensory relief space accessible from primary activity zone without requiring corridor transit | NDV: person approaching meltdown may have reduced ability to navigate complex routes; direct access enables use when capacity is most depleted | DYNSEO; OT clinical reasoning | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — direct access geometry as sensory relief space provision absent from BPC |
