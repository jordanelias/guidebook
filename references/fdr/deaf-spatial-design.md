## FDR-DEAF-01 — d310 + deaf / HoH → spatial communication (sightlines, signing space, lighting)

**Sources:** Gallaudet University DeafSpace guidelines (2009); RDG Planning & Design Deaf Urbanism; NEA DeafSpace article; KODAheart; Medium/E&E Architects

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Signing space — minimum interpersonal distance | ≥1500mm between communicating parties for dyadic sign; ≥2400mm for group conversation circle | Deaf/signing: sign language requires clear sightline to face AND full signing space (torso to head); proximity closer than 1500mm obscures lower signing space | Gallaudet DeafSpace guidelines; RDG Deaf Urbanism | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — signing space as interpersonal distance specification absent from BPC |
| Clear sightlines — open plan preferred | Avoid dividing walls, columns, or visual obstacles between primary occupation zones and entry paths; glazed or open connections between adjacent spaces | Deaf: spatial awareness and social orientation rely on visual monitoring of surroundings; walled rooms restrict ability to know who is approaching | Gallaudet DeafSpace; NEA | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — visual sightline provision as deaf-specific layout specification absent from BPC |
| Glazed corners at corridor intersections | Glass panels at corridor corners replace solid walls; allows visual preview of approaching person before collision | DeafSpace: rounded or blind corners cause collisions as deaf pedestrians cannot hear approaching footsteps; glazed corner provides visual advance warning | NEA DeafSpace (Gallaudet iterative lesson); Gallaudet guidelines | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — glazed corridor corner as deaf-specific collision prevention absent from BPC |
| Lighting — diffuse, glare-free | Diffuse natural or artificial light; no direct glare sources behind signer or in signer's eye-line; avoid harsh contrast between signing person and background | Deaf: eye strain during sustained visual communication (signing) is directly affected by glare and background contrast — signing against a bright window is effectively invisible | Gallaudet DeafSpace; NEA; RDG | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — glare-free signing light as deaf-specific lighting specification absent from BPC |
| Furniture — moveable for conversation circles | All seating in communal areas moveable; default arrangement avoids theatre/classroom rows; circular/semi-circular preferred | Deaf: group communication requires all participants in mutual sightline — fixed linear seating excludes peripheral participants from signing conversation | Gallaudet DeafSpace; RDG Deaf Urbanism | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — moveable furniture and circular seating as deaf-specific communal design absent from BPC |
| Communal hub — central to movement | Communal space at centre of dwelling/building circulation flow; allows visual awareness of others' movement | Deaf: social/spatial awareness achieved through peripheral vision of movement, not sound; central communal hub allows passive spatial monitoring | KODAheart DeafSpace features; Gallaudet | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — central communal hub for deaf spatial awareness absent from BPC |

## FDR-DEAF-02 — d310 + deaf → alerting systems (visual and vibrotactile)

**Sources:** KODAheart DeafSpace; Deaf Vibes deaf apartments; NEA Gallaudet; Medium DeafSpace

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Visual door alerting | Video doorbell with visual flash receiver in all rooms; flashing strobe at door and at receiver in each primary space | Deaf: auditory doorbell inaudible; visual flash alert must be perceptible from any room in dwelling — single entry-only flash is insufficient | KODAheart; Deaf Vibes | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — multi-room visual doorbell alert coverage as deaf-specific provision absent from BPC |
| Visual fire alarm — all spaces | Visual strobe fire alarm in every room including bathrooms; flash rate and intensity to ADA/NFPA 72 standard | Deaf: auditory fire alarm inaudible; visual alarm required in all occupied spaces without exception; bathroom inclusion is the most commonly missed space | ADA/NFPA 72; KODAheart | Tier 5–6 | CONFIRMS visual fire alarm requirement; **NOVEL** — bathroom inclusion emphasis and all-rooms standard as deaf-specific gap absent from BPC |
| Vibrotactile floor alerting — bed alarms | Vibrating device or vibrotactile floor pad at bedside for nocturnal alerts (fire, doorbell, baby monitor) | Deaf: visual alerts ineffective during sleep; vibrotactile is the primary nocturnal alerting channel | KODAheart; Deaf Vibes | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — vibrotactile nocturnal alerting as built-environment provision (wiring, pad mounting point) absent from BPC |
| Surface vibration transmission — flooring | Flooring material must transmit vibration effectively; soft carpet absorbs vibration signal; hard or semi-hard flooring preferred in deaf-occupied spaces | Deaf: floor-stomp communication (calling attention across room) and vibrotactile alerts both rely on vibration propagation — thick carpet damps signal | KODAheart DeafSpace features; OT clinical reasoning | Co-2 | **NOVEL** — flooring vibration transmission as deaf-specific floor specification absent from BPC |
