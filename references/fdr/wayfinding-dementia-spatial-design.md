## FDR-DEMWAY-01 — d460 + dementia → floor plate geometry (corridor layout, visibility)

**Sources:** PMC8725382; PMC11931140; PMC8545728

| Parameter | Value | Condition | Source | Tier | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Corridor — no dead ends | Continuous circulation loop (or clear visible termination); avoid corridors that end without destination | Dementia: dead-end corridors create entrapment perception and disorientation — person continues until path ends without understanding why; loops allow continuous movement without decision requirement | PMC8725382 | Tier 2 | **NOVEL** — corridor loop geometry as dementia-specific floor plate specification absent from BPC |
| Key room visibility from corridor | Bathroom, dining room, and activity room doors visible from bedroom doors and corridor decision points | Dementia wayfinding case studies: destinations that are directly visible are reached significantly more reliably than those requiring navigation through unseen space | PMC11931140; PMC8725382 | Tier 2 | REFINES FDR-DEM-01 destination visibility; adds floor plate geometry implication |
| Toilet visible from common areas | WC entry visible from or directly adjacent to primary common areas | Dementia + incontinence: inability to locate toilet quickly enough is a primary incontinence trigger — visual access to WC from common areas reduces navigation demand to zero | PMC6867684; OT clinical reasoning | Tier 2/Co-2 | **NOVEL** — WC visibility from common areas as dementia-specific spatial adjacency absent from BPC |
| Corridor width — social visibility | ≥1800mm clear width in corridors used by dementia residents — allows person to pause and observe without blocking circulation | Dementia case study: participants frequently paused at high-visibility points to orient; narrow corridors prevent orientation pausing without blocking others | PMC11931140 | Tier 2 | **NOVEL** — dementia-specific corridor pausing allowance as width specification absent from BPC |
| Outdoor access — safe enclosed garden | Direct, visible, level access to enclosed outdoor space; exit visible from primary common area; no gate that appears to be a domestic front door | Dementia: walking is a common regulatory behaviour; safely enclosed outdoor space provides wandering outlet without elopement risk; door that resembles a domestic front door triggers elopement | Universal Design Guidelines; PMC8545728 | Co-2/Tier 2 | **NOVEL** — enclosed garden access with dementia-specific door specification absent from BPC |
