## SECTION A: New-Build Accessibility Premium (§11.1 / §11.2.1)

**The core finding:** Accessible design integrated from project inception adds <1% to total construction cost for commercial/public buildings and 0.25–3% for residential, depending on scale and type.

### Synthesis — New-Build Premium

**Commercial / public buildings:** 0.006% – 0.13% (Schroeder & Steinfeld). Confirmed <1% by World Bank, ADA National Network, Singapore URA.

**Multi-family residential:** 0.25% – 1.0% (Schroeder & Steinfeld, CMHC, French, Swedish studies). German study at 3.01–3.2% is outlier but author dismisses as negligible.

**Single-family residential:** 0.5% – 3.0% (Schroeder & Steinfeld, HUD). Highest premium at smallest building scale. Cost-per-unit differential dilutes to <0.5% at project level (Ottawa evidence).

**Critical insight (World Bank 2005):** In two studies redesigning 17 buildings total, no additional space was necessary — only rearrangement of existing plans. The presumption that accessibility requires more space is empirically false for most building types.

| Building Type | New-Build Premium | Retrofit Cost (% of original) | Ratio (retrofit ÷ new-build) |

| Single-family housing | 3.0% | 21.0% | ~7× |

Source: Schroeder & Steinfeld (1979). Both comparisons in same study using same buildings.

**Headline finding:** Retrofit costs 4–20× more than building-in from the start. The single-family ratio (7×) is the most relevant for residential guidebook context; the commercial ratio (4–20×) for non-residential.
