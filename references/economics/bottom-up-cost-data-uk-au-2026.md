## United Kingdom (GBP)

### Funding Framework
- **Disabled Facilities Grant (DFG):** Up to £30,000 (England), £36,000 (Wales), £25,000 (Northern Ireland)
- **Total DFG allocation 2024–26:** £711 million (increased to £761 million with £50m top-up for 2025–26)
- **~50,000 people/year** benefit from DFG
- **Scotland:** Scheme of Assistance (separate mechanism, not DFG)
- **Minor adaptations ≤£1,000** (grab rails, dropped kerbs, outside lights): provided free by local authority — no DFG application needed
- **Means-tested** for adults; **not means-tested** for children under 18/19
- **VAT relief:** 0% VAT for disabled/chronically sick persons; 5% VAT for over-60s

## Australia (AUD)

### Funding Framework
- **NDIS Home Modifications:** Classified by cost/complexity
  - **Category A minor:** Under $10,000 (grab rails, lever taps, portable ramps)
  - **Category B minor:** $10,000–$20,000 (non-structural; bathroom floor mods, doorway widening)
  - **Complex Home Modifications (CHM):** Over $20,000; structural changes; requires OT assessment + builder quotes + permits
  - **CHM over $30,000:** Requires NDIS-registered Building Works Project Manager
- **NDIS total funding 2025–26:** $44.6 billion (all categories)
- **751,446 active participants** as of September 2025
- **ROI evidence:** $2.25 return for every $1 spent on home modifications (NDIS research)
- **Victorian Aids & Equipment Program (VA&EP):** State-level subsidies for grab rails, showers, non-slip flooring
- **Australian Standard AS 1428.1:** Governs accessible design dimensions (shower base min 1160mm × 1100mm; ideal 1500mm × 1500mm)
