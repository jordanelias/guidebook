### Canadian standards landscape (2025-2026)

Three-tier Canadian accessibility standards architecture:
1. **CSA/ASC B651:23** — Accessible design for the built environment (public/commercial)
2. **CSA/ASC B652:23** — Accessible dwellings (residential — first edition Jan 2023)
3. **CAN/ASC-2.8:2025** — Accessible-Ready Housing (voluntary; highest ambition)

**2024 Ontario Building Code** (effective Jan 1, 2025): largest OBC change ever with 1,700+ items revised. Harmonized with NBC. All pedestrian entrances barrier-free. ≥15% of multi-unit suites with basic accessibility. References CSA B652:23.

**CMHC Housing Design Catalogue** (2025-2026): standardized accessible-ready and enhanced-accessible designs with Class B construction cost estimates. Regional designs. Supports prefab/off-site construction. Aligned with ASC 2.8 and CSA B652:23.

### Cross-reference to CMHC/Société Logique cost data

The CMHC/Société Logique 2015 study (see workplan/economics-audit-research-2026-05-03.md §B.1) found 6-12% per-dwelling cost premium for 60-feature accessibility across five Canadian cities. This cost premium applies to comprehensive accessibility; basic visitability provisions (step-free entry, wider doors, reinforced walls) cluster at the lower end of the individual-feature cost distribution (57% of features at $0-$100).

The 2024 Ontario Building Code's 15% barrier-free suite requirement and the NCC 2022 Silver-equivalent provisions represent the basic-visitability end of this spectrum. The CSA B652:23 and CAN/ASC-2.8:2025 represent the comprehensive end.
