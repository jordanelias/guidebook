## 1. The Economic Case: Design-Stage Inclusion vs. Retrofit

*Framing note: economics-researcher rule 1 — positive case first.*

### 1.1 New-Build Universal Design Premium

The cost of incorporating universal design at the construction stage is well-documented across multiple independent studies and consistently falls below conventional assumptions:

| Source | Tier | New-build premium | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| ADA National Network (2025) | E-6 | <1% of construction cost | US; all newly constructed public accommodation |
| Rick Hansen Foundation / HCMA (2024) | E-6 | ~1% or zero | Canada; RHFAC certification levels across public, commercial, residential |
| Ielegems & Vanrie (2024) | E-1 | 0.94%–3.92% (avg 1.19% for large projects) | Belgium; 12 cases across schools, town halls, retail — peer-reviewed |
| Société Logique / CMHC (2015) | E-1 | Not directly accessed | Canada; modest residential houses |

**Corroboration status: STRONG.** Four independent sources across three countries and two evidence tiers converge on <1% to ~2% for most building types, rising to ~4% only for small/complex typologies. This meets economics-researcher rule 4 (≥2 independent sources).

### 1.2 Retrofit Cost Penalty

| Source | Tier | Retrofit premium | Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ielegems & Vanrie (2024) | E-1 | 2.24%–14.9% of total building cost | 12 cases; renovation to 100% inclusive |
| ADA National Network / Page Foundation (2025) | E-6 | 2%–20% of project cost | US; retrofitting same features that cost <1% at construction |
| HUD/Woodstock (2020) | E-2 | $12,200–$15,150 per unit (average) | Chicago CDBG HomeMod; 1,100+ modifications 2003–2018 |

**Key ratio:** Retrofit costs are 2× to 15× higher than design-stage inclusion. Elevators, sanitary facilities, corridors, doors, and ramps are the most expensive retrofit categories (Ielegems 2024).

### 1.3 ⚑ Opus Review Required

The new-build vs. retrofit cost differential is the strongest quantitative argument for Part 10 (Adaptable Readiness) and the guidebook's foundational case for design-stage inclusion. Per standing rule 6, Sonnet flags this for Opus synthesis. The evidence base is strong (E-1 + E-6 corroborated), but the economic framing for the guidebook requires best-practice determination.
