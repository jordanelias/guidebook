### Best-practice synthesis
**PROVISIONAL (pre-Opus, from 2026-03-18 — will be superseded after Co-1 pass and Opus re-synthesis):**
**Most inclusive provision:** Trauma-informed design (TID) throughout — safety, choice, collaboration, empowerment, and cultural humility as spatial principles; quiet retreat room provided in all multi-occupancy settings.
**Most targeted provision:** De-escalation/safe retreat room (distinct from A-16 sensory room); predictable spatial sequence; no dead-end corridors; user control of lighting and acoustic conditions.
**Conflict resolution:** NDV/AUT muted palette vs NDV/MH (PTSD) bright-colour preference — spatial separation required; adjustable multi-mode design preferred.
**Highest-ambition actionable specification:** Dedicated quiet retreat room ≥9 m² with user-adjustable lighting (2700–6500 K), acoustic treatment (RT60 ≤0.4 s), and no through-traffic; all corridor routes loop or terminate at a named destination.

### PubMed academic pass (2026-03-27)

**Oostermeijer, Brasier, Harvey, Hamilton, Roper, Martel, Fletcher, Brophy (2021)**
Source: BMJ Open 11(7):e046647. DOI: 10.1136/bmjopen-2020-046647. [Tier 3 rapid systematic review]
[PubMed PMID: 34233981]
Populations: NDV/MH / ALL (adult psychiatric inpatient)
35 studies. Design features reducing seclusion and restraint: beneficial physical environment, sensory/comfort rooms, private/uncrowded/calm spaces.
Evidence quality: Tier 3. Evidence marker: ●

**Faerden, Rosenqvist, Håkansson et al. (2022)**
Source: HERD 16(2):55–72. DOI: 10.1177/19375867221136558. [Co-1 / Tier 1 pre-post with control]
[PubMed PMID: 36567605]
Populations: NDV/MH (acute psychiatric, seclusion unit). NO/Oslo University Hospital.
User-driven service design. N=618 questionnaires, 159 staff, 4-week pre-post with control. ES: Cohen's d = 2.0 (patient support), 1.7 (staff support).
Design concepts: Welcoming atmosphere (oak frame, Nordic colours), contact with nature (landscapes, wood flooring), room for privacy (bathrooms, cupboards), close staff contact, designated smoking area.
Evidence quality: Co-1 + Tier 1 (pre-post with control). Evidence marker: ●

**Haig, Hallett (2022)**
Source: Int J Ment Health Nurs 32(1):54–75. DOI: 10.1111/inm.13065. [Tier 3 systematic review]
[PubMed PMID: 36082841]
18 studies. Sensory rooms in adult psychiatric inpatient settings. Reduce distress but weak evidence for S&R reduction.
Evidence quality: Tier 3. Evidence marker: ●○

**Price, Papastavrou Brooks, Johnston et al. (2024)**
Source: Health Technol Assess 28(3):1–120. DOI: 10.3310/FGGW6874. [Tier 1 feasibility trial]
[PubMed PMID: 38343036]
EDITION: co-produced de-escalation intervention including physical environment changes. 10 UK wards. Signal of effect on conflict/containment.
Evidence quality: Tier 1 feasibility. Evidence marker: ●○

### Citation mining pass (2026-03-27)

**van der Schaaf, Dusseldorp, Keuning, Janssen, Noorthoorn (2013)**
Source: British Journal of Psychiatry 202:142–149. DOI: 10.1192/bjp.bp.112.118422. [Tier 1 multicentre]
[PubMed PMID: 23307922]
Populations: NDV/MH / ALL. NL. 199 wards, 23,868 admissions, 14,834 patients.
14 design features significantly affect seclusion risk. Private space per patient, comfort level, visibility REDUCE seclusion. Outdoor space, special safety measures, large patient numbers INCREASE seclusion.
Evidence quality: Tier 1. Evidence marker: ●

**Bodryzlova, Lemieux, Dufour, Côté, Lalancette, Crocker (2024)**
Source: J Psychiatr Res 178:94–106. DOI: 10.1016/j.jpsychires.2024.07.045. [Tier 3 umbrella review]
[PubMed PMID: 39128221]
CA (Montreal). 14 reviews analysed. Realist approach. Design features address stress, social interaction, autonomy, control, privacy/dignity, under-/overstimulation management.
Evidence quality: Tier 3. Evidence marker: ●

**Schreiber, Metzger, Flammer, Rinke, Fallgatter, Steinert (2022)**
Source: BMC Health Serv Res 22(1):941. DOI: 10.1186/s12913-022-08322-6. [Tier 1 quasi-experimental controlled]
[PubMed PMID: 35869551]
DE. Open-door policy on acute psychiatric wards. No increase in adverse events. Coercive measures decreased at one site.
Evidence quality: Tier 1. Evidence marker: ●

**Weltens, Drukker, van Amelsvoort, Bak (2023)**
Source: BMJ Open 13(2):e067943. DOI: 10.1136/bmjopen-2022-067943. [Tier 1 ESM]
[PubMed PMID: 36806071]
NL. Experience sampling method. Exceeding bed capacity OR 5.36 for aggression. Ward atmosphere and nurse interaction quality matter.
Evidence quality: Tier 1. Evidence marker: ●

**Husum, Bjørngaard, Finset, Ruud (2010)**
Source: BMC Health Serv Res 10:89. DOI: 10.1186/1472-6963-10-89. [Tier 1 multicentre]
[PubMed PMID: 20370928]
NO. 32 acute psychiatric wards. 1,016 involuntary patients. Substantial between-ward variance in coercive measures. Ward factors influence seclusion/restraint use.
Evidence quality: Tier 1. Evidence marker: ●

**Askew, Fisher, Beazley (2019)**
Source: J Psych Ment Health Nurs 27(3):272–280. DOI: 10.1111/jpm.12576. [Co-1 qualitative]
[PubMed PMID: 31755614]
UK. Forensic inpatients' experience of seclusion room. IPA, N=7. Extreme fear, neglect, abuse, power struggle. Environment shapes traumatic experience.
Evidence quality: Co-1. Evidence marker: ●○

**Quinn, Jutkowitz, Primack et al. (2024)**
Source: Int J Ment Health Nurs 33(3):600–615. DOI: 10.1111/inm.13277. [Tier 3 systematic review]
[PubMed PMID: 38193620]
US (VA). 48 reports. Architecturally positive designs, sensory rooms, Brøset Violence Checklist, multi-component interventions may reduce seclusion. Low certainty.
Evidence quality: Tier 3. Evidence marker: ●○

**De Cuyper, Vanlinthout, Vanhoof et al. (2023)**
Source: J Psych Ment Health Nurs 30(3):580–593. DOI: 10.1111/jpm.12890. [Tier 2 / Co-2 Delphi]
[PubMed PMID: 36565433]
BE. 77 best practice recommendations. Evidence + human rights + consensus. Expert-professionals and experts-by-experience. Covers time limits, second opinion, registration.
Evidence quality: Tier 2 / Co-2. Evidence marker: ●

**Goulet, Lessard-Deschênes (2022)**
Source: Santé mentale au Québec 47(1):151–180. [Tier 3 integrative review]
[PubMed PMID: 36548797]
CA/FR. 138 articles. Ecological model: physical environment = microsystem factor in S&R prevention. French-language source.
Evidence quality: Tier 3. Evidence marker: ●
