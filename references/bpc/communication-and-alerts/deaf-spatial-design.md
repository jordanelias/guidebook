### Best-practice synthesis

**Most inclusive provision:** All primary corridors and circulation routes to be a minimum 2440 mm (8 ft) wide, sufficient for two signers walking in conversation side by side with peripheral scan space; secondary corridors not less than 1830 mm (6 ft). Public exterior pathways to be a minimum 3050 mm (10 ft) wide where signing groups are anticipated. [DeafSpace Design Guidelines, Gallaudet University 2010; DeafScape, Vaughn 2018]

**Most targeted provision:** Sightlines for signed conversation to be unobstructed across a minimum 3 m depth with no mid-field obstacles at shoulder height or below; standing signing conversation space to provide 900 mm–3050 mm (3–10 ft) between interlocutors depending on group size; glazed corners and intersections to replace opaque corners at all circulation junctions. Lighting to be diffuse, matte-reflected, shadow-free, ≥300 lux in communication zones; backlighting to be eliminated; wall colour to provide medium-contrast neutral tones (muted greens, blues) against which skin tone is legible for lip-reading and facial expression. [DeafSpace Design Guidelines 2010; RIT InfoGuides DeafSpace]

**Conflict resolution:** Where fire life-safety requirements (strobes, visual alarms) conflict with glare-free lighting, diffuse strobe placement takes precedence; sign language communication quality is secondary to life-safety alerting but both can be achieved through diffuser technology.

**Highest-ambition actionable specification (guidebook prose register):**
Primary internal corridors serving Deaf users are to achieve a clear width of not less than 2440 mm. Signing conversation distance of 900 mm to 3050 mm is to be accommodated between interlocutors at all gathering nodes. Corridor junctions are to be glazed to provide visual advance warning of approaching persons. Lighting in communication zones is to be diffuse and uniform, achieving not less than 300 lux at face height, with no point sources generating glare or backlighting. Wall and surface finishes are to be matte, with colour selected to contrast clearly with skin tones. Furniture is to be movable and without fixed armrests to allow formation of communication circles. Ramps are to be preferred over stairs on all primary signed-conversation circulation routes.

**Cross-language divergence — IMPORTANT:** The corridor width specification (8 ft / 2440 mm) is derived from ASL proxemics at Gallaudet University. ASL uses a larger signing space than several other signed languages. BSL (UK), DGS (Germany), NGT (Netherlands), LSF (France), and LIS (Italy) all have distinct spatial grammars. The 2440 mm figure is likely conservative for BSL and potentially insufficient for multi-person ASL group conversations. Until cross-language spatial grammar research is completed, 2440 mm is the maximum evidenced minimum but should be treated as an ASL-derived floor, not a universal value. [Syncretic Finding S-06, Compendium v2; concept boundary warning applied]
