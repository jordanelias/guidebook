### Best-practice synthesis
**Most inclusive provision:** Continuous loop circulation with no dead ends, toilet visible from primary seating without navigation, and distinctive 3D object landmarks at every route junction. Removes orientation barriers simultaneously for DEM, NEU, NDV, and VIS populations.

**Most targeted provision (DEM):** Small-group residential scale (≤9 residents per cluster); no repetitive corridor elements; 3D objects (not colour alone) as primary wayfinding cues at decision points; consistent furniture layout across identical room types.

**Most targeted provision (NDV/AUT):** Sequenced single-route flow; transition alcoves at zone boundaries; compartmentalised activity zones; minimal visual clutter in circulation.

**Conflict resolution:** DEM requires high-contrast landmark objects; NDV/AUT requires minimal visual clutter. Resolution: landmarks placed only at navigationally relevant decision points using 3D objects rather than wall graphics, satisfying both populations simultaneously.

**Opus synthesis note:** This conflict resolution is one of the strongest in the compendium — both populations are served without compromise. The 3D object (not 2D graphic) distinction is clinically meaningful: DEM users process three-dimensional landmarks more reliably than flat images; NDV/AUT users are not triggered by isolated 3D objects the way they are by complex wall surfaces.

**Highest-ambition actionable specification:**
- Loop circulation; no dead ends; single clear route per destination
- 3D object landmark at every junction (culturally familiar, high-contrast, positionally consistent)
- Toilet visible from primary occupied space without navigation
- Consistent furniture arrangement within room types; no mirror-image pairs
- Residential cluster ≤9 persons (DEM); transition alcoves at zone boundaries (NDV/AUT)
- Single-word + pictogram signage at all junctions; suspended signs ≥2250mm AFF; sans-serif typeface

### Consensus findings
| Finding | Languages confirming | Jurisdictions confirming | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| Loop circulation / no dead ends reduces DEM spatial disorientation | EN, DE, NL, FR, NO, SV, KO, JA, ZH | UK, DE, NL, NO, SG, AU, CA, US, KR, JP | Tier 3 (van Buuren & Mohammadi 2022; Marquardt 2011) + Co-1 (DSDC EADDAT 2022) |
| Toilet visibility from primary seating reduces distress and incontinence incidents | EN, DE, NL, FR | UK, DE, NL, US | Tier 3 (Marquardt 2011; Davis & Weisbeck 2016) |
| 3D object landmarks at decision points > colour-coded walls for DEM orientation | EN, DE, NL, JA | UK, DE, NL, JP, AU | Tier 3 (Marquardt 2011; van Buuren 2022) |
| Small-group residential scale (≤9) supports DEM wayfinding and autonomy | EN, DE, NL, NO, FR, JA | UK, DE, NL, NO, FR, JP, SG | Tier 5/Co-1 (KDA; DSDC; Helsedirektoratet; SG AIC-CLC; JP group home standards) |
| Consistent furniture layout critical for DEM tactile memory navigation | EN, DE, NL, FR, SG | UK, DE, NL, SG | Tier 3 + Co-1 (DSDC; Alzheimer Nederland) |
| NDV/AUT: spatial sequencing, escape spaces, transition zones, compartmentalisation | EN, DE, IT, NL | UK, US, IE, DE, IT, NL | Tier 3 (Tola et al. 2021; Black et al. 2022; ASPECTSS 2.0 Mostafa 2021) |
| One-directional or sequenced circulation preferred for AUT | EN | US, IE, AU | Tier 3 (Mostafa 2023; Black et al. 2022) |

### Divergent findings
| Topic | Lang/Jurisdiction A | Lang/Jurisdiction B | Cause |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dementia unit size | SG AIC-CLC (≤6–8, HDB high-density) | DE KDA (≤9, suburban) | Built environment constraint |

| Outdoor loop routes | AU/NZ (secure garden loop = normative best practice) | ZH/KO (outdoor access less common; safety primary) | Cultural care model differences |
