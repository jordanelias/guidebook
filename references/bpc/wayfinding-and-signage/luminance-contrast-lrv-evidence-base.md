**Consensus finding:** Luminance contrast thresholds for the built environment are NOT internationally consistent. At least six mathematically incompatible formula types exist across jurisdictions, and several major jurisdictions (China, South Korea, Denmark) specify no numerical threshold at all. The "≥30 LRV difference" framing is specific to UK/AU/ES and should not be presented as a global consensus. Even jurisdictions that mandate numerical thresholds use different formulas (LRV subtraction, Michelson ratio, NCS lightness, luminance ratio, percentage-based), meaning identical surfaces can pass in one jurisdiction and fail in another. The UK-origin 30% threshold derives from DDA regulatory guidance, not peer-reviewed empirical evidence.
**Key evidence:**
  - Regulatory origin confirmed: 30% threshold adopted from UK DDA guidance; directly carried into AS 1428.1:2009 and AS 1428.1:2021 without independent empirical validation (GAP-007 research, 2026)
  - Functional benefit of contrast enhancement: real-world camera study found combined step-face and edge contrast enhancement reduced fall-related events vs. control stairway (OR = 2.87, p = .023) — supports enhancement but does not validate 30% as a threshold (Harper et al. 2022, Tier 3, Journal of Ergonomics DOI: 10.35248/2165-7556-22.12.303)
  - High-contrast nosing significantly reduced descending gait speed (coeff. −0.07, p = .010), consistent with improved hazard perception (Brown et al. 2023, Tier 3, Ergonomics DOI: 10.1080/00140139.2022.2141347)
  - Computational modelling: low-contrast nosings become invisible at moderate visual impairment levels even at 30% LRV under certain lighting conditions (Thompson 2017/2022, PMC PMC8993136)
  - For people with severe visual impairment: approximately 65% Michelson contrast (equivalent to ~75% LRV difference) is needed for reliable surface boundary detection — approximately 2.5× the current 30% standard (Dain et al., cited in Manandhar et al. 2022)
  - CNIB (2024): explicitly states 30% LRV as a minimum regulatory floor, not a functional optimum; recommends maximum achievable contrast
  - DSDC EADDAT validation (Marquardt 2011): uses contrast as binary variable (present/absent) — does not validate specific threshold
**Practical guidance specification (replacing the 30% floor as design target):**
  - Code compliance minimum: varies by jurisdiction — ≥30 LRV difference (UK, AU, ES for doors); ≥0.4 Michelson (DE, NO general); ≥0.8 Michelson (NO warning/signage); ≥0.40 NCS lightness (SE); ≥40% luminance contrast (IT); qualitative only (JP, CN, KR, DK, FI, NL). No single number applies globally.
  - Guidebook best practice target: ≥50% LRV difference (NIHR-funded review recommendation; consistent with US Access Board 2-inch contrast band guidance)
  - Severe vision impairment: aim for ≥65% Michelson contrast at all critical junctions (stair nosings, platform edges, kerb lines)
  - Width of contrast strip: ≥50 mm (UK NIHR review); ≥51 mm (US Access Board) — narrower strips (<38 mm) fail to show gait benefit
  - Active research gap flag: no peer-reviewed study has established 30% as sufficient, and emerging evidence suggests it is substantially insufficient for the most severely impaired users. All C-items must carry: "Note: The 30% LRV minimum is a regulatory floor with unknown empirical basis. This guidebook recommends achieving the highest practicable contrast at all critical junctions."
**Standards confirmed (with ACTUAL contrast metric per jurisdiction):**
  - UK BS 8300: ≥30 LRV difference (simple subtraction)
  - AU AS 1428.1:2021: ≥30% Bowman-Sapolinski weighted ratio
  - DE DIN 32975/18040-2: ≥0.4 Michelson-type (L₂−L₁)/(L₂+L₁) — NOT LRV
  - NO TEK17/NS 11001: ≥0.4 general / ≥0.8 warning — Michelson-type — NOT LRV
  - SE Boverket BBR/BFS: ≥0.40 NCS lightness — NOT LRV
  - JP MLIT: qualitative "high luminance ratio" only; JCRI guidance 1.5–2.0 ratio — NO legislated threshold
  - FR NF P98-351: ≥70% luminance ratio (for tactile indicators) — NOT 30% LRV
  - ES CTE DB-SUA: >30 LRV for doors; ≥50% luminance for elevator buttons — mixed
  - IT DM 236/89: ≥40% luminance contrast — percentage-based
  - CN GB 55019-2021: qualitative "color or material difference" only — NO numerical threshold
  - KR Accessibility Act: qualitative "clearly contrasting" — NO numerical threshold
  - DK BR18: function-based, qualitative — NO numerical threshold
  - CA CAN/ASC-24: Michelson + Weber, task-dependent — NOT a single LRV number
  - US A117.1 (proposed): 70% + LRV≥45 — criticised as lacking evidence (PMC5433805)
  - FI Decree 241/2017: dimensional focus — NO contrast numbers
  - NL BBL: qualitative; NEN 9120 (2025, voluntary) via NEN-EN 17210

**Opus synthesis note:** The original Opus synthesis (2026-03-29) correctly identified that 30% LRV lacks empirical basis, but incorrectly attributed ≥30% LRV to six standards that actually use different formulas. Multilingual search (2026-05-10, 13 languages) revealed at least 6 mathematically incompatible contrast formula types across 16 jurisdictions. The core finding — that no empirical study validates any specific threshold — is strengthened, not weakened, by this correction. The practical guidance (≥50% LRV general, ≥65% Michelson severe VI) remains sound but must be framed as applying where LRV is the jurisdictional metric, not as a universal standard. **Divergent findings:** Multilingual search (2026-05-10) found fundamental divergence: jurisdictions use mathematically incompatible formulas (LRV subtraction, Michelson ratio, NCS lightness, simple luminance ratio, percentage-based, qualitative-only). Two identical surfaces can pass in one jurisdiction and fail in another. East Asian jurisdictions (JP, CN, KR) specify no numerical thresholds at all. The previous BPC text attributing "≥30% LRV" to DIN, TEK17, BFS, and MLIT was factually incorrect — each uses a different formula.

### Cross-jurisdictional contrast formula taxonomy (2026-05-10)
| Formula type | Jurisdictions | Metric | Threshold(s) |
|---|---|---|---|
| LRV difference | UK, AU, ES (doors) | Simple subtraction of LRV values | ≥30 points |
| Michelson-type | DE, NO, CA | (L₂−L₁)/(L₂+L₁) | ≥0.4 general; ≥0.8 warning (NO) |
| NCS lightness | SE | NCS lightness scale comparison | ≥0.40 (guidance) |
| Luminance ratio | JP (JCRI guidance) | L_high / L_low | 1.5–2.0 (guidance, not legislation) |
| Percentage-based | IT, FR (tactile) | Percentage luminance contrast | ≥40% (IT); ≥70% (FR tactile) |
| LRV + percentage (mixed) | ES, US (proposed) | Element-dependent | >30 LRV (ES doors); 70%+LRV≥45 (US) |
| Qualitative only | CN, KR, DK, FI, NL, JP (legislation) | "clearly contrasting" / "color difference" | None specified |

**Implication for guidebook:** The practical guidance cannot assume LRV as the universal metric. Recommendations should specify the formula type alongside the threshold, or use the universal recommendation of "highest achievable contrast" which is valid across all formula types.
**Notes:** Decision D-03 confirmed: all C-items must carry the regulatory-floor caveat. This is one of the most significant evidence-standards gaps in the entire guidebook. DO NOT cite 30% as an evidence-based optimum. Cite it as a regulatory minimum and recommend highest achievable contrast as design intent.
