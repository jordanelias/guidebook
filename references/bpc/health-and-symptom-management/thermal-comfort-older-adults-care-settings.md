## thermal-comfort-older-adults-care-settings

**Updated:** 2026-03-29 (Opus synthesis)  **Original search:** 2026-03-18 22:00  **Evidence tier:** 1–3  **Opus synthesis:** YES [OPUS-SYNTHESIS]
**Consensus finding:** Standard PMV/PPD thermal comfort model has limited suitability for older adults; age-differentiated targets are required. Neutral temperature for older adults in care settings is approximately 24.9°C (comfort zone 18.3–26.3°C). Unheated bathroom cold shock is the leading residential thermal hazard in cold climates, responsible for 6,073 bathtub drowning deaths in Japan (2023).
**Key evidence:**
  - Neutral temperature older adults (care homes, cold winter climate): 24.9°C; comfort zone 18.3–26.3°C (Baquero et al. 2023, Tier 3, n=1065 nursing home residents)
  - Standard PMV/PPD tools unreliable for cognitively impaired older adults — subjective feedback cannot be elicited reliably (van Hoof et al. 2010, Tier 3)
  - Japan heat shock: 6,073 bathtub drowning deaths (MHLW 2023) — root cause is 5–15°C differential between unheated bathrooms and heated living areas; inter-room differential ≤5–7°C recommended (Nakayama 1981 via Japan CAA — foundational study; consistently cited in Japanese policy for 40+ years despite age)
  - MS populations: 60–80% heat-sensitive; ambient temperature target ≤19–21°C with personalised HVAC control
  - OFS/MCAS/POTS populations: cool ambient environment (≤19–21°C) is primary built-environment requirement; zero built-environment standard addresses this
**Range:** Older adult care: 24.9°C neutral; 18.3–26.3°C comfort zone · MS/OFS: ≤19–21°C preferred · Inter-room differential: ≤5–7°C
**Jurisdictions confirmed:** Temperature targets absent from all building codes and standards across all 14 searched languages. H-04 (HVAC/Thermal) in all jurisdictions addresses acoustic NC levels, not temperature ranges for disability populations. Confirmed universal gap.
