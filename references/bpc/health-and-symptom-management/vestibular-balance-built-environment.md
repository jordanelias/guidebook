## Best-practice synthesis (provisional — pass 1)

Marker key: `○ [UNVERIFIED]` = direction from a source whose content is not yet read; `○ [CODE]` = code/standard-derived dimensional value (T4–T6, convergence-not-evidence, weak band). **No `●`/`◐` claims survive this pass** — nothing here rests on a verified evidence base yet.

**Design directions (evidence pending verification):**
1. **Support balance *recovery*, not just steadying.** Under balance loss people grasp available surfaces and apply large multidirectional forces; handrail height across the code range appears not to compromise reach-to-grasp timing (REF-00911, REF-00913 `○ [UNVERIFIED]`; REF-00915 compensatory stepping/reaching rationale `○ [UNVERIFIED]`). *Note: REF-00912 measured loading in **younger** adults — population-proxy only.*
2. **Injury-reducing (compliant) flooring in high-risk zones** (REF-00905 scoping review, T3, `○ [UNVERIFIED]`).
3. **Do not provoke visual vertigo (the A-STABLE core).** Vision-induced dizziness in "visually charged"/optokinetic/repeating-pattern environments; visual-motion perception is integral to balance (REF-00908, REF-00907 `○ [UNVERIFIED]`). → avoid large high-contrast repeating floor/wall patterns, specular/reflective floors, strong optic-flow surfaces in circulation.
4. **Environmental modification is first-line and OT-led** (REF-00792 Cochrane, REF-00902 USPSTF SR, REF-00904 SR of CPGs `○ [UNVERIFIED]`; REF-00903 is a Delphi *consensus* guideline → `○ [EXPERT-CONSENSUS]`, not an evidence base).
5. **Beware naive "sensory correction"** — a vision intervention *increased* falls in the Cochrane review; caution before "fixing vision" (REF-00792 `○ [UNVERIFIED]`).
6. **Perceived risk & fear-of-falling are environment-driven** — the built environment shapes fall risk perception and behaviour (REF-00916 qualitative `○ [UNVERIFIED]`); stairs are the critical hazard geometry (REF-00913, REF-00914, REF-00396 `○ [UNVERIFIED]`).

**Quantified specifications — code-derived (`○ [CODE]`, convergence-not-evidence; NOT full-strength):**
- **Handrails:** height **900–1000 mm**; circular cross-section **32–51 mm**; clearance **≥50 mm** from wall; continuous, both sides on stairs/ramps. `○ [CODE]` (UK Approved Doc K; IBC §1014; AS1428.1; cf. ISO 21542) — REF-00921. *Recovery-load rationale is T1-pending (§1); the prescription itself is code-convergence, not evidence.*
- **Stairs:** institutional max rise **150 mm** / min going **280 mm**; other buildings max rise **190 mm** / min going **250 mm**. `○ [CODE]` (UK Approved Doc K) — REF-00921.
- **Stair/transition illuminance:** **≥100 lux** general; **≥300 lux** for older adults; glare-free. `○ [CODE]` (IES) — REF-00923. *Clinical dose-response (falls ↓ per +100 lux) is T3-pending.*
- **Floor finish:** matte, low-reflectance **≤30 gloss units** (existing item **B-08**); plain / low-geometric-pattern (items **C-03/C-06**); LRV contrast **≥30** at critical junctions (item **C-04**). Value `○ [CODE]`; visual-vertigo mechanism (§3) verification-pending.

**Held for Phase-B verification (not asserted):** clinical effect sizes (Cochrane home-safety **RaR** vs **RR**; stair-descent COM-velocity increase; proportion of home stairs failing dimensional guidance) — all require full-text + page confirmation before assertion.

**Gaps registered:** the guidebook has **no handrail-dimension item** and **no stair going/riser item** — the code-derived specs above have no home item. See GAP entries (this session).
