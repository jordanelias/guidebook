## Best-practice synthesis (provisional — pass 1)

Marker key: `●` = content-verified confirmed evidence base (T1/T2); `○ [T3]` = content-verified but Tier-3 (review/scoping/consensus/qualitative — thin, provisional-only); `○ [CONTENT-PENDING]` = metadata-verified, full text not yet read; `○ [CODE]` = code/standard-derived dimensional value (T4–T6, convergence-not-evidence, weak band). Phase-B (2026-07-24) read the core anchors; effect sizes below are quoted from the source abstracts (PubMed).

**Design directions (Phase-B content-verified unless flagged):**
1. **Support balance *recovery*, not just steadying.** T1 biomechanics: handrail height did **not** compromise reach-to-grasp timing/speed during slope descent — and the authors flag center-of-mass control *after* grasping as the more decisive factor (REF-00911 `●`, 14 younger + 13 **older** adults — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apergo.2019.102873)). Under balance loss people use compensatory stepping and apply large multidirectional handrail forces; higher perturbation → higher COM velocity and rail force (REF-00913 `●`, 18 **younger** adults — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apergo.2020.103317)). Two-handed *facing* grasp yields the highest forces; forces scale with body weight (REF-00912 `●`, 50 **younger** adults — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apergo.2018.11.006)). *Population-proxy caveat: REF-00912/00913 are younger-adult samples; REF-00911 includes older adults. Design implication: stairways that enable compensatory stepping and handrails permitting multidirectional force — not a single "correct" rail height.* REF-00915 (compensatory stepping/reaching rationale) `○ [CONTENT-PENDING]`.
2. **Injury-reducing (compliant) flooring — with a real trade-off.** Scoping review: compliant flooring reduces fall-impact forces with **minimal effect on standing/walking balance** and *may* reduce injuries, **but may increase the risk of falls** (REF-00905 `○ [T3]` scoping review — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0171652)). The injury/fall trade-off must be weighed, not asserted as unqualified benefit. Narrative-review corroboration REF-00906 `○ [T3]` ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1093/ageing/afy059)).
3. **Do not provoke visual vertigo (the A-STABLE core).** The visual-vertigo / vision-induced-dizziness provocation in optokinetic / "visually charged" environments rests on **Bronstein 2016 alone** (REF-00908 `○ [T3]` — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/B978-0-444-63437-5.00004-2)): patients report dizziness on exposure to optokinetic stimuli / visually charged environments. Saftari & Kwon establishes only the *supporting* mechanism — visual-motion perception (vection, sensory reweighting) is integral to balance control — and does **not** address visual-vertigo/optokinetic provocation (REF-00907 `○ [T3]`, mechanistic support only — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1186/s40101-018-0170-1)). → avoid large high-contrast repeating floor/wall patterns, specular/reflective floors, strong optic-flow surfaces in circulation. *This is the anchor for the two VES `provisional` determination cells (B-08, C-06); single T3 review for the provocation claim → provisional, never stated.*
4. **Environmental modification is first-line and OT-led** — `●` verified. Cochrane SR (T2): home safety assessment & modification reduced **rate of falls 0.81 (95% CI 0.68–0.97; 6 trials, 4208 ppts)** and risk of falling **RR 0.88 (0.80–0.96)**, "more effective in people at higher risk of falling, including those with severe visual impairment," and "more effective when delivered by an occupational therapist" (REF-00792 `●` — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1002/14651858.CD007146.pub3)). USPSTF SR REF-00902 and SR-of-CPGs REF-00904 `○ [CONTENT-PENDING]`; World Falls guideline is a modified-**Delphi consensus** (verified) → REF-00903 `○ [T3 consensus]` ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1093/ageing/afac205)), not an evidence base.
5. **Beware naive "sensory correction"** — `●` verified. In the same Cochrane SR a vision-*treatment* intervention **significantly *increased* falls: rate of falls RaR 1.57 (95% CI 1.19–2.06), risk of falling RR 1.54 (1.24–1.91)**; and switching habitual multifocal wearers to single-lens glasses cut outside falls only in the active subgroup while *increasing* them in the inactive subgroup (REF-00792 `●`). "Fixing vision" is not automatically protective and can backfire.
6. **Perceived risk & fear-of-falling are environment-driven; stairs are the critical geometry.** Mixed-methods (n=22): despite all participants perceiving their stairs as safe, **40% of home staircases failed UK pitch/rise/going guidance** (REF-00396 `○ [T3]` — [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0326850)). Perceived-risk qualitative REF-00916 and stair-perturbation REF-00914 `○ [CONTENT-PENDING]`.

**Quantified specifications — code-derived (`○ [CODE]`, convergence-not-evidence; NOT full-strength):**
- **Handrails:** height **900–1000 mm**; circular cross-section **32–51 mm**; clearance **≥50 mm** from wall; continuous, both sides on stairs/ramps. `○ [CODE]` (UK Approved Doc K; IBC §1014; AS1428.1; cf. ISO 21542) — REF-00921. *Recovery-load rationale is T1-pending (§1); the prescription itself is code-convergence, not evidence.*
- **Stairs:** institutional max rise **150 mm** / min going **280 mm**; other buildings max rise **190 mm** / min going **250 mm**. `○ [CODE]` (UK Approved Doc K) — REF-00921.
- **Stair/transition illuminance:** **≥100 lux** general; **≥300 lux** for older adults; glare-free. `○ [CODE]` (IES) — REF-00923. *Clinical dose-response (falls ↓ per +100 lux) is T3-pending.*
- **Floor finish:** matte, low-reflectance **≤30 gloss units** (existing item **B-08**); plain / low-geometric-pattern (items **C-03/C-06**). Value `○ [CODE]`; **the visual-vertigo mechanism (§3) is now content-verified (T3)** and anchors VES `provisional` cells **B-08×VES** and **C-06×VES** (determination: direction provisional on T3 reviews, numeric threshold inherited/conventional). *LRV contrast **≥30** at junctions (item C-04) is a detection spec compatible with — but not asserted for — VES: localised junction contrast aids edge detection, whereas the vestibular harm is large repeating field patterns. No C-04×VES cell is asserted.*

**Phase-B outcome (2026-07-24):** the three previously-held clinical figures are now verified from source abstracts — Cochrane home-safety **0.81 (0.68–0.97)** rate of falls (the abstract prints "RR" but by the review's own methods this is the rate-of-falls / RaR estimate; risk-of-falling is the separate RR 0.88) and vision-intervention **RaR 1.57 (1.19–2.06)**; Gosine COM-velocity increases with perturbation magnitude (directional, no single effect size); Wharton **40%** of home stairs failing UK guidance (n=22). **Still content-pending (metadata-verified only):** REF-00902, 00904, 00810, 00910, 00914, 00915, 00916.

**Gaps registered:** the guidebook has **no handrail-dimension item** and **no stair going/riser item** — the code-derived handrail/stair specs above have no home item (GAP-300, GAP-301). Non-EN language strata remain NOT-RUN (pass-2).
