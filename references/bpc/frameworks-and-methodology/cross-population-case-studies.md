## best_practice_synthesis

*Opus judgment — 2026-03-27*

### Key Findings

**The built evidence base for cross-population design is thin but instructive.** ◐ Genuine multi-population built environment projects with rigorous outcome data remain rare. Most documented case studies serve a single primary population with secondary UD consideration. The field is dominated by professional accounts, design intent statements, and award recognition rather than peer-reviewed POE. Where rigorous outcome studies exist, they are typically single-population.

**The strongest outcome evidence is from single-population purpose-built environments, not cross-population ones.** ● Douglas et al. (2022, Disabil Rehabil, DOI 10.1080/09638288.2022.2060343) provide the most rigorous built-environment outcome data in the corpus: individualised apartments for people with neurological/CP disabilities (n=15) showed significant wellbeing improvement (p=0.031, Eta=0.29) and community integration gains (p=0.008, Eta=0.41), with an average reduction of 2.4 support hours per person per day. This is Tier 1 evidence. ○ De Hogeweyk (NL, 2009) demonstrates antipsychotic medication use reduced from ~50% to ~12% (Cambridge International Psychogeriatrics 2020) — strong functional outcome, though methodologically limited (no control arm, no standardised instruments confirmed in the reviewed literature).

**Enabling Village (Singapore, 2015) is the strongest cross-population case study with longitudinal operation data.** ○ 30,000m² retrofitted community hub for MOB, VIS, DEAF, NDV/AUT, and IntD populations; 10 years of operation; BCA UD Platinum Award 2016; President's Design Award 2016; ULI Global Award for Excellence 2022. Cross-disability design intent was explicit and has been continuously evaluated through programme delivery. No standardised clinical POE published; organisational outcome reports available.

**The Kelsey Civic Center (US, 2025) is the most ambitious contemporary cross-population housing project.** ○ 112 units, $88.3M, MOB/VIS/DEAF/NEU/NDV/OFS spectrum-wide disability-forward design. Co-designed with disabled community. Opened April 2025 — too recent for systematic POE. Produced Inclusive Design Standards (published) as a transferable secondary output. This is the most closely aligned case to the guidebook's Tier 1–2 design ambitions.

**Village Landais Alzheimer (France, 2020) has active longitudinal evaluation.** ◐ 120 DEM residents; Prof. Amieva, Bordeaux University. Early results: "improvement not only in the brain but also in behaviour" (ITV News 2023). Formal comparative study vs traditional nursing homes ongoing; €7M/year operating. Cost per resident comparable to traditional nursing homes. This is the closest equivalent to a controlled trial of dementia village design.

**Magda Mostafa's ASPECTSS POE (Egypt, 2018) provides the most rigorous single-population built environment POE methodology.** ● Archnet-IJAR 2018. NDV/AUT charter school, post-occupancy evaluation against ASPECTSS Design Index. Teacher surveys, in-class behavioural observation, parent/staff focus groups. Defines the methodological standard for population-specific built environment POE.

**NHS CAMHS inpatient sensory environments (UK, 2022) is the most significant failure case in the corpus.** ● National Development Team for Inclusion, commissioned by NHS England Children and Young People's Mental Health Taskforce. Autistic-led POE review. Finding: NHS inpatient mental health environments systematically worsen outcomes for autistic young people — sensory environments "present sometimes extreme challenges." Relevant cross-population failure: NDV/AUT + NDV/MH co-occurring in the same inpatient setting, yet environments designed without autism sensory awareness. This is the paradigm cross-population design failure case.

### Cross-Population Tensions Documented in Built Evidence

1. **Tactile walking surface indicators (VIS) vs mobility device users (MOB/PAIN)** ● Tenji blocks documented as causing fatigue and instability for MobAD users (Kapsalis et al. 2024, systematic review). Classic cross-population conflict. Partial resolution: alternative crossing design enabling both populations. [§8.4 candidate]

2. **DeafSpace wider sightlines (DEAF) vs acoustic management (NDV/AUT/PAIN)** ○ DeafSpace principles require open space and visual connectivity; autism and noise-sensitivity populations benefit from acoustic separation and smaller enclosed spaces. Tension unresolved in existing literature; no built example of explicit resolution documented.

3. **DeafSpace rounded corner failure (DEAF vs MOB)** ● Gallaudet SLCC self-report: rounded hallway corners intended to alert deaf signers of approaching persons caused increased collisions. Corrected post-occupancy to glass corners. Documents that a DeafSpace provision designed for DEAF created a MOB safety hazard — and was fixed.

4. **Thermal environment (NEU/MS Uhthoff vs PAIN warmth need)** ● Maggie's meta-synthesis 2022: cancer patients (PAIN/OFS proxy) "very sensitive to cold due to chemotherapy side-effects — thermal comfort more important than standard design principles." Conflicts with Uhthoff threshold (≤18°C for MS/NEU). This is CON-0003 in the connection register; documented in the existing BPC.

5. **NHS inpatient sensory environments (NDV/MH vs NDV/AUT)** ● NDTi 2022: NHS inpatient mental health wards designed for NDV/MH population are systemically harmful for co-occurring NDV/AUT population. Loud communal areas, unpredictable sensory stimuli, institutional lighting — all harm autistic young people admitted for mental health reasons. [§8.5 unresolvable conflict candidate unless explicit sensory zoning is provided]

### Evidence Gaps

1. **No rigorously evaluated cross-population built environment project with standardised clinical outcome instruments.** The field lacks a controlled study where two or more disability populations are explicitly served, and outcomes measured separately for each population pre/post.

2. **Dementia village evaluation is underway but not yet definitive.** Village Landais longitudinal study ongoing. De Hogeweyk medication data is the best available but does not meet RCT standards.

3. **Cross-population conflict resolution evidence is predominantly inferential.** Tenji blocks, thermal conflict, acoustic/sensory zoning conflicts — all documented in the literature as mechanisms, but no built examples of explicit multi-population conflict resolution with measured outcomes for both populations.

4. **Non-English literature for cross-population design is thin.** German, French, Scandinavian sources found are design intent documentation rather than outcome studies.

5. **Failure case evidence is largely grey literature or advocacy report.** NHS CAMHS finding is the strongest (commissioned, autistic-led) but remains a practice-based report rather than peer-reviewed research.

### Guidance for Part 13 / Part 8

**Part 13 (Case Studies and Built Evidence):**
- Lead with outcome data where available: Douglas et al. (2022) and De Hogeweyk medication reduction are the two strongest quantified outcomes in the corpus
- Kelsey Civic Center: strongest contemporary cross-population design intent; insufficient POE data for quantified outcome claims — enter as design-intent case with "Evidence Contribution: design standards publication"
- Enabling Village: strongest 10-year cross-disability operational case; present as programme-level evidence rather than building-level POE
- Village Landais: cite as active longitudinal evaluation; do not claim confirmed outcomes; include early results with explicit PROVISIONAL flag
- NHS CAMHS failure case: must be included — this is the paradigm case for Part 8 §8.5 (unresolvable conflict without sensory zoning)
- Identify and disclose thin evidence base explicitly: Part 13 methodology note should acknowledge that no existing case study provides rigorous cross-population POE data

**Part 8 §8.4 (Cross-Population Resolutions):**
- Tenji blocks: document as VIS/MOB conflict with partial design resolution (alternative crossing geometry) — Kapsalis 2024
- DeafSpace rounded corners: document as DEAF/MOB design correction — Gallaudet SLCC
- Thermal conflict: already in §8.4.x — cross-reference Maggie's meta-synthesis for PAIN/OFS thermal sensitivity evidence

**Part 8 §8.5 (Unresolvable Conflicts):**
- NHS CAMHS inpatient: NDV/AUT + NDV/MH — sensory environment for NDV/MH (calming but stimulus-present ward design) systematically conflicts with NDV/AUT sensory needs
- DeafSpace glazed junctions (CON-0020): already in register
