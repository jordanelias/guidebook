## european-accessibility-act-scope-clarification

**Updated:** 2026-03-30 (v4 schema synthesis)  **Original search:** 2026-03-18 23:30  **Evidence tier:** 5 (regulatory)  **Opus synthesis:** YES [OPUS-SYNTHESIS]
**Consensus finding:** The European Accessibility Act (Directive EU 2019/882) applies to digital products and services ONLY. It does not mandate physical built environment accessibility requirements in any EU member state. Any reference to EAA in the context of physical building standards is a misattribution and must be corrected.
**Key facts:**
  - EAA transposition deadline: 28 June 2022
  - EAA enforcement began: 28 June 2025
  - Transitional period: ends 28 June 2030
  - Self-service terminal exception: up to 2045
**EAA scope (covers):** Electronic communications, AV media, passenger transport info (digital), banking (digital), e-commerce, self-service terminals, consumer electronics, e-books
**EAA scope (does NOT cover):** Physical built environment, building codes, construction standards
**Built environment governance in EU:** National building codes (DIN 18040, NEN 1814, NS 11001, BFS 2024:12, etc.); EN 17210:2021 (functional requirements, not uniformly mandatory); CRPD obligations
**Guidebook corrective action:** If EAA cited in context of physical provisions: add correction note. EAA IS relevant to H-series items (touchscreens, self-service terminals, BLE beacons, building management interfaces).
**Netherlands Bbl 2024 update:** In force 1 January 2024, replacing Bouwbesluit 2012.
**Spain DALCO UNE 41500:2020 update:** DALCO series is the standards framework for specifying accessibility by functional need.
**New Zealand NZS 4121:2001 update:** Under revision by Whaikaha; updated standard expected ~mid-2026. ANZA 2023 provides reform framework.

### Best-practice synthesis
**Most inclusive provision:** EN 17210:2021 as functional requirements standard for accessible built environment across EU (companion CEN/TR 17621 + CEN/TR 17622).
**Most targeted provision:** EAA relevant to H-series items (self-service terminals, touchscreens, BLE beacons) — these fall within EAA digital product scope and must comply from June 2025.
**Conflict resolution:** Not applicable — scope clarification, not design conflict.
**Highest-ambition actionable specification:** Correct all EAA misattributions in guidebook. Apply EAA to H-series items. Apply national building codes + CRPD for physical built environment.
**Opus synthesis note:** Regulatory scope clarification. Primary value is preventing misattribution of EAA to physical built environment provisions.
