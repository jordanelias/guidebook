## Scope
Purpose-built accessible homes, home modification programmes with documented disability outcomes, OT-assessed residential adaptation studies, wheelchair standard housing, specialist disability accommodation (SDA), adaptable housing programmes. Residential settings only.

Excluded: Institutional settings, non-residential buildings, general housing standards without disability outcome data.

## Evidence by Jurisdiction

### UK [Co-1 / Tier 2 / Co-2 — STRONG]

**Habinteg Housing Association — SCIE Case Study (Leeds, ongoing)**
Source: SCIE (2023). Habinteg Housing Association case study. scie.org.uk. [Co-1 / Tier 2]
Populations: MOB / ALL
Design intent: Wheelchair-accessible bungalow with adjustable counters, wider entrances, wet room, single level. Part of mainstream housing scheme (integrated community).
Outcomes documented: Independence in ADL (cooking, washing, external access); reduced anxiety; improved social participation; ability to spend time with family. Qualitative tenant testimony, supported by Habinteg annual reports.
Cost: DFG-delivered or Habinteg social housing programme (cost not isolated in source).
Evidence quality: Co-1 lived experience + Co-2 OT-authored housing guidance. Evidence marker: ●○ (Co-1 confirmed; single case qualitative)

**Habinteg / Hounslow — Upper Butts, Brentside (2024–25)**
Source: New London Architecture (2025). Creating Inclusive, Low-Energy Public Housing in Hounslow. nla.london. [Tier 2 grey / programme]
Populations: MOB / ALL
Design: 3 × M4(3) wheelchair-accessible bungalows + 3 × M4(2) accessible and adaptable homes. Small-site infill. CAE access consultancy. Designed by Levitt Bernstein.
Outcomes: Described as "intersectional EDI priorities"; social value framing. No quantified clinical outcomes. Part of 13-scheme Habinteg London programme.
Evidence quality: Tier 2 programme evidence. Evidence marker: ○ (expert/programme)

**Habinteg Forecast 2025**
Source: Habinteg (2025). A forecast for accessible homes 2025: one decade on, milestone or millstone? housinglin.org.uk. [Tier 2]
Key data:
- Only 12.6% of England's housing stock has basic access features (English Housing Survey 2025, Centre for Ageing Better)
- M4(3) open market: 3.3% of new homes planned; M4(2): 69% of draft plans
- Regional disparity: 1 wheelchair user home per 210 people (London) vs 1 per 2,006 (North West)
- 41% of new homes planned will not be M4(2) or M4(3) standard
Evidence quality: Tier 2 national advocacy data. Evidence marker: ●

**Habinteg cost data (2024 Accessible Homes Week)**
Source: Habinteg (2024). #ForAccessibleHomes campaign. habinteg.org.uk. [Tier 2 / VERIFIED]
Key data: M4(1)→M4(2) adaptation cost ~£27,000 per household (grab rail + stairlift + wet room). DFG allocation 2024/25: £625M (185% increase on 10 years prior).
Evidence quality: Tier 2 advocacy with verified cost modelling. Evidence marker: ● [VERIFIED]

**Habinteg / RIBA — Inclusive Housing Design Guide (2024)**
Source: Runnalls J., Walker M. (2024). Inclusive Housing Design Guide. Habinteg / CAE / RIBA. [Co-2 / Tier 2]
Authors: Specialist housing OTs (Runnalls, Walker). Peer reviewed. Covers M4(2) and M4(3) standards with OT rationale, case studies, scale drawings.
Populations: MOB / ALL
Evidence quality: Co-2 OT CPG. Evidence marker: ● [Co-2]

### Australia [Co-1 / Tier 1–2 — STRONG]

**Summer Foundation — SDA Tenant Outcomes Programme (ongoing)**
Source: Douglas, Winkler, D'Cruz et al. (2024). BMJ Open 14:e082802. DOI: 10.1136/bmjopen-2023-082802. [Tier 1 qualitative / Co-1]
Populations: NEU (ABI/neurological) / MOB / ALL complex disability
Design intent: Individually funded SDA apartments (NDIS); purpose-built to high-care specification. Replacing group homes and residential aged care.
Outcomes: Being a pioneer — qualitative study of moving into SDA from perspective of adults with neurological disability. Adjustment challenges documented; transition support needs identified. >2,000 SDA apartments enrolled nationally.
Key finding: "Quality of support provided in the home is a greater determinant of tenant quality of life than the built environment." — direct OT evidence for Part 5 Tier 1 primacy of support over specification.
Evidence quality: Tier 1 qualitative (BMJ Open peer-reviewed). Evidence marker: ●

**Summer Foundation / Winkler et al. (2024) — NCC Implementation**
Source: Winkler D., Liddicoat S., D'Cruz K., Wellecke C., Mulherin P., Douglas J. (2024). Supporting the design and construction sector to transition to minimum accessible standards: A qualitative study. Summer Foundation. [Tier 2]
Populations: ALL (universal design / residential sector)
Key data: Design/construction sector barriers to implementing NCC 2022 LHA Silver standard identified. Qualitative methods.
Evidence quality: Tier 2 grey. Evidence marker: ○

**Summer Foundation — Budgetary Impact (2022)**
Source: Winkler D., Rathbone A. (2022). Budgetary impact of timely SDA payment approvals. Summer Foundation. [Tier 2 / economic]
Key data: Projected government budget cost of timely SDA approval: +$338M in 2026, -$121M savings by 2031. Only 39% of those eligible for SDA are living in it.
Evidence quality: Tier 2 economic modelling (grey). Evidence marker: ○ [PROVISIONAL]

### Germany / Austria [Tier 6 / statistical — THIN]

**Statistisches Bundesamt — Mikrozensus 2018 / 2022**
Source: Statistisches Bundesamt (2019/2024). Mikrozensus-Zusatzprogramm Wohnen. destatis.de. [Tier 6 statistical]
Key data: Only 2% of German housing stock fully barrier-free (2018). 80% of new builds 2011–2015 not fully accessible. 85% of senior households have no step-free access. 2022 update confirms minimal improvement.
