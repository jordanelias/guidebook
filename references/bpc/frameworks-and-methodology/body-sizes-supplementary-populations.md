## body-sizes-supplementary-populations

**Updated:** 2026-03-29 (Opus synthesis)  **Original search:** 2026-03-18 23:59  **Evidence tier:** 3–5 (anthropometric data, standards, limited OT evidence)  **Opus synthesis:** YES [OPUS-SYNTHESIS]
**Consensus finding:** All four supplementary populations (children, short stature/dwarfism, extreme height, large body size) are entirely absent from all reviewed international accessibility standards (ADA, BS 8300, ISO 21542, DIN 18040, NS 11001, BCA 2025, CSA B651). Design provisions must be derived from anthropometric databases and expert consensus in the absence of dedicated standards.
**CHD — Children (age-staged):**
  - All mainstream guidebook items are specified against adult anthropometric norms — systematic design failures wherever children are primary users
  - CHD sub-code framework: CHD/INF (0–2 yrs, ~75–87 cm); CHD/PRE (3–5 yrs, ~96–110 cm); CHD/ESA (6–8 yrs, ~116–128 cm); CHD/MCA (9–12 yrs, ~133–150 cm); CHD/ADO (13–17 yrs)
  - Sources: WHO Child Growth Standards; CDC/NCHS NHANES 2015–2018 (McDowell et al. 2021)
  - Key item: child-height handrail secondary rail required (500–600 mm); standard 865–965 mm rail unreachable for CHD/INF and CHD/PRE
  - Five primary building typologies: paediatric healthcare, primary/secondary school, childcare, indoor recreational facility, children's library
  - Zero dedicated children's accessibility standards identified in any jurisdiction
**LPA — Short stature/dwarfism:**
  - Most common condition: achondroplasia — adult standing height 122–134 cm (female 122 cm median, male 130 cm median) (Hoover-Fong et al. 2021, CLARITY study, n=1,374, Tier 3)
  - 5th percentile female: ~110 cm — this is the LPA design baseline for reach and height specifications
  - Arm span ~110–122 cm; max comfortable forward reach ~700–850 mm
  - Entirely absent from all international standards including ADA, BS 8300, ISO 21542, DIN 18040
  - Critical conflicts: standard accessible WC (designed for wheelchair user) is inaccessible to ambulant LPA user — grab bars at wrong height, raised seats compound difficulty
  - Reception counters: LPA users may be invisible below standard counter height — complete communication barrier
  - Controls, ATMs, service points systematically above comfortable reach
  - Sources: Hoover-Fong2021-CLARITY · Tofts2017 · Merker2018 · LPA-UK-RGA · AUCD-Language2024
**EXH — Extreme height (≥200 cm or acromegaly/gigantism):**
  - All standard door heights (2000–2100 mm) create hazard — minimum 2400 mm clear height required
  - Standard accessible WC grab bars at 840–915 mm are too low for seated-height clearance
  - No international standard addresses EXH provisions — entirely first-principles derivation
  - Primary failure points: door clearance, ceiling height, seated-counter eye level, reach zones inverted
**BAR — Large body size (not a disability; design quality provision):**
  - Large body size is NOT classified as a disability under ADA, Equality Act 2010, CRPD, or any reviewed jurisdiction
  - Key specifications: door ≥950–1000 mm clear; corridor ≥1500–1800 mm in BAR areas; turning circle ≥1800 mm (new build: ≥1900 mm, per Steinfeld 2006); seating ≥250–300 kg rated; WC 1800×2200 mm; structural floor review for 300 kg point loading (SE sign-off required); grab bar noggins in all bathroom walls
  - Dignity principle: no segregated bariatric-only areas — integrated provision required
  - Seating: ≥10% armrest-free in public buildings; ≥300 kg rated clinical; ≥250 kg rated public
  - Healthcare: 15–20% higher air exchange rate recommended; overhead lift track structural provision
  - Residential adaptable standards (Lifetime Homes, CSA B652, NS 11001-2) do not address BAR — gap confirmed across all jurisdictions
**Jurisdictions confirmed:** No jurisdiction has dedicated standards for CHD, LPA, EXH, or BAR built environments. BAR provisions in healthcare settings: US FGI Guidelines, VA BFDS 2025, NHS HBN 00-03 are the strongest available sources.
**Key sources:** Hoover-Fong2021-CLARITY · WHO-Growth-Standards · McDowell2021-NHANES · Steinfeld2006-RESNA · VA-BFDS-2025 · NHS-HBN-00-03 · FGI-Guidelines
