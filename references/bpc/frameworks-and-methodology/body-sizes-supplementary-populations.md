## body-sizes-supplementary-populations

**Updated:** 2026-03-30 (v4 schema synthesis)  **Original search:** 2026-03-18 23:59  **Evidence tier:** 3–5 (anthropometric data, standards, limited OT evidence)  **Opus synthesis:** YES [OPUS-SYNTHESIS]
**Consensus finding:** All four supplementary populations (children, short stature/dwarfism, extreme height, large body size) are entirely absent from all reviewed international accessibility standards (ADA, BS 8300, ISO 21542, DIN 18040, NS 11001, BCA 2025, CSA B651). Design provisions must be derived from anthropometric databases and expert consensus in the absence of dedicated standards.
**CHD — Children (age-staged):**
  - CHD sub-code framework: CHD/INF (0–2 yrs, ~75–87 cm); CHD/PRE (3–5 yrs, ~96–110 cm); CHD/ESA (6–8 yrs, ~116–128 cm); CHD/MCA (9–12 yrs, ~133–150 cm); CHD/ADO (13–17 yrs)
  - Sources: WHO Child Growth Standards; CDC/NCHS NHANES 2015–2018 (McDowell et al. 2021)
  - Key item: child-height handrail secondary rail required (500–600 mm)
  - Five primary building typologies: paediatric healthcare, primary/secondary school, childcare, indoor recreational facility, children's library
  - Zero dedicated children's accessibility standards identified in any jurisdiction
**LPA — Short stature/dwarfism:**
  - Most common condition: achondroplasia — adult standing height 122–134 cm (Hoover-Fong et al. 2021, CLARITY study, n=1,374, Tier 3)
  - 5th percentile female: ~110 cm — LPA design baseline
  - Arm span ~110–122 cm; max comfortable forward reach ~700–850 mm
  - Critical conflicts: standard accessible WC (designed for wheelchair user) is inaccessible to ambulant LPA user
**EXH — Extreme height (≥200 cm or acromegaly/gigantism):**
  - All standard door heights (2000–2100 mm) create hazard — minimum 2400 mm clear height required
  - No international standard addresses EXH provisions
**BAR — Large body size (not a disability; design quality provision):**
  - NOT classified as a disability under ADA, Equality Act 2010, CRPD, or any reviewed jurisdiction
  - Key specifications: door ≥950–1000 mm clear; corridor ≥1500–1800 mm; turning circle ≥1800 mm; seating ≥250–300 kg rated; WC 1800×2200 mm
  - BAR relocated out of main taxonomy per project-standards — Supplementary Volume only
**Jurisdictions confirmed:** No jurisdiction has dedicated standards for CHD, LPA, EXH, or BAR built environments.

### Best-practice synthesis
**Most inclusive provision:** Age-staged CHD sub-code framework with five building typologies; LPA design baseline at 5th percentile female (~110 cm); EXH minimum 2400 mm clear door height.
**Most targeted provision:** LPA: standard accessible WC is inaccessible to ambulant LPA user — separate specification required. BAR: ≥300 kg structural floor review with SE sign-off; dignity principle (no segregated bariatric-only areas).
**Conflict resolution:** LPA and standard wheelchair accessibility conflict at WC grab bar heights and seat heights — dual-height or adjustable provisions required.
**Highest-ambition actionable specification:** CHD secondary handrail 500–600 mm; LPA forward reach ≤850 mm; EXH door clearance ≥2400 mm; BAR turning circle ≥1800–1900 mm with ≥300 kg rated seating and structural floor provision.
**Opus synthesis note:** All specifications are first-principles derivations from anthropometric data. No dedicated standards exist in any jurisdiction. THIN-BASE disclosure required on all items.
