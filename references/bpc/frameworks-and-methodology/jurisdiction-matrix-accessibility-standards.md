## jurisdiction-matrix-accessibility-standards

**Updated:** 2026-03-30 (v4 schema synthesis)  **Original search:** 2026-03-18 22:00  **Evidence tier:** 4–5 (standards and regulatory documents)  **Opus synthesis:** YES [OPUS-SYNTHESIS]
**Consensus finding:** Mandatory residential accessibility requirements exist in Australia (NCC 2022, Class 1a+2), India (MoHUA 2021/RPwD 2023), Norway (TEK17), and Sweden (BBR). UK, Canada, and USA rely on voluntary/sector-adopted standards. Major gap: individual residential unit accessibility is not mandated in Brazil (NBR 9050 covers common areas only).
**Key facts by jurisdiction:**
  - ISO 21542:2021 (Ed. 2): wheelchair dims 800×1300 mm; 1524 mm turning — gap with ANSI A117.1-2017 (1702 mm for powered chairs)
  - EN 17210:2021: EAA optional for EU member states; patchwork adoption
  - Australia NCC 2022: mandatory Class 1a+2; Livable Housing Design requirements
  - Brazil NBR 9050:2020+Errata: mandatory for common areas; individual units NOT mandated
  - Canada CSA B651/B652:2023: voluntary federal; updated anthropometrics
  - Germany DIN 18040-1/2:2023 drafts: revision impasse (570+380 objections); new editions expected summer/autumn 2025
  - India MoHUA 2021 + RPwD Amendment 2023: mandatory all public buildings
  - Japan Barrier-Free Act 2006/2020: scale-based (≥2,000 m²)
  - Norway TEK17/NS 11001-1:2018: mandatory; step-free ≤1:15; 1800 mm width
  - Singapore BCA Code 2025: supersedes 2019; anthropometric data section added
  - Sweden BBR July 2025: new regulations in force; EN 81-70 for almost every lift
  - Switzerland SIA 500/BehiG: mandatory; 5%/20% cost proportionality rule
  - UK BS 8300-1/2:2018: best practice only; Changing Places mandatory since 2021
  - USA ANSI A117.1-2017: 1702 mm turning for powered chairs; voluntary but widely adopted
**Not yet searched:** Belgium · Israel · Netherlands · New Zealand · Portugal · South Korea · Spain · UAE
**Divergent findings:**
  - Turning radius: ISO 1524 mm vs. ANSI 1702 mm vs. CSA 2100 mm (U-turn)
  - Ramp gradient: ADA 8.3% vs. DIN/TEK17 6–6.7% vs. France 5% preferred
  - Mandatory residential: AU/NO/IN/SE mandatory vs. UK/US/CA voluntary

**UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD)**
- Adopted: 13 December 2006 (UNGA A/RES/61/106)
- Entered into force: 3 May 2008
- States parties: 191 (as of 2024)
- Optional Protocol: 104 states parties
- Monitoring body: Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD Committee)

**Key articles for accessible built environment:**

| Article | Title | Core obligation |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 9 | Accessibility | States parties shall take measures to ensure access to the physical environment, transportation, information/communications, and facilities/services open to the public. |
| Art. 19 | Living independently | Right to live in the community; adequate support services; access to mainstream community services on equal basis. |
| Art. 20 | Personal mobility | Facilitate personal mobility with maximum independence; access to quality mobility aids at affordable cost. |
| Art. 24 | Education | Inclusive education requires accessible school buildings; reasonable accommodation for individual needs. |
| Art. 27 | Work and employment | Accessible workplaces; reasonable accommodation obligation. |

**CRPD General Comment No. 2 (2014) on Article 9 — Accessibility:**
- Accessibility as precondition for other CRPD rights; universal design as the standard; reasonable accommodation as individual obligation (≠ accessibility which is systemic)

**CRPD General Comment No. 5 (2017) on Article 19 — Living independently:**
- Accessible housing as a right; deinstitutionalisation requires accessible community infrastructure

### Best-practice synthesis
**Most inclusive provision:** Australia NCC 2022 mandatory Livable Housing Design requirements for all Class 1a+2 dwellings — most comprehensive mandatory residential accessibility.
**Most targeted provision:** ANSI A117.1-2017 1702 mm turning for powered chairs — most generous turning radius in any standard. CSA B651:23 2100 mm for U-turn.
**Conflict resolution:** Turning radius discrepancy (ISO 1524 mm vs. ANSI 1702 mm vs. CSA 2100 mm) — guidebook should recommend 1702 mm for new build as minimum, note CSA 2100 mm as highest ambition.
**Highest-ambition actionable specification:** Mandatory residential accessibility (AU/NO/IN/SE model); 1702 mm turning (ANSI); 1:20 ramp gradient (evidence-aligned); zero-threshold entry.
**Opus synthesis note:** Matrix is reference data. 8 jurisdictions not yet searched. DIN 18040 revision and BBR July 2025 are pending updates.
