## jurisdiction-matrix-accessibility-standards

**Updated:** 2026-03-18 22:00  **Evidence tier:** 4–5 (standards and regulatory documents)
**Consensus finding:** Mandatory residential accessibility requirements exist in Australia (NCC 2022, Class 1a+2), India (MoHUA 2021/RPwD 2023), Norway (TEK17), and Sweden (BBR). UK, Canada, and USA rely on voluntary/sector-adopted standards. Major gap: individual residential unit accessibility is not mandated in Brazil (NBR 9050 covers common areas only).
**Key facts by jurisdiction:**
  - ISO 21542:2021 (Ed. 2): current international standard; wheelchair dims 800×1300 mm; 1524 mm turning — gap with ANSI A117.1-2017 (1702 mm for powered chairs)
  - EN 17210:2021: EAA optional for EU member states; Ireland and others did not adopt built environment provisions — patchwork adoption; revised harmonised standards due September 2025
  - Australia NCC 2022: mandatory Class 1a+2; Livable Housing Design requirements; most states effective 2023–24
  - Brazil NBR 9050:2020+Errata: mandatory for common areas; individual units NOT mandated — confirmed policy gap
  - Canada CSA B651/B652:2023: voluntary federal; updated anthropometrics; luminance contrast guidance
  - Germany DIN 18040-1/2:2023 drafts: revision impasse (570+380 objections); new editions expected summer/autumn 2025
  - India MoHUA 2021 + RPwD Amendment 2023: mandatory all public buildings
  - Japan Barrier-Free Act 2006/2020: scale-based (≥2,000 m²); 2020 revision added soft measures
  - Norway TEK17/NS 11001-1:2018: mandatory (TEK17); step-free ≤1:15; 1800 mm width
  - Singapore BCA Code 2025: supersedes 2019; anthropometric data section added; elder-friendly enhanced
  - Sweden BBR July 2025: new regulations in force; EN 81-70 for almost every lift
  - Switzerland SIA 500/BehiG: mandatory (BehiG); 5%/20% cost proportionality rule
  - UK BS 8300-1/2:2018: best practice only (not statutory); Changing Places mandatory since 2021
  - USA ANSI A117.1-2017: 1702 mm turning for powered chairs; voluntary but widely adopted
