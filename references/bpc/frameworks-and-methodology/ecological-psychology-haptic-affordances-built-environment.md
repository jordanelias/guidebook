**Consensus finding:** Affordances are organism-specific action possibilities — the same environment affords different actions to different users. Accessibility specifications (900 mm corridor, 175 mm riser, 1200 mm counter) are minimum functional thresholds at which affordances switch from available to unavailable. Handrails and grab bars are haptic navigation structures, not safety add-ons — interruption at any point destroys the haptic route.
**Key frameworks:**
  - Gibson (1979): affordances as action possibilities specific to the organism
  - Heft (2001): affordances in nested hierarchies (room → building → site)
  - Reed & Jones (1982): affordances are individual-specific; accessibility standards are minimum thresholds
  - Turvey & Carello (1995): haptic affordances — handrails as continuous haptic pathways
  - Barker (1968): behaviour settings prescribe and regularize behaviour; accessible design ensures full participation
**Design implications:**
  - Specifications are functional thresholds, not optimal values
  - Misaffordances (signalling non-existent action possibilities) are actively harmful
  - Haptic pathway continuity: handrail interruptions break the tactile route
  - Behaviour setting audit: confirm prescribed behaviour is achievable for all anticipated populations

### Best-practice synthesis
**Most inclusive provision:** Design above minimum functional thresholds where foreseeable populations require it; specify handrails as continuous haptic pathways (no interruptions at doorways, corners, or landings).
**Most targeted provision:** Behaviour setting audit for each room: confirm that prescribed behaviour is achievable by the full range of disability groups the room will serve. Identify and eliminate misaffordances (e.g. high-gloss floor appearing wet to DEM user).
**Conflict resolution:** Not applicable — theoretical framework, not design specification.
**Highest-ambition actionable specification:** Continuous handrail specification (no interruptions); behaviour setting audit at design review; misaffordance checklist.
