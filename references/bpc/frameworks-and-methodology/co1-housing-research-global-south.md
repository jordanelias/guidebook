## Cross-Slug Feeds (Session 1 — provisional)

### → thermoregulation-built-environment
Global South housing for disabled people is predominantly informal/slum typology (Ethiopia, India confirmed). These typologies have no passive cooling, no mechanical ventilation, no thermal mass design. SCI/MS thermoregulatory risk in these environments is extreme but completely absent from the existing evidence base.

### → sensory-space-global-south  
Communal living in informal settlements (shared rooms, shared facilities, household overcrowding) makes private sensory withdrawal impossible for NDV populations. The PAS 6463 individual retreat model assumes private residential space — which most poor disabled Global South residents do not have.

### → wayfinding-global-south
India slum (E10): no ramps, no tactile, non-functional lifts in redevelopment buildings. Informal settlement pathways are unpaved, irregular, shared with vehicles — natural wayfinding cues absent or unreliable. TWSI is irrelevant in these contexts without first addressing the basic pathway surface.

### → bathroom-typology-global-south
India slum (E10): community toilets in slums have no disability provisions. Overcrowding means disabled people wait at end of queue. This Co-1 evidence confirms the shared sanitation gap identified from the research literature in Phase 1-D.

## Primary Cross-Slug Finding for Opus

The formal/informal city divide is the meta-barrier that existing guidebook BPC entries do not address. All existing BPC entries specify minimum standards for formal, compliant construction. In the Global South, the majority of disabled people (who are also disproportionately poor) live in informal housing where these standards do not apply, are not enforced, and the construction typology cannot accommodate them without fundamental redesign. 

The guidebook must determine whether it addresses: (a) formal accessible housing in new construction (current scope — unchanged); (b) informal settlement retrofitting (would require a new chapter/section); or (c) both with explicit scope differentiation.
