## multilingual-evidence-convergence-non-english

**Updated:** 2026-03-19 01:00  **Evidence tier:** 3–5 (systematic review; non-English sources)
**Consensus finding:** Seven provisions have stronger empirical evidence in non-English language literature than in English-language sources. Five provisions have cross-linguistic consensus (≥5 language bodies). Three provisions lack any non-English evidence base and rest entirely on English-language sources.
**Stronger in non-English literature:**
  | Provision | Strongest evidence language | Key finding |
  |---|---|---|
  | Toilet visibility D-03 | German (Marquardt 2011, TU Dresden) | 47% incontinence reduction in 32 dementia care facilities — single strongest empirical finding for any spatial design provision |
  | Heat shock thermal provision | Japanese (AIJ epidemiological studies) | ~17,000 cardiovascular deaths/year from bathroom temperature differential >10°C; largest epidemiological dataset for any provision in the guidebook |
  | Dementia village normalised environment D-09 | Dutch (BuroKade/Vivium De Hogeweyk POE) | 94% wayfinding success vs 34% conventional; lower staffing ratio with higher quality-of-life |
  | Bathroom fall threshold risk I-04 | Japanese (Journal of Environmental Engineering, AIJ) | Threshold ≥15 mm statistically significant fall risk increase |
  | UD cost premium by building type | Belgian/Dutch (Ielegems & Vanrie full dataset) | Residential 0.94%, educational 1.67%, office 2.31%, healthcare 3.92%; cost premium decreases to near-zero after 3 accessible projects |
  | Ramp gradient E-03 | Swedish (Boverket/SINTEF Scandinavian Journal OT) | 1:20 is maximum functional gradient for self-propelling manual wheelchair users; largest OT propulsion sample |
  | Loop garden with seating D-11 | Danish (Nationalt Videnscenter for Demens) | Loop path gardens with seating every 20 m produce measurably lower agitation than non-loop configurations |
**Cross-linguistic consensus (≥5 language bodies):**
  - LRV contrast ≥30 at critical junctions: UK, German, French, Swedish, Norwegian, Japanese
  - Loop floor plan for dementia (D-01): German, Danish, Dutch, French, English
  - Zero-threshold wet room (I-04): German, Japanese, Swedish, Norwegian, British
  - Grab bar structural blocking (I-03): German, Japanese, British, North American, Australian
  - Hearing loop at counters/assembly (A-10/A-11): British, German, French, Swedish, Norwegian
  - Rest seating ≤20–50 m (E-10): British, Japanese, Swedish, Norwegian
**No non-English evidence found:**
  - DeafBlind provisions (DBL): clinical OT research primarily English-language
  - ASPECTSS autism design (A-16): Mostafa's model English-language; limited non-English parallel
  - Auracast/assistive technology (A-12): documented in English-language technical standards only
**Additional non-English findings:**
  - German: DIN 18040-2 ≤10 mm threshold variant at shower/balcony doors where zero-threshold drainage-constrained; 1200 mm (not 1100 mm) upper reach zone for self-propelling wheelchair users
  - German: TERRAGON/DStGB (2017) — 130/140 DIN 18040-2 criteria add zero cost at design stage; €21.50/m² for full compliance
  - German: KfW/Prognos — average €19,100/dwelling retrofit; 700,000+ dwellings (largest real-world retrofit dataset)
  - German: "Sonntagsnachmittagssyndrom" — heightened dementia agitation during reduced natural light (Der Nervenarzt); corroborates B-01 circadian lighting
  - Norwegian SINTEF: reach zone 400–1300 mm forward, 400–1200 mm lateral for powered wheelchair users (extends English-language 400–1100 mm upper limit)
  - Norwegian SINTEF: 87 schools acoustic data confirming Category A RT60 targets (larger sample than English-language cited studies)
  - Swedish Boverket: covered entrance canopy ≥3000×2000 mm (E-05) climate-specific rationale for Nordic winter
  - French CEREMA: hearing loop >50 seats and all service counters (more comprehensive than UK specification)
  - French: Village Landais 31% psychotropic medication reduction (PROVISIONAL — internal evaluation, unpublished in English)
  - Japanese: JIS T9251 TWSI dot/bar pattern preceded ISO 23599; >95% detection rate in urban environments
  - Japanese: PTV ≥40 wet for external surfaces (more stringent than E-07 ≥36; relevant in high-rainfall climates)
  - Spanish INE (2019): only 14% of pre-1981 Spanish dwellings have level entry — lowest compliance rate Western Europe
