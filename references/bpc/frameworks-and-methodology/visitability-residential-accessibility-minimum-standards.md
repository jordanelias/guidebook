## visitability-residential-accessibility-minimum-standards

**Updated:** 2026-03-29 (Opus synthesis)  **Original search:** 2026-03-19 00:30  **Evidence tier:** 4–5 (programme evaluation + cost data)  **Opus synthesis:** YES [OPUS-SYNTHESIS]
**Consensus finding:** Visitability — three core requirements for all new single-family and low-rise residential construction — adds approximately $100–600 to new construction cost and achieves basic access for wheelchair users to visit any home. It is the documented minimum accessible residential provision, below which a building functionally excludes wheelchair users from social participation at private homes.
**Three core visitability requirements (Concrete Change/Eleanor Smith 1987):**
  1. At least one zero-step entrance
  2. Minimum 815 mm (32 inches) clear passage on all ground-floor doors; 915 mm (36 inches) hallways
  3. At least one half-bath (toilet + handwash basin) on the main floor accessible to a wheelchair user
**Cost at construction:** Approximately $100–600 additional in new residential construction — below 0.5% of typical house cost
**Key design specifications from early research synthesis:**
  - Ramp gradients: ADA 1:12 (8.33%); UK 1:12 ≤2m, 1:15 ≤5m, 1:20 ≤10m; AU 1:14 max; DIN 18040 prefers 1:20
  - Minimum ramp width: 915 mm (ADA); 1000 mm (UK/AU); 1200 mm (DIN 18040)
  - Thresholds trending toward zero globally: ADA 13 mm max; DIN 18040 0 mm preferred, 20 mm max; BS 8300 15 mm max; AS 1428.1 5 mm without treatment
  - Entry bench: 430–485 mm seat height; 500–600 mm depth; ≥1112 N (250 lbf) structural strength; adjacent grab bar at 840–915 mm
  - Laundry: front-loading washers on 200–380 mm pedestals bring drum to accessible height; controls front-mounted ≤1220 mm; ≤22.2 N force; top-loaders not ADA-compliant from seated position
  - Home office work surfaces: ADA 710–865 mm; sit-stand desks 640–1300 mm range; no crossbars obstructing knee clearance
  - Wall blocking for future grab bars: 19 mm plywood or min 2×10 lumber between studs at ~915 mm AFF; must withstand 1112 N at any point (Fair Housing Act); document with photographs before drywall
  - Reinforced flooring for future ceiling hoists: standard residential joists insufficient; structural reinforcement required at design stage
**Visitability policy adoption:** Atlanta, Arizona, Texas, Illinois, and other US jurisdictions; Concrete Change estimates 10,000+ visitability homes built globally
**Jurisdictions confirmed:** US (Fair Housing Act 7 features; Concrete Change standards); UK (Lifetime Homes; Part M4(1) mandatory; M4(2) optional); AU (NCC 2022 Livable Housing mandatory); Canada (CAN/ASC B652); Norway (TEK17)
