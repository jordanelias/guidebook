## visitability-residential-accessibility-minimum-standards

**Updated:** 2026-03-30 (v4 schema synthesis)  **Original search:** 2026-03-19 00:30  **Evidence tier:** 4–5 (programme evaluation + cost data)  **Opus synthesis:** YES [OPUS-SYNTHESIS]
**Consensus finding:** Visitability — three core requirements for all new single-family and low-rise residential construction — adds approximately $100–600 to new construction cost and achieves basic access for wheelchair users to visit any home. It is the documented minimum accessible residential provision, below which a building functionally excludes wheelchair users from social participation at private homes.
**Three core visitability requirements (Concrete Change/Eleanor Smith 1987):**
  1. At least one zero-step entrance
  2. Minimum 815 mm (32 inches) clear passage on all ground-floor doors; 915 mm (36 inches) hallways
  3. At least one half-bath (toilet + handwash basin) on the main floor accessible to a wheelchair user
**Cost at construction:** Approximately $100–600 additional — below 0.5% of typical house cost
**Key design specifications from early research synthesis:**
  - Ramp gradients: ADA 1:12; UK 1:12 ≤2m, 1:15 ≤5m, 1:20 ≤10m; AU 1:14 max; DIN 18040 prefers 1:20
  - Minimum ramp width: 915 mm (ADA); 1000 mm (UK/AU); 1200 mm (DIN 18040)
  - Thresholds trending toward zero globally
  - Entry bench: 430–485 mm seat height; 500–600 mm depth; ≥1112 N structural strength
  - Wall blocking for future grab bars: 19 mm plywood or min 2×10 lumber at ~915 mm AFF; must withstand 1112 N
  - Reinforced flooring for future ceiling hoists: standard residential joists insufficient
**Visitability policy adoption:** Atlanta, Arizona, Texas, Illinois; Concrete Change estimates 10,000+ visitability homes built globally
**Jurisdictions confirmed:** US (Fair Housing Act; Concrete Change); UK (Lifetime Homes; Part M4(1) mandatory; M4(2) optional); AU (NCC 2022 mandatory); Canada (CAN/ASC B652); Norway (TEK17)

### Best-practice synthesis
**Most inclusive provision:** Three core visitability requirements at $100–600 per dwelling: zero-step entrance, 815 mm clear passage, ground-floor accessible half-bath.
**Most targeted provision:** Wall blocking for future grab bars (19 mm plywood, ≥1112 N) and reinforced flooring for future ceiling hoists — DAR provisions within the visitability minimum.
**Conflict resolution:** Not applicable — minimum standard definition, not design conflict.
**Highest-ambition actionable specification:** Visitability as absolute floor for all new residential construction; Lifetime Homes (UK), CAN/ASC B652 (Canada), NS 11001-2 (Norway) as progressive above-minimum standards.
**Opus synthesis note:** Visitability is the documented minimum. Cost evidence ($100–600) is the strongest argument for policy adoption. Cross-reference residential-dar-provisions for the full DAR cost multiplier register.
