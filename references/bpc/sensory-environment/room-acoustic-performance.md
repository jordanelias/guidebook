### Best-practice synthesis

**Population differentiation is the defining issue** in room acoustic performance specifications for disability populations. All jurisdictions agree on general population classroom RT60 targets (0.4–0.6 s depending on room volume). The critical divergence emerges when disability-specific populations are considered:

**For DEAF / hearing device users (hearing aids, cochlear implants):**
Best-practice provision: RT60 ≤ 0.3 s in classrooms ≤ 283 m³. This requirement is now peer-reviewed (Iglehart, 2020, AJA) and formally encoded in ANSI/ASA S12.60-2010/Part 1 Footnote e. It applies to children with hearing aids or cochlear implants. The standard general-population requirement (0.6 s) is demonstrably insufficient for hearing device users; speech perception scores are significantly better at 0.3 s than 0.6 s for this population. Background noise target: ≤ 30–35 dB(A) (more stringent than the standard ≤ 35 dB(A)).

**For NDV/AUT (autism spectrum, sensory hypersensitivity):**
Best-practice provision: No internationally agreed quantified RT60 target specific to autistic users. Bettarello et al. (2021, *Applied Sciences*) and Caniato et al. (2024) confirm that existing standards (ANSI/ASA S12.60, BB93, UNI 11532-2) are calibrated to neurotypical populations and are insufficient for NDV/AUT. Autistic users are significantly more affected by modest background noise increases (52→55 dB(A)) and by sudden noise events than neurotypical users. Evidence supports: lower background noise targets (≤ 35 dB(A); aspiration: ≤ 30 dB(A)), broadband absorption to suppress flutter echo and reflective peaks, no sound masking systems (A-13 doctrine), and sensory gradient zoning (F-01). Sound masking is contraindicated for NDV/AUT.

**For DEM (dementia):**
Best-practice provision: Acoustic calm as primary therapeutic intervention. BrainXchange (Canada, Tier 2 Co-1) documents background noise management as clinically significant for dementia care. Devos et al. (2019, PMC) confirm RT60 measurement and acoustic intervention as standard practice in Belgian nursing homes, with measurable outcomes. Lyngby-Taarbæk case study (Part IX-06) documents 40% reduction in agitation events following acoustic ceiling installation. Targets consistent with NDV: NC-25 (≤ 35 dB(A)), RT60 ≤ 0.5 s in occupied common areas.

**For NEU/PCS:**
Best-practice provision: Flicker-free, low background noise, NC-25 or better. No specific RT60 target for PCS confirmed beyond general accessibility guidance. The PCS population is absent from all room acoustic standards reviewed in any language.

**For OFS/PAIN:**
No room acoustic standard in any jurisdiction addresses these populations acoustically. THIN-BASE disclosure applies.

### Divergent findings
- IT (UNI 11532-2) is the most advanced standard for NDV-equivalent provisions: explicitly names students with hearing deficits or attention/concentration disorders as a distinct category (categoria A3.1/A4) with more stringent RT60 and STI requirements
- ANSI/ASA S12.60-2010 is the only standard with a mandatory RT60 ≤ 0.3 s for DEAF users — no equivalent in DIN 18041, BB93, or any other reviewed standard
- DIN 18041:2016 introduced disability/inclusion as a driver for its 2016 revision (explicitly citing BGG, Behindertengleichstellungsgesetz, UN-CRPD) — the only standard that explicitly frames its requirements within a disability rights framework
