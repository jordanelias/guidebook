**Simpson, K., Adams, D. & Dargue, N. (2025)** — "The built school environment and autistic students: Systematic review." *International Journal of Inclusive Education*. 23 studies. **Key findings:** Noise and visual environmental elements have the strongest associations with autistic student outcomes. Cross-references sensory-relief-space-design for MSE provision within school settings. **Tier 3.** [GREY — DOI required; journal not indexed in PubMed]

**Impact on NR-EDU (Part 7 education matrix):** This SR provides the first systematic evidence base specifically for school built environment design for autistic students. Noise (acoustic: RT60, background NC) and visual (glare, pattern, colour saturation) elements confirmed as highest-impact. Corroborates A-08 (NC-25) and A-02 (RT60) specifications for NR-EDU contexts.
