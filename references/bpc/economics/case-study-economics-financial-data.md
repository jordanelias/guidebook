### Cross-case analysis (Sonnet — no best-practice determination)

**Cost range pattern (VERIFIED + PROVISIONAL only):**
- Large-scale multi-disability housing (new build): $88.3M/112 units = ~$788k/unit (SF 2025 market context — not isolated AD premium)
- Individual home modifications (retrofit): $700–$8,000 depending on scope (UK/US)
- AD premium for wheelchair standard uplift (new build): ~£22,000 additional per M4(3) unit vs M4(2) (UK 2021-22 prices)
- Government grants covering retrofit: up to £30,000/home (UK DFG), CAD $20,000 (BC HAFI), ~$2,825 total (CAPABLE)
- Dementia village models: €19.3M–€36M new build; €5,000–€6,571/resident/month operating costs
- Per-participant outcome-generating modification cost (CAPABLE): ~$700–$1,400 producing measurable functional independence gains

**Gap flag:** No case study provides an isolated AD premium for a multi-disability accessible building from schematic-stage new construction. CS-23 provides M4(3) wheelchair standard premium, but that is a single-population specification. This gap is consistent with existing evidence that AD premium is hard to isolate post-project.

**Highest-quality economic evidence in corpus:** CS-22 (CAPABLE RCT) — per-participant cost, disaggregated home modification component, documented functional outcomes, multi-site US. CS-23 (LSE/Habinteg) — formal cost analysis against standard, Ministry of Housing data.
