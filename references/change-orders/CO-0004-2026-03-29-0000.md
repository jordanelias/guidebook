### Design Principle

Part 3 is the architect's decision-making toolkit. It answers: *How do I think about combining provisions? How do I sequence decisions? What do I do when specifications conflict?* It does not contain concrete item-level application — that follows in Parts 4–7.

The resolution decision tree (§3.8) and strategies menu (§3.9) are the Part 8 proposal content that migrates here. They are methodology, not application. The architect learns the framework before encountering the items it operates on.

## New Part 5: Building-Level Co-Occurrence Resolution

### Scope Definition

Part 5 owns **building-wide and system-level co-occurrence conflicts that no single room matrix in Parts 6/7 can contain.** These are conflicts operating across:

- Multiple rooms (e.g., visual alerting system throughout all circulation)
- Whole-building systems (e.g., HVAC thermal zoning, acoustic zoning, lighting control systems)
- Cross-disciplinary boundaries (e.g., acoustic specification vs hearing loop coverage vs fire alarm visual alerting)

**Part 5 does NOT contain:**
- Room-level conflicts → resolved inline in Parts 6/7 matrix annotations
- Methodology → Part 3 §3.8–§3.9
- Item specifications → Part 4
- Engineering coordination → Part 8
- Consultant appointment → Part 9

### Known §5.2 Entries (building-wide system conflicts)

| Entry | Conflict | Type | Systems involved |
|---|---|---|---|
| Visual alerting vs photosensitivity | DEAF/DBL need strobes throughout; NEU seizure risk; NDV/AUT photosensitivity | INTER | Fire alarm, visual paging, emergency lighting — all circulation |
| Acoustic zoning vs hearing loop coverage | Hearing loop requires specific acoustic conditions; NDV/AUT needs quiet zones without loop interference | INTER | Acoustic treatment, assistive listening — whole building |
| Thermal zoning | Uhthoff's phenomenon (≤20°C) vs fibromyalgia/PAIN (≥22°C); same or different occupants | BOTH | HVAC — whole building |
| Wayfinding system coherence | VIS needs TWSI tactile indicators on floor; DEM/PAIN needs plain vibration-free flooring | INTER | Flooring, wayfinding — all circulation |
| Glazed internal partitions | DEAF sightlines (need transparency) vs DEM glare vs NDV visual overstimulation vs VIS reflection | BOTH | Partition system — all internal junctions |
| Circadian lighting system | DEM/NEU benefit from high melanopic daytime light; NDV/AUT photosensitivity; MH needs individual control | BOTH | Lighting control — all occupied spaces |
| Acoustic differentiation vs sensory calm | VIS uses acoustic variation for spatial orientation; DEM needs uniform acoustic calm | INTER | Acoustic treatment — all circulation |
| Vibrotactile alerting in sleeping areas | Resolves strobe/photosensitivity conflict for bedrooms but introduces vibration sensitivity for PAIN/OFS | INTER | Alerting system — residential sleeping areas |

**Estimated entries:** 8–12 building-wide conflicts + 4–6 unresolvable conflicts + 3 worked examples.

### Known §5.3 Entries (unresolvable)

| Entry | Why unresolvable |
|---|---|
| Glazed junction four-way (CON-0020) | No single glazing specification satisfies DEAF transparency + DEM anti-glare + NDV low-stimulation + VIS anti-reflection simultaneously |
| Thermal co-occurrence in shared space (FDR) | MS Uhthoff's and fibromyalgia in same person in a space without individual thermal control (e.g., corridor) |
| Acoustic differentiation vs calm | VIS navigation needs variation; DEM needs uniformity; same corridor |

### §5.4 Worked Examples

Three building-scale scenarios (per DEC-14 preliminary):

1. **Residential compound** — DEM+MOB+PAIN+VIS individual in specialist housing. Walk through building-wide systems: thermal zoning, lighting control, alerting, wayfinding coherence. Show how Part 3 decision tree applies across the whole building, not just one room.
2. **Public library inter-group** — DEAF / NDV/AUT / VIS populations as different visitors. Building-wide acoustic zoning, visual alerting strategy, wayfinding system design.
3. **Hospital ward compound+inter** — MOB+PAIN+DEM+OFS patients (intra) + different patients with different profiles (inter). Ward-level systems: nurse call, lighting, thermal, acoustic, wayfinding.

## Parts 6/7: Room-Level Conflict Resolution Inline

### Change to Matrix Format

Parts 6/7 matrices currently list items per room per population. Where items in the same room conflict for a co-occurring or multi-population scenario, the matrix gains an inline conflict note.

**Format:**

```
**Conflict note — [Room code]:**
Items [X-NN] and [Y-NN] conflict for [POPULATION+POPULATION]:
[brief description]. Resolution: [Part 3 §3.9.N strategy].
See Part 5 §5.2-NN if building-wide.
```

**Examples:**
- R-BA (Bathroom): C-04 (LRV ≥30) and C-05 (low LRV differential) conflict for VIS+DEM. Resolution: tiered specification (Part 3 §3.9.5 SRW). Room-level — resolved here.
- R-BED (Bedroom): B-10 (visual fire alarm strobe) and §NDV photosensitivity. Building-wide system → cross-reference Part 5 §5.2-01.

This ensures room-level conflicts are resolved where the architect encounters them. Building-wide conflicts point to Part 5.

## Volume Structure

### Old (3 volumes)

| Volume | Parts | Title |
|---|---|---|
| I | 1–3 | Principles, Evidence and Population Guidance |
| II | 4–10 | Application Manual and Engineering |
| III | 11–13 | DAR, Economics, Evidence and Case Studies |

### New (2 volumes)

| Volume | Parts | Title |
|---|---|---|
| I | 1–9 | Accessible Design: Principles, Specifications and Application |
| II | 10–12 | Evidence, Economics and Case Studies |

### Rationale

The old I/II split was theory/practice. In practice, disability categories (old Vol I Part 2) are heavily prescriptive, and the architect needs continuous reference between populations and specifications. A single operational volume eliminates the artificial boundary. Volume II retains content that is supplementary/reference (DAR frameworks, cost data, case studies) — material the architect consults rather than applies sequentially.

## Item Code Prefix Change

Old prefix: `V2-P8-xx` (Volume II, Part 8 = Item Specification Library)
New prefix: `V1-P4-xx` (Volume I, Part 4 = Item Specification Library)

**However:** Item category codes (A-01 through I-03) are Part-independent per project-standards. The volume-part prefix is used only in cross-volume references. Since Items are now in the same volume as everything else that references them, the prefix becomes less necessary.

**Recommendation:** Retain item codes as `A-01`, `B-06`, etc. without volume-part prefix in body text. Use `P4-A-01` only when referencing from Volume II (Parts 10–12) or Supplementary Volume. This simplifies all cross-references within Volume I.
