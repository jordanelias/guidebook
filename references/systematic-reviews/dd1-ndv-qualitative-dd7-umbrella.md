### 1.3 Key Findings Across Reviews

**Convergence (confirmed across ≥3 reviews):**
1. **User control of sensory inputs is the highest-confidence design variable** — confirmed by Watchorn 2025, Leonardi 2025, Rashid 2025, Kiblen 2024. This aligns with project BPC finding (Unwin 2022/2023 in sensory-relief-space-design.md).
2. **Acoustic environment is the most-researched sensory domain** for NDV — Simpson 2025 (23 studies), Rashid 2025, Bertone 2021 all identify noise/reverberation as primary barrier.
3. **Transition zones between stimulation levels** are consistently recommended — Rashid 2025, Bertone 2021, Kiblen 2024. Project A-16 specifies ≥3m decompression space; this is confirmed.
4. **Retreat/quiet spaces** are near-universally recommended — all 9 reviews mention them. But Watchorn 2025 and the project's own Kawa analysis note the private-retreat model is culturally specific.
5. **No evidence-based dimensional specifications exist** for sensory rooms — Leonardi 2025 explicitly states this. The project's ≥8m² minimum (vs NCC AU 3m²) remains unsupported by primary research.

**Divergence/gaps:**
1. **Adult vs paediatric** — Leonardi 2025 finds improvements in children but not adolescents/adults. Most research is paediatric. Adult sensory space evidence is thin.
2. **Outdoor vs indoor** — MDPI Land 2024 is the only identified study on outdoor sensory environments for NDV. The project has no outdoor sensory specification.
3. **Spatial configuration vs interior elements** — Rashid 2025 identifies that most frameworks overemphasise interior elements over spatial configuration (adjacency, sequencing, approach). This echoes the project's BPC finding.
4. **Hyposensitivity** — Most research focuses on hypersensitivity (over-stimulation). Hyposensitivity (under-stimulation) is under-researched. Project FDR registry notes this as a gap (sensory-relief-space-design PARTIAL).
