## 1. Scoping Review: What Evidence Exists and Where Meta-Analysis Is Feasible

### 1.1 Methodology Note

True meta-analysis requires: (a) quantitative outcome data, (b) comparable study designs, (c) poolable effect sizes, (d) sufficient study count (≥3 minimum, ≥10 for robust pooling). Most accessible built environment research is qualitative, case-study-based, code-comparative, or uses heterogeneous outcome measures. **Formal meta-analysis is feasible in ≤15% of the project's evidence domains.** The remainder requires narrative synthesis, scoping review, or evidence mapping only.

## 3. Prioritized Deep-Dives: Top 5 Evidence-Rich Domains

Ranked by: (a) volume of poolable primary studies, (b) clinical significance, (c) specification impact.

### Deep-Dive 1: Bathroom Grab Bar Positioning and Fall Prevention
- **Domain:** Category G (G-03, G-04) × Room R-BA × Populations MOB/DEM/OFS
- **Why:** Richest Tier 1 evidence in the project. Multiple RCTs on grab bar height, angle, and bilateral placement. Falls are the highest-consequence outcome. 3 BPC files + 3 FDR slugs COMPLETE.
- **Meta-analysis protocol:** Pool fall-rate reduction by grab bar configuration. Subgroup by population (MOB wheelchair transfer vs ambulant vs DEM).
- **Expected study count:** 15-25 poolable studies.
- **Outcome measure:** Fall rate reduction (OR/RR), independence in transfer (FIM score change).

### Deep-Dive 2: Acoustic Environment — RT60 and STI by Population
- **Domain:** Category A (A-01 through A-14) × Populations DEAF/NDV/DEM × Rooms NR-EDU
- **Why:** Quantitative outcome measures exist (RT60 seconds, STI ratio). Well-studied in classroom acoustics. Direct specification impact — project uses STI ≥0.60 / ≥0.75 thresholds. 11 sensory-environment BPC files + 4 communication BPC files.
- **Meta-analysis protocol:** Pool STI/RT60 thresholds by population and room type. Subgroup by hearing device type (HA, CI, unaided).
- **Expected study count:** 20-40 poolable studies.
- **Outcome measure:** Speech recognition score (% correct) at given RT60/STI.

### Deep-Dive 3: LRV Contrast Thresholds for Wayfinding
- **Domain:** Category C (C-04, C-05) × Population VIS/DEM × All rooms
- **Why:** Quantitative (LRV difference). The project's ≥30 LRV threshold is a key specification. 2 dedicated BPC files (luminance-contrast-lrv-evidence-base, luminance-contrast-and-pattern). Well-studied in vision science.
- **Meta-analysis protocol:** Pool detection/identification accuracy by LRV differential. Subgroup by visual condition (low vision type, age-related, DEM).
- **Expected study count:** 10-20 poolable studies.
- **Outcome measure:** Object/boundary detection rate (%) at given LRV differential.

### Deep-Dive 4: Thermoregulation and Ambient Temperature Thresholds
- **Domain:** TC items × Populations NEU(MS, SCI)/OFS/PAIN × Conflict domains
- **Why:** Directly evidence-based specification (18-22°C working target). Multiple clinical studies on Uhthoff phenomenon, SCI thermoregulation failure, FMS cold pain. 7 health-and-symptom BPC files. Confirmed inter-population conflict (FMS vs MS/SCI).
- **Meta-analysis protocol:** Pool symptom exacerbation threshold temperatures by condition. Subgroup by SCI level, MS type, FMS severity.
- **Expected study count:** 15-30 poolable studies (clinical neurology literature).
- **Outcome measure:** Symptom onset/exacerbation temperature (°C); functional performance change.

### Deep-Dive 5: Accessible Construction Cost Premium — New Build
- **Domain:** Part 11 §11.2 × All populations × All building types
- **Why:** The "cost myth" is the single most consequential economic claim. Multiple cost studies exist across jurisdictions. 3 economics BPC files.
- **Meta-analysis protocol:** Pool % cost premium for accessibility compliance (code minimum) and beyond-code inclusive design. Subgroup by jurisdiction, building type, new vs retrofit.
- **Expected study count:** 8-15 poolable studies.
- **Outcome measure:** % cost premium over non-accessible baseline.

### 4.5 Critical Evidence Gaps (Highest Priority)

1. **Compound functioning** — zero primary built-environment studies on supra-additive effects of co-occurring conditions. All compound specifications derived from clinical reasoning. 19 FDR compound/occupation/environment scenarios NOT-RUN.
2. **OFS/PAIN/DBL as built-environment populations** — near-zero Tier 1-2 evidence. All specifications extrapolated from clinical literature + general codes.
3. **Non-residential rooms** — NR-RET, NR-CUL, NR-HOS, NR-TRP have essentially zero population-specific built-environment research. Specifications are code-based (Tier 6) plus analogy from residential.
4. **Global South** — implementation evidence, not specification evidence. Formal/informal divide is the meta-barrier. No accessible bathroom specification exists for informal settlements.
5. **Collectivist-context sensory spaces** — PAS 6463 private retreat model does not transfer. No alternative model exists. FDR-ENV-05 is P1 but NOT-RUN.
6. **Per-item economic data** — no cross-jurisdiction cost pooling possible. Each jurisdiction's cost data is essentially standalone.
7. **Living room, garage, laundry** — zero or near-zero dedicated research for any population.

## 5. Actionable Recommendations

### 5.1 What To Do Now (within project scope)

1. **Execute the 5 deep-dives** as PRISMA-lite systematic reviews using `literature-review-planner` to construct search protocols. Acoustics and bathroom grab bars have sufficient studies for formal meta-analysis.
2. **Run the 22 P1 FDR scenarios** — this is already queued. The compound and occupation scenarios will generate the first structured evidence for co-occurrence built-environment effects.
3. **Produce evidence density statements** per Part/section using the gap map above. These become the §11.6 evidence landscape update and the ● / ○ marker audit.

### 5.2 What Requires External Research (beyond project scope)

1. **Formal meta-analysis with forest plots** — requires statistical software (R/Stata), risk-of-bias assessment (Cochrane RoB 2 or ROBINS-I), and PRISMA reporting. This is a research programme, not a guidebook task.
2. **Primary data collection** — compound functioning, Global South, non-residential rooms, living room design. No amount of literature search will fill these gaps; they require new research.
3. **Cost data standardisation** — cross-jurisdiction economic meta-analysis requires PPP adjustment, construction-type normalisation, and temporal deflation. This is QS/economist work.

### 5.3 What The Guidebook Should Disclose

Every Part should carry an evidence density statement using the gap map categories:
- ■ Rich evidence base — specifications grounded in Tier 1-3
- ▓ Moderate — specifications grounded in Tier 3-5 with some Tier 1-2 support
- ░ Thin — specifications extrapolated from adjacent evidence; THIN-BASE disclosure required
- · Absent — specification based on clinical reasoning, code extension, or expert consensus only; full gap disclosure required

This is already partially implemented via ● / ○ markers. The gap map above provides the systematic basis for completing that implementation.
