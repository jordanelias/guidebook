## Appendix B: Operational Requirements Register

> **Purpose:** This appendix consolidates all operational (non-architectural) requirements from the guidebook body into a single FM-facing document. These requirements are policy, staffing, cleaning, and maintenance specifications that must be embedded in the FM brief, lease agreements, and staff handbooks — not in construction drawings. The architectural items they support are referenced for traceability.

> **Authority:** Part 4 is the specification source. This appendix is an extraction, not an independent specification. Where any discrepancy exists, Part 4 governs.

---

### OPS-01: Fragrance-Free Building Policy

**Source items:** F-06, F-04 (Air Quality)
**Populations:** OFS/MCAS, NDV/SENS, NDV/AUT, NEU/PCS
**Design stage:** Brief (must be in occupancy agreements before construction)

**Requirements:**
1. Written fragrance-free policy embedded in: FM brief, lease/occupancy agreements, staff handbook, and cleaning contracts.
2. Policy scope: occupants, staff, contractors, cleaning operatives.
3. Cleaning products: fragrance-free, low-VOC formulations specified throughout the whole building. No aerosol sprays during occupied hours.
4. No air freshener, scent diffuser, or fragranced cleaning product in any OFS/MCAS-occupied space.
5. Staff working in OFS/MCAS-occupied environments instructed in no personal fragrance rule.
6. Compliance audit: quarterly FM walk-through of fragranced product presence.

**Evidence:** Mast Cell Action guidance (Tier Co-1); clinical plausibility (MCAS mast cell degranulation trigger).

### OPS-02: Furniture Consistency Policy

**Source items:** D-09, D-02
**Populations:** DEM, DBL, NDV/AUT, VIS
**Design stage:** Operational (ongoing — post-RFO)

**Requirements:**
1. No furniture rearrangement in DEM, DBL, or NDV-designated spaces without user consultation and re-orientation walk-through.
2. Furniture positions in shared residential rooms documented in room plan (photographic record at handover).
3. Replacement furniture to match spatial footprint of original (no dimensional changes that affect circulation or tactile routes).
4. DBL-specific: identical furniture layout across all units in multi-unit residential settings.

**Evidence:** De Hogeweyk POE (Dutch, BuroKade/Vivium); DEM spatial predictability evidence (Marquardt 2011).

### OPS-03: Queue Management

**Source items:** G-07, E-08
**Populations:** OFS, MOB, PAIN, DEM
**Design stage:** Operational (ongoing)

**Requirements:**
1. Seating available within the queue area — not at a separate accessible position — so OFS users can wait without standing.
2. Take-a-number or digital queue systems are an acceptable alternative to physical queuing lines.
3. No serpentine queue barriers within wheelchair turning circle zones.
4. No barrier or queue management system (post, rope, barrier arm) within the 1500 mm wheelchair turning circle at any service counter.

**Evidence:** Raj 2013 (orthostatic onset time 2–10 min at standing); JAN POTS accommodation.

### OPS-04: Cleaning Specification

**Source items:** E-07, C-01, C-03, F-06
**Populations:** ALL
**Design stage:** Brief (embedded in cleaning contract)

**Requirements:**
1. Cleaning regime must not reduce PTV below ≥36 threshold — specify in cleaning contract.
2. High-traffic burnishing of LVT/vinyl can increase gloss above 30 GU — cleaning specification must maintain matte finish (≤30 GU).
3. Fragrance-free cleaning products throughout (see OPS-01).
4. Low-VOC cleaning products in all OFS/MCAS-occupied spaces.
5. No aerosol sprays during occupied hours.
6. Cleaning schedule timed outside peak OFS/NDV occupancy hours where practicable.

**Evidence:** PTV standards (BS 7976-2); clinical plausibility for fragrance/VOC requirements.

### OPS-05: Maintenance Schedule

**Source items:** E-07, A-11, B-01, H-02, H-05
**Populations:** ALL
**Design stage:** Brief (embedded in FM maintenance plan)

**Requirements:**
1. Hearing loop (A-11): IEC 60118-4 field strength test annually; battery replacement schedule for portable loops.
2. Circadian lighting (B-01): melanopic EDI verification at commissioning and every 5 years (lamp degradation).
3. Slip resistance (E-07): PTV re-test annually in high-traffic wet areas; after any floor treatment or replacement.
4. Environmental control (H-02): ECU system test quarterly; UPS battery replacement per manufacturer schedule.
5. Nurse call (H-05): pull cord and push button test monthly; battery replacement in wireless pendants.
6. Automatic doors (E-11): sensor calibration and force test biannually per BS 7036.

**Evidence:** IEC 60118-4; BS 7036; NHS HTM guidance.

### OPS-06: Emergency Response Protocols

**Source items:** H-05, B-10, K-04
**Populations:** DEM, DBL, MOB, NEU
**Design stage:** Brief + RFO (operational protocol)

**Requirements:**
1. Personal Emergency Evacuation Plan (PEEP) for each occupant with mobility, sensory, or cognitive support needs — documented at move-in.
2. Vibrotactile fire alarm (B-10/K-04): building management system linked to vibrotactile alerting in all DBL-occupied sleeping areas.
3. Staff training: annual fire evacuation drill including assisted evacuation procedures for wheelchair users, DEM residents, and DBL/VIS occupants.
4. Refuge areas: communication system between refuge and building management centre tested monthly.

**Evidence:** BS 9999; Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 (UK); clinical consensus.

---

### OPS-07: Staff Training Requirements

**Source items:** F-06, D-09, H-02, H-05
**Populations:** ALL (staff-facing)
**Design stage:** RFO (operational)

**Requirements:**
1. Fragrance-free policy briefing at induction for all staff working in OFS/MCAS environments.
2. Environmental control (H-02) operation training for care staff — how to adjust lighting, temperature, and acoustic settings per individual care plans.
3. Nurse call (H-05) response protocol training — including DBL vibrotactile alert interpretation.
4. Furniture consistency (D-09) briefing for cleaning and maintenance staff — no rearrangement protocol.
5. PEEP training for all staff in buildings with mobility/sensory-impaired occupants.

**Evidence:** NHS England HTM guidance; CQC (UK) staff training requirements.
