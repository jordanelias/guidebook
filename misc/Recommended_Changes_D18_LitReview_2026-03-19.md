## PART A: EDITORIAL DECISION D-18
### OT Professional Body Guidelines Elevated to Tier 3

**Decision:** OT professional body clinical guidelines, practice standards, and position
statements are elevated from Tier 5–6 to Tier 3, co-equal with systematic reviews and
meta-analyses. They supersede regulatory codes (Tier 6) and national beyond-code frameworks
(Tier 5) throughout the guidebook.

**Revised Evidence Hierarchy:**

| Tier | Type |
|---|---|
| 1 | OT clinical research — intervention-tested; person-environment-occupation measured |
| Co-1 | Lived experience and participatory design (CRPD Art. 4.3) — co-primary with Tier 1 |
| 2 | Disability-led NGO/DPO/advocacy guidelines |
| **3** | **Systematic reviews AND meta-analyses AND OT professional body clinical guidelines/standards/practice documents** |
| 4 | International standards with evidence basis (ISO, IEC, CRPD Art. 9, EN, PAS) |
| 5 | National beyond-code frameworks |
| 6 | Statutory codes — reference baseline only |

After the line:

```

**Insert:**
```
[EVIDENCE NOTE — ENGINEERING-DERIVED: The 300 kg / 3 kN static load specification for
bariatric grab bars is derived from clinical engineering load calculations based on body
weight distributions and dynamic transfer forces, not from peer-reviewed OT clinical
research establishing this value as a functional bariatric threshold. HMInfo/UNSW (2015)

bariatric clients." The most directly relevant OT biomechanical evidence (Guay et al.,

transfer populations, not bariatric-specific. Tier 4–5; engineering-derived; evidence
