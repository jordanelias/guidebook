## Rationale

The existing evidence base has three structural weaknesses:

1. **Jurisdictional bias.** All 24 current jurisdictions are Global North or East Asia. This produces specifications calibrated to Northern European housing typologies, cold/temperate climates, high-income health systems, and regulatory enforcement contexts that do not transfer to most of the world's disabled population.

2. **Linguistic gap.** 14 current languages omit Arabic (~400M speakers), Hindi (~600M), Indonesian/Malay (~280M), Swahili (~250M), and Bengali (~230M) — languages with substantial disability research, NGO networks, and standards activity.

3. **Evidence source narrowing.** The search protocol prioritises national OT bodies and statutory codes. In Global South contexts, primary evidence production runs through WHO, UN-HABITAT, CBR networks, ESCAP, and disability-led NGOs operating outside Western institutional structures. These are Co-1 and Tier 2 sources by the project's own hierarchy — they have not been retrieved.

These gaps are not decorative. They distort specifications for thermoregulation (OFS, MST, TCOA), spatial economy (crowded urban contexts), climate exposure (tropical humidity, extreme heat), and disability prevalence (Global South carries ~80% of the world's disabled population by WHO estimates). They also undermine CRPD Article 9 coverage, which binds all signatory states including low- and middle-income countries.

## Section 1 — Scope and Research Questions

### Primary question
What evidence exists on accessible built environment design for disabled people across Global South jurisdictions and non-Western language bodies, and how does it compare to, extend, or contradict current BPC specifications?

### Secondary questions
1. Which BPC slugs have evidence confined to ≤3 jurisdictions, all Global North?
2. What accessible design frameworks exist in Africa, South and Southeast Asia, MENA, and Latin America beyond Brazil?
3. Where do climate, housing typology, or health system differences produce genuinely different optimal specifications (not just different standards baselines)?
4. What does Global South disability-led (Co-1) literature say about built environment priorities that current BPC entries do not reflect?
5. What additional languages contain peer-reviewed OT and accessibility research not captured in the current 14-language pass?

### Population codes in scope
All: MOB · VIS · DEAF · DBL · DEM · NDV · NDV-MH · NEU · OFS · PAIN · IntD · TCOA

### Building typologies in scope
All guidebook typologies. Priority: residential and health facility (highest Global South research volume).

### Date range
2000–2026. Pre-2000 foundational sources retained where no more recent evidence exists.

### Exclusion criteria
- Sources from already-covered jurisdictions adding no new evidence to existing BPC entries
- Grey literature without traceable institutional authorship
- Sources that use "accessible design" as rhetorical frame without functional specifications
- Standards-only sources with no evidence of effect (these go to Tier 6; do not replace Tier 1–3 search)

## Section 3 — PRISMA-Aligned Protocol

### Identification
- Databases: existing 8 (multilingual-research skill) + 7 new (§2a above)
- Grey literature: Tier 2 DPO targets (§2c) — direct retrieval from organisation websites
- Citation mining: backward + forward for every Tier 1–2 source identified
- Temporal scope: 2000–2026; foundational exceptions documented

### Screening (title/abstract)
Include if:
- Relates to physical built environment (spatial, thermal, acoustic, tactile, circulation)
- Involves disabled people as primary population or sub-population
- Reports functional outcome, user preference, or spatial parameter
- Any language (full-text translation flagged for unavailable languages)

Exclude if:
- Purely medical/clinical without environmental dimension
- Technology-only solution (app, wearable) without spatial correlate
- Rehabilitation without housing/building context

### Eligibility (full-text)
| Criterion | Decision rule |
|---|---|
| Study design | Any (RCT preferred; observational, qualitative, POE, participatory included) |
| Population | Any disability population in guidebook scope |
| Outcome | Spatial parameter value, functional independence measure, user experience |
| Date | 2000–2026 (pre-2000 retained if no later evidence on slug) |
| Jurisdiction | Priority: Global South + already-covered jurisdictions where slug is THIN |

### Data extraction fields
Author-Year · DOI · Language · Jurisdiction · Study design · Population code(s) · N · Setting (residential/public/health/education) · Functional outcome measured · Spatial parameter(s) · Value or range · Guidebook tier · Climate zone · Housing typology · Co-1 status (lived experience / DPO authorship)

### Quality appraisal
| Design | Tool |
|---|---|
| RCT | PEDro |
| Observational | NOS |
| Systematic review | AMSTAR-2 |
| Qualitative / participatory | CASP |
| Standards / frameworks | AGREE-II |
| Co-1 (lived experience) | No quality exclusion — tier recorded, method documented |

## Section 6 — Synthesis Framework

### Quantitative
Where ≥3 studies across ≥2 jurisdictions report a common spatial parameter → specification table. Compare Global South value to current BPC value. If divergent, note and flag for Opus synthesis.

### Narrative
Where evidence is qualitative, participatory, or heterogeneous → structured narrative with explicit framing:
- Convergent with current BPC: confirm with jurisdiction note
- Divergent from current BPC: state cause (climate, typology, resource, culture) — do not resolve
- Genuinely absent: GAP entry with P classification

### Standards gap
Where Global South statutory standard differs from current BPC specification → comparison table. Example: Indian Harmonised Guidelines 2021 ramp gradient (1:12) vs. current BPC recommendation. State both; flag for Opus.

### Cross-jurisdictional climate split
Where evidence clusters by climate zone rather than jurisdiction (e.g., tropical vs. temperate thermoregulation):
- Produce a climate-stratified table rather than jurisdiction table
- Tag each BPC entry with applicable climate zone(s)

### Co-1 weight
Global South Co-1 findings (DPO reports, CBR participatory outputs) receive equal weight to Tier 1 clinical research per §1.5. Where Co-1 and Tier 1 conflict, surface both without resolution. Opus queue.

## Notes on Global South Evidence Quality

The project's evidence hierarchy already accommodates this expansion without rule changes:

- **CBR network publications** are Co-1 where authored by or with disabled people, Tier 2 where produced by disability-led NGOs. Both are above Tier 3.
- **WHO and UN-HABITAT reports** are Tier 4 where grounded in evidence, Tier 5 where framework-level.
- **Thin bases** are expected in new jurisdictions. THIN flag + P classification is correct; do not downgrade to P3 simply because Global South evidence is sparse — the absence itself is a gap that must be named.
- **Language barriers:** Where full-text retrieval is unavailable (e.g., Amharic-language materials), log RETRIEVAL-BLOCKED as distinct from THIN. Do not count unavailable sources as absent evidence.
- **Translation verification:** Machine-translated search results should be flagged; do not treat machine translation as confirmed source content.
