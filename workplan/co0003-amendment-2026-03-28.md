## Conceptual Model Change

The workplan's integration points for `connection-scout` and `functional-deficit-researcher` should be understood through the co-occurring lens:

- **connection-scout** findings are predominantly **intra-individual co-occurrence** patterns (one person benefits from a provision for compound reasons across multiple conditions). The "cross-population connection" concept was misleading — most connections describe provisions that serve co-occurring conditions in individuals, not negotiations between groups.

- **functional-deficit-researcher** scenarios are inherently individual-level — ICF codes describe one person's functional profile. FDR findings that span multiple population codes are evidence of co-occurrence, not cross-population extension.

- **Part 8 §8.4 target** (20–25 documented resolutions) should be reclassified: intra-individual resolutions (Tier 2, OT-managed) vs inter-group resolutions (Tier 0/1, architecturally managed). Expected split: ~70% intra-individual, ~30% inter-group.

## Part 8 Structural Revision

The workplan Session 17 (P3-W08) scope expands:

**Old structure:**
- §8.1 Introduction
- §8.2 Methodology
- §8.4 Documented Resolutions (20–25 entries)
- §8.5 Unresolvable Conflicts

**New structure:**
- §8.1 Introduction — why co-occurrence is the norm; why "cross-population" was inadequate
- §8.2 Methodology — three-tier hierarchy as resolution framework; Lawton docility-proactivity mapping (CON-0028)
- §8.3 Intra-Individual Co-Occurrence Resolutions (Tier 2) — OT assessment resolves competing needs within specification ranges
- §8.4 Inter-Group Multi-Population Resolutions (Tier 0/1) — architectural provisions for buildings serving multiple disability profiles
- §8.5 Conflicts Requiring Both Resolution Pathways
- §8.6 Unresolvable Conflicts — spatial separation required; no specification range contains both needs

This is a scope expansion, not a scope change. The evidence base (connection register) is the same; the organisation is clearer.
