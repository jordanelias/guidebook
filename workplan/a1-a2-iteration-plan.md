## Why iterate

Single iterative phase produces both."* The two deliverables (`governance/audience-priority.md` and `governance/mission-and-epistemics.md`) cannot be written in parallel and hoped to cohere. Each pass tests one against the other. This file specifies the passes.

## What "canonical" means

A document is canonical when:
1. It is internally consistent.
2. It is consistent with the other A1-A2 deliverable (cross-test pass).
3. It is consistent with the doctrinal commitments in `references/project-standards.md` and the revised D-03 in `governance/pre-stage-a-decisions.md`.
4. Every claim is either cited or explicitly flagged as uncited.
5. The author has read it front-to-back and made no further revisions.

Until all five conditions hold, the document remains marked iter-N.

## Pre-launch solo constraint on A1-A2

Per Amendment 7 and revised D-03: A1-A2 is solo authorship. There is no Co-1 review at iteration boundaries. The acceptance test (item 4 above — read front-to-back) is the strongest available rigor pre-launch. The methodological limit is declared in the canonical mission (§3 operational reality, §6 questions-led teaching, §Methodological boundaries final paragraph).

This is a known weaker rigor than external Co-1 review would provide. It is what pre-launch solo allows. Future-state collaboration (post-launch contingent) could introduce external review as a downstream revision pass; that revision is not in scope for A1-A2 pre-launch.

## What this plan does not do

- **Does not commit canonical documents.** Iter 1 (this session) produces a marked iteration draft, not canonical.
- **Does not finalize epistemic citations.** Iter 3 does that.
- **Does not write the public-facing mission.** Iter 5 (optional) does that.
- **Does not pre-empt A3.** A3 conceptual model is the next phase after A1-A2 closes. Inputs to A3 are identified in iter 6 or at iter 4 close.
