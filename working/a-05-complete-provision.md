## The question a designer arrives with
"Carpet softens reverberation and footfall — and it's cheap and comfortable. So why is the item literally named *'where VIS-navigation is maintained'*? Because carpet is the clearest case in the whole set where **one technique serves one functional capacity by harming another** — and best practice is the honest adjudication, not the carpet."

Unlike A-02 (where the naive move backfires acoustically), A-05's tension is *between capacities*. It is a genuinely conditional technique.

## 1 · What carpet actually delivers (performance)

| Effect | Magnitude | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Reverberation absorption | modest — the corpus's own (uncited) carpet figure is **NRC ≈ 0.20–0.35** | **supplementary**, not primary; the ceiling (A-02) does the heavy lifting. The exact value is **unstratified** — `GAP-055` (closed-systemic) already flags A-05's numbers as lacking an evidence stratum, and this figure is one of them. |
| Impact / footfall noise | meaningful reduction | carpet's genuine acoustic strength is *impact* sound, not airborne absorption (general acoustics; model-knowledge, not a graded corpus claim) |
| Whole-body vibration (WBV) | lower than hard floors via underlay damping | floor type is the largest WBV determinant (Larivière et al. 2021, REF-00398, T3). The cross-surface *magnitude* is **not quantified in-corpus** — no multiplier is asserted here. |

Carpet is therefore not an alternative to ceiling treatment for RT60/STI — it is a *supplementary* absorber whose distinctive value is footfall and vibration, and whose cost lands on other capacities.

## 2 · The adjudication — one technique, capacities with opposite signs

This is the spine of the record. Carpet's sign flips depending on which functional capacity you're serving:

| Functional capacity | Carpet's effect | Who it lands on | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Auditory clarity** under reverberation — *autism · dementia · general* | **+ benefit** (supplementary absorption + footfall reduction lowers reverberant/impact load) | autism (sensory load) · dementia (cognitive load) · general intelligibility | in-corpus reasoning; carpet modest vs ceiling |
| **Auditory access — Deaf/HoH specifically** | **not the lever** — the project's `GAP-053` adjudicated carpet a *noise-reduction* item where **Deaf/HoH device-users benefit from amplification, not noise reduction** (GAP-053's own words; personal/soundfield FM is the conventional means) — which is why DEAF is *intentionally not* an A-05 population | Deaf/HoH | `GAP-053` CLOSED-FALSE-POSITIVE (see §7); tension with A-02 flagged, not resolved |
| **Visual wayfinding** | **− harm *if patterned*** — busy / high-contrast flooring patterns can be misread as level changes or hazards, disorienting low-vision and dementia wayfinding; plain + contrasted is a *benefit* | low-vision · dementia | project reasoning-doc names the trade-off; convergent dementia-wayfinding design guidance — REF-00408 (DSDC, T2), REF-00128 (Marquardt, T3), REF-00301 (van Buuren, T3), REF-00094 (PAS 6463, T5) |
| **Mobility — comfort/pain (WBV)** | **+ benefit** — carpet-on-underlay is a *low-vibration* surface (damping); the clean win is **powered / attendant-pushed** users | wheelchair — pain/spinal | T3 (Larivière 2021 REF-00398; Misch & Sprigle 2022 REF-00399) |
| **Mobility — propulsion** | **− cost** — higher rolling resistance, greater upper-limb load, shorter stroke | manual wheelchair users | T3 rolling-resistance + **T1 upper-limb overuse (Sawatzky 2015, REF-00070)** |
| **Air quality / infection** | **− cost** — retains particulates and allergens; harder to clean | MCAS/allergy · immunocompromised · clinical | project reasoning-doc ("carpets retain particulates and allergens"); *specific* pathogen/contraindication claims are **model-knowledge, not corpus-verified** (see §7) |

A **manual** wheelchair user carries the WBV benefit *and* the propulsion cost at once — so even within one person the sign is split. There is no setting where carpet is unconditionally best practice. The design act is choosing **whether**, **where**, and **which carpet** so the served capacity outweighs the harmed ones — and mitigating the harms.

## 3 · The technique — how to do it *right*, when you do it

1. **No busy pattern.** The visual harm is specifically *pattern*, not carpet. Use plain or very-subtle low-contrast field; avoid large-scale, swirled or high-contrast block patterns (misread as level changes). This is the single most important move. *(Design-guidance basis: the dementia-wayfinding sources above; specific hallucinatory misperceptions cited in that literature are illustrative, not corpus-quoted here.)*
2. **Keep the wall–floor contrast.** Non-patterned floor *plus* a clear tonal contrast at the floor/wall junction actively *aids* dementia and low-vision wayfinding — you lose the pattern, keep the boundary cue.
3. **Low-pile, on resilient underlay.** Low-pile balances traction, trip-safety and propulsion; the underlay is what delivers the WBV benefit.
4. **Washable carpet tile in infection-sensitive settings.** Replaceable tiles (not broadloom), impervious backing — lets the acoustic/vibration benefit survive the cleanability requirement. *(Design reasoning; the specific clinical protocol — where carpet is permitted vs contraindicated — is model-knowledge, not a corpus-verified authority. Where infection control is genuinely governing, prefer move 5.)*
5. **Adjudicate by dominant capacity.** Where manual-wheelchair propulsion effort or clinical infection control dominates, *don't* carpet — treat reverberation at the ceiling (A-02) and footfall with resilient hard floors (cushioned vinyl, rubber, cork).

## 4 · Products — manufacturer-neutral

| Option | Serves | Costs | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low-pile loop carpet **tile**, plain, impervious backing | acoustic · footfall · WBV · cleanable | some propulsion resistance | preferred carpet form |
| Broadloom carpet | acoustic · footfall · WBV | cleanability · propulsion | avoid in clinical / high-soiling |
| Cushion-backed vinyl / LVT | footfall · cleanable · low resistance | less airborne absorption | hard-floor compromise where carpet contraindicated |
| Rubber / cork | footfall · WBV · resilient · cleanable | cost | good middle path |

*Carpet is a supplementary absorber (corpus's uncited NRC ≈ 0.20–0.35), not a substitute for ceiling treatment. Without A-02 the RT60/STI target won't be met — specify carpet for footfall and vibration, and treat reverberation at the ceiling.*

## 5 · Failure modes
- **Patterned carpet as a fall/agitation hazard** — busy or high-contrast patterns misread as steps/holes. The named reason the item is conditional.
- **High-pile** — trip risk at thresholds and increased propulsion load.
- **Broadloom in clinical / allergy settings** — infection and air-quality cost with no easy remediation.
- **Carpeting a manual-propulsion-dominant route** — trading a modest acoustic gain for a daily upper-limb cost the user can't opt out of.
- **Treating carpet as the primary acoustic fix** — it isn't; without ceiling treatment (A-02) the RT60/STI target won't be met.
- **Assuming carpet serves Deaf/HoH** — per `GAP-053`, it is not the lever for device-users; amplification (FM/SFA) and primary ceiling treatment are.

## 6 · Functional-capacity map — honest winners and losers

| Population | Net for them | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Autism / dementia / general** (auditory) | **+** with plain low-pile | reverberation + footfall down; *provided* no busy pattern (dementia is also a visual loser if patterned) |
| **Deaf / HoH** (auditory access) | **not served by carpet** | `GAP-053`: noise-reduction item; device-users need amplification, not modest absorption — intentionally not an A-05 population |
| **Low-vision · dementia** (visual) | **− if patterned / + if plain + contrasted** | the whole conditional lives here |
| **Wheelchair — pain/spinal, powered/attendant** (WBV) | **+** | lower vibration than hard floor |
| **Wheelchair — manual propulsion** | **− (and + on WBV at once)** | higher rolling resistance + upper-limb load |
| **MCAS / allergy / immunocompromised** | **−** unless washable tile | retention of allergens/particulates |
