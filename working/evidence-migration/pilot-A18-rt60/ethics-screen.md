## Kawa cultural-transfer flag (L405)

- **The RT60 value is physics-based → transfers universally.** Reverberation time is an acoustic-physics
  parameter; per Kawa doctrine (physics-based sensory parameters transfer universally), the **0.3 s / 0.55 s
  targets carry across cultures without adaptation.** No `kawa-universalized` violation in stating them
  globally.
- **But the spatial-use model does not.** If the provision implies *how* a quiet/low-reverberation space is
  used (a private retreat vs a shared calm space), that social/spatial model is **culturally specific** and
  must be flagged for Person-Mode co-design with cultural context — not universalized. A-18 as written is a
  pure physical target, so this is a note, not a live flag.

## CRPD frame (L383)

CRPD Art. 9 (accessibility) obligates *accessible acoustic environments* but mandates **obligations, not
specifications** — it does not set 0.3 s. The guidebook fills that specification gap and must **not**
present 0.3 s as a legal mandate (S2): it is an evidence-derived best-practice target, offered to help the
reader ask a better question, with the legal floor (codes) shown separately.
