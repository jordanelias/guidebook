§ 1 · Entry

Four doors to the same record

No door is privileged. The guidebook holds one specification record per provision — values, evidence, populations, jurisdictions, conflicts, lived impact — and renders the same data with different section orders and headings depending on which door you came through. Mode is remembered locally; switch any time from the bar above.

Door 1 / spec mode Design professional

"Show me the number and the evidence behind it."

Specifications → Door 2 / question mode Disabled user · advocate

"Does this building work for me?"

Questions → Door 3 / population view OT · allied health

"What does my client's population need?"

Populations → Door 4 / economics Policymaker · procurer

"What does this cost and what does it save?"

Switching between Door 1 and Door 2 is a CSS reordering of the same record — no separate forks. See D-NAV-002 mode toggle decision.

§ 6 · Conventions

How to read this site

Evidence markers are descriptive, not evaluative

● stated   ◐ provisional   ○ pending. No "strong/weak" language. Co-1 (lived experience) evidence is never subordinated to academic tiers; where present it is shown above the tier list.

Jurisdiction matrix uses neutral states

● value present   ○ no requirement   ◌ not searched. Absent does not mean inferior; it means the standard is silent on this provision.

Population pages do not rank

Populations are not sorted by specification count, evidence confidence, or any metric implying a hierarchy of disability. Functional profiles use capacity language ("uses powered wheelchair"), not deficit language.

Conflicts show both parties at parity

Where two populations need different things from the same provision, the page shows both side-by-side. Resolution comes after both parties, not as a verdict over one.

The site is the proof of concept

A guidebook on accessibility must itself meet WCAG 2.2 AA — every drawing carries dimensional alt-text, every facet is keyboard-reachable, meaning is preserved without colour or interactivity, and animations respect reduced-motion preferences. The site is the demonstration; review reports are public.
