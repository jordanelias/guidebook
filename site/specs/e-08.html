§ 1 · Plain language

What this specification says

~30 sec · plainlang_v3
◇ plain-language summary

On any corridor people are expected to use to get somewhere — not a service alley, not a one-way fire egress — leave at least 1200 mm (about 3 ft 11 in) of clear floor between walls, handrails, and fixed obstacles. That is the width at which two power wheelchairs can pass each other without either user backing up, and at which a person walking with a forearm crutch can pass a wheelchair without the metabolic cost of a near-miss.

§ 2 · Why it matters

Width as a participation factor

cohort · n=42

Below 1200 mm, a power-wheelchair user passing another power-wheelchair user must reverse and find a wider point. The metabolic cost of that manoeuvre, measured against open-corridor travel, rises by 30–45 % at 900 mm — the floor still permitted in much retrofit construction. Width at this dimension is therefore not a comfort factor but a participation factor: it determines whether the corridor is a route or an obstacle to be planned around. ⚠ opus · editorial synthesis of Koontz 2017

The threshold is universal — every population coded against this specification benefits, and no population is harmed. Where conflict appears, it is with budget and floor-plate efficiency, not between disabled users. ⚠ opus · editorial framing

§ 3 · Values

The dimension, by design mode

measurement table · 3 modes

Each row is the canonical record for one design mode. Open it for the source query, the evidence behind the number, the discussion that produced it, and any conflict notes. ◆ from data/specifications/e-08.yaml

★ universal Primary circulation routesbetween occupied spaces ≥ 1200 mm · 3′ 11¼″ ›
◆ value

Measured between the nearest faces of any continuous obstruction, from finished floor to 2050 mm AFF. Handrails projecting more than 25 mm from a wall count toward the deduction; recessed grab rails do not.

● evidence

Calibrated to two-power-wheelchair passing observation (Koontz 2017) plus the harmonised ISO/EN baseline.

▣ discussion

1200 mm is the median of stated international requirements. The chair envelope is 700 mm + 100 mm separation tolerance + 700 mm — not exactly summing to 1200 because typical adult power-chair widths including footplate flare are rounded up, and the empirically-recorded minimum near-miss strip is 100 mm.

▲ conflict notes

No cross-population conflict recorded. Trade-off domain is structural / floor-plate efficiency. See .

◐ universal · constrained Secondary routes only≤ 6 m long with turnaround at each end ≥ 1000 mm · 3′ 3⅜″ ›
◆ value

Permitted only where the corridor segment is one-way passage to a single destination (e.g. plant room access) and a turnaround circle of 1500 mm diameter is provided at both ends.

◐ evidence

Provisional. Adopted from CSA B651-18 minimum (1100 mm rounded down) for retrofit feasibility, with stricter end-condition requirements added by the Guidebook editorial group.

▣ discussion

Never permitted on a primary route. The constrained allowance exists for legacy corridors where masonry walls cannot be moved; new construction must meet 1200 mm everywhere two wheelchairs may meet.

▲ conflict notes

Lived-account 0218 records that 1000 mm fails when an obstruction (fire extinguisher, hand-sanitiser stand) sits in the pinch point. Specify all FF&E in this band wall-recessed.

◇ population-specific Where two power chairs are scheduled to passe.g. care-home dining transit, day-hospital corridor MOB ≥ 1500 mm · 4′ 11″ ›
◆ value

Triggered by population MOB primary-role assignment combined with scheduled simultaneous transit. The brief must call this out — the building cannot infer it.

● evidence

NBR 9050 (Brazil) preferred value, IN Harmonised Guidelines minimum, and lived-account corroboration.

  • ◆ Co-1 · acc-0142
▣ discussion

Mode S (person-specific) does not apply: clear width resolves at building level, never at an individual's plan of care.

Plan, section, elevation — and the failure case

Three views of the same corridor. The accessible-feature highlight (blue) marks the protected clear-width zone. The right-hand pane is the failure case — same corridor at 900 mm — at equal weight rather than hidden behind a toggle. Tap any numbered marker to expand its annotation.

① annotation Handrail projectionwhat counts as a wall, what counts as obstruction 50 mm typical ›
◆ definition

1200 mm is measured between the nearest faces of any continuous obstruction. Continuous handrails count even though intermittent in plan, because a wheelchair user cannot pass through them. 50 mm is typical for stainless rails; brass / timber project 60–75 mm.

● source

·

▣ counterexample

Wall-recessed grab rails do not count toward the deduction. Movable equipment (catering trolleys, gurneys at rest) is excluded — measured against design intent, not transient occupancy.

② annotation Passing separationwhy 100 mm and not zero 100 mm tolerance ›
◆ definition

The 100 mm strip between two passing power wheelchairs is the empirical near-miss tolerance recorded by Koontz et al. across mixed-skill drivers. Below 50 mm separation, drivers slow to walking pace; below 30 mm, contact occurs in 12 % of passes.

● source

— cohort observation, n=42, two-pass corridor task. ● Tier 4 — peer-reviewed cohort.

③ annotation Clear-zone definitionfull-height vs. shoulder-height 2050 mm AFF datum ›
◆ definition

"Clear" applies from finished floor to 2050 mm AFF. Above that height, signage, luminaires, HVAC diffusers may project without counting — out of head- and shoulder-strike envelope of any standing or seated user.

▣ threshold

Below 2050 mm AFF, every projection > 25 mm counts and is deducted. Cross-ref: .

§ 8 · Conflicts

Where this provision is contested

3 trade-off domains · 0 cross-population

No cross-population conflict is on record for E-08: every population coded benefits from the wider dimension. Conflict is with non-disability constraints — building footprint, structural grid, retrofit. ⚠ opus · editorial summary of conflict register

▲ FLOOR-PLATENet-to-gross efficiencymore circulation = less programmetrade-off›
◆ nature

Wider corridors increase the circulation share of the floor plate, reducing net-to-gross.

▣ resolution

Locate non-circulation programme adjacent to corridors so the corridor pays for itself in flexibility (Steinfeld & Maisel 2012). Corridor as social space, not waste space.

▲ RETROFIT-WALLExisting structural wallsmasonry that cannot be movedtrade-off›
▣ resolution

Use the constrained allowance (1000 mm · § 3) only for short secondary routes with turnaround at each end; never on primary routes.

▲ FIRE-EGRESSFurniture & fixtures in egress pathsFF&E that creep in post-occupancytrade-off›
▣ resolution

Mark the 1200 mm zone in floor-plate hatching at FF&E review. Specify obstacles > 100 mm to be wall-recessed. Schedule a post-occupancy walk-through against the hatched zone.

CORRIDOR CLEAR WIDTH — PRIMARY ROUTES

Minimum:    1200 mm clear, wall-to-wall less projections.
Constrained: 1000 mm clear permitted only for secondary routes
            ≤ 6 m long with turnaround at each end.
Datum:      Clear envelope from FFL to 2050 mm AFF.
Obstacles:  Any continuous projection > 25 mm deducts from
            the clear width. Wall-recess fire extinguishers,
            hand sanitisers, emergency call stations and any
            FF&E in the corridor zone.
Reference:  Guidebook E-08 · CAN/CSA B651-18 §4.2.1 ·
            BS 8300-2:2018 §10.2 · ISO 21542:2021 §11.5.
