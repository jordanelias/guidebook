# Held Tensions Register

**Status: PROPOSED — pending owner ratification.** Decision wrapper:
`decisions/DR-2026-07-21-held-tensions-register.md`. Public-facing rendering source:
`governance/grounds.md`. This document states doctrine; it executes nothing.

**What this is.** The project's foundational tensions, held deliberately rather than
resolved — each with a declared stance, a disclosed cost, a navigation instrument, and
a standing question. The register does not introduce a new philosophy: it **names the
pattern the canon already practices in at least eight places** (§1) and extends it from
the project's content to the project's foundations. It is second-order transparency:
the mission already requires radical transparency about what the project derives; this
register adds radical transparency about where it stands, how it shows, and for whom.

**Relationship to canon.** Operates under, and never overrides:
`governance/mission-and-epistemics.md` (CANONICAL — including its Test §"Test against
which all downstream decisions are evaluated", its questions-led commitment #6, and its
§Operational reality declarations), `governance/audience-priority.md`,
`governance/population-taxonomy.md` (CANONICAL),
`governance/armature_v4.md` (pre-decision armature), and
`decisions/DR-2026-07-21-product-posture-thinking-tool-not-authority.md` (PROPOSED).
Where this register quotes a stance, the quoted document remains the authority.

## §0 The discipline of holding

A tension is **held**, not abandoned, when four operations are performed on it:

1. **Declare** — the project's working stance and why. The project still adjudicates
   (posture DR: adjudication is required); holding a tension is not both-sides-ism.
   Each entry states what evidence or testimony would move the stance.
2. **Disclose** — what the stance systematically misses. **Anti-fig-leaf rule:** every
   disclosure must carry at least one of (a) a bound (where the limitation does not
   apply), (b) a correction path (how it gets fixed, by whom), or (c) a standing
   question that keeps it open. Disclosure without one of these is confession used as
   license, and is non-compliant with this register.
3. **Navigate** — the tension becomes an instrument the reader can operate: a lens
   toggle, a displayed delta, a stratum label, a parity display. Tensions appear on
   content pages only as instruments, never as essays (progressive disclosure —
   the essays live in `grounds.md`, one click away).
4. **Interrogate** — the tension's standing question becomes literal interface. Per
   mission commitment #6, questions are first-class data; the standing questions in
   §2 are the register's payload and the content model for the questions-led entry
   surface (mission #6; armature entry paths).

## §2 The eight held tensions

Format per entry: **Poles → Stance (declared) → Cost (disclosed, with bound/path) →
Instrument (navigate) → Standing question (interrogate) → Canon anchors.**

### T1 — Universal embodiment vs cultural situatedness

**Poles.** Everybody has a body: vestibular conflict, fatigue, pain, continence,
thermoregulation are species-level facts, which is what lets one guidebook address the
world. And: no body exists outside culture — the same physiology toilets differently
(sitting/squat), prays differently (pew/prostration), lives at chair height or floor
level, on reliable or intermittent grids; the "generic body" in design history has
always been a specific body in disguise.

**Stance.** A stratification discipline: every claim declares its stratum — (i)
invariant mechanism (physiological), (ii) typology-conditional parameter (e.g.
sitting-WC vs squat-WC geometry; grid-dependent vs passive provision), (iii)
culturally situated application — and a stratum-(iii) claim may never wear stratum-(i)
clothing. Universality is claimed for **questions**, not answers: the mission's own
purpose sentence locates the project's value in "the questions they need to ask,"
and answers are graded, situated, and banded.

**Cost.** The corpus is English-built (search-language table: EN-heavy; AR/HI/BN/SW at
zero) and the project specifies for formal construction only — informal-settlement
housing is out of scope, recorded in the mission as "the project's most significant
scope-shaped exclusion." *Bound:* stratum-(i) mechanism claims survive translation;
they are the exportable layer. *Path:* the non-English concept-validation gate on the
functional taxonomy (PR #52 checklist); `term_aliases` / native-alias machinery;
`lang_jur_map` population **deferred pending owner-defined criteria** per
DR-2026-06-11-remove-colonial-role — that DR is this tension's precedent: classify
only under ratified criteria, and correct provenance honestly when wrong.

**Instrument.** Stratum labels on claims; jurisdiction scope chips (armature §4.6);
typology-conditional values rendered as conditions, not exceptions.

**Standing question.** *Which layer is this claim made at — body, building tradition,
or culture — and would it survive translation?*

**Canon.** mission §Methodological boundaries; `jurisdiction-philosophy.md`;
DR-2026-06-11; armature §4.4 (jurisdictional scope dimension).

### T2 — Disability vs function

**Poles.** Disability is a political, legal, historical, identity category — rights,
communities, and anti-discrimination law attach to it; no movement organizes around
"reduced grip strength." Function is a continuum including everyone — the undiagnosed,
the injured, the aging — destigmatizing, but it erases the specificity and politics of
disabled experience.

**Stance.** The armature's, verbatim: two content layers, functional axes and
population identity — *"Neither reduces to the other. Both are first-class"*
(armature §4.2) — with two entry paths of which *"Neither is primary. Neither is
fallback"* (§4.3). PR #52 (`functional-taxonomy.md`) is the data-model execution of
this stance; the armature's mapping-confidence spectrum (high → minimal-predictive)
is the honesty mechanism that keeps diagnosis-to-function mapping a *navigation
hypothesis, not a prescription* (armature §5.2).

**Cost.** Any schema privileges some queries; axis-entry must be proven equal in
evidence depth to population-entry (armature §4.3 names reverse retrieval as
architecturally significant). Function-pole capture shows up as deficit language;
medical-pole capture shows up as diagnosis-gating. *Path:* dual-entry parity is a
testable build requirement; diagnosis-optional membership is doctrine (taxonomy §5);
the two adversarial passes of 2026-07-21 are the standing audit form for capture.

**Instrument.** The disability ↔ function lens toggle (population entry ↔ axis entry);
mapping-confidence disclosure at every population pre-fill.

**Standing question.** *Am I asking what this body needs here, or what this community
knows — and can I ask both?*

**Canon.** armature §§4.2–4.3, 5.2–5.3; `population-taxonomy.md` §3.4 (codes step
aside at Person Mode); mission #1.

### T3 — Frameworks vs lived experience

**Poles.** Frameworks (ICF, tiers, capability theory) generalize, compare, scale, and
justify budgets; testimony is first-person, situated, authoritative about itself.
Framework without witness sees the world at the coarseness of its codes; witness
without framework cannot adjudicate contradictory testimony or scale.

**Stance.** Co-1 is co-primary with Tier 1 — already encoded (`tier: 1,
evidence_type: co1`; mission #3), so lived-experience evidence anchors full-strength
(●) claims; convergence with clinical evidence is itself evidence; divergence is
documented, never suppressed. Testimony is curated *as testimony*: the `situations`
entity (PR #52 §5) holds first-person, language-carrying, episode-level accounts as a
peer attachment point that axis decomposition can reference but never replace.

**Cost.** The verification pipeline is DOI-keyed; knowledge that circulates as gray
PDFs, oral testimony, or community practice — disproportionately Global-South and
disproportionately from populations medicine dismissed — fails intake before any
tier doctrine applies. Populations disbelieved by medicine have thin Tier-1
literatures *because* they were disbelieved; unguarded, the bands reproduce that
injustice in print. *Bound:* Co-1's tier-1 encoding means the guard exists at the
doctrine level. *Path:* the gray/oral/DPO intake route (taxonomy §5, PR #52); the
mission's post-launch participation commitment — "to be answered with disabled
people, not for them" — with its honest calibration that this may never activate.

**Instrument.** Situations attach to items and profiles and render beside
specifications; provenance chips distinguish `dpo_research` from `advocacy_position`
from clinical sources; evidence-state cells distinguish absence-of-evidence from
evidence-of-absence (mission, Cochrane yardstick).

**Standing question.** *What does the framework see that no witness can — and what did
the witness see that no framework can?*

**Canon.** mission #3, §Operational reality; `co1-operational.md`; evidence-state
machine; armature §6 (transparency obligations).

### T4 — Categorization of people, disabilities, functions

**Poles.** Every category cuts someone at its edges; classification is administrative
power (certification regimes, benefit gates) and categories loop back to reshape the
people they name. And: without categories there is no findability, no evidence
aggregation, no rights class — to curate at all is to categorize.

**Stance.** Categories are held as **revisable instruments with disclosed costs**:
"an organizing convenience, not a definition of people" (`population-taxonomy.md`
§1.1); "a person is not defined by their disabilities" (project-standards, quoted
therein). Every category carries provenance (who authored it), contestation (who
disputes it), a visibility claim (what it finds), a blindness claim (what it hides),
and — symmetrically, per the 2026-07-21 adversarial pass — conditions under which it
would be dissolved. Established categories are not furniture while new ones audition.

**Cost.** The looping effect is not fully controllable: naming `VES` or `LCOV` will
shape search, evidence, and possibly identity. Codes assigned without ratified
criteria caused the COLONIAL error. *Path:* category changes route through DR with
owner ratification (population-taxonomy §5 process; DR-2026-06-11 precedent);
mapping-confidence documents where a category does *not* predict function (armature
§4.2: IntD minimal-predictive — the diagnostic label "does not usefully predict a
functional-axis profile").

**Instrument.** Category provenance chips; dissolution conditions in the axis/profile
registers; the validator's category rules (population-taxonomy §4).

**Standing question.** *Who made this box, who is cut by its edges, and what would I
fail to see without it?*

**Canon.** `population-taxonomy.md` §§1, 5; armature §§4.2, 5.1–5.3; DR-2026-06-11.

### T5 — The stakeholder chain: lived experience / advocacy / policy / design / practice

**Poles.** Five epistemic communities with different validity criteria: what happened
to me (witness); what my community strategically demands (advocate — motivated by
function, not flaw; the schema already distinguishes `dpo_research` from
`advocacy_position`); what survives review and budget (policy); what my client will
accept by Friday (designer); what this client's body can do (practitioner). "Best
practice" means five different things across these chairs.

**Stance.** The armature's: *"Role does not change the underlying data"* (§4.5) — it
changes emphasis, register, and framing; five roles including **carer as a distinct
role, not a proxy for the disabled person**. Extended by this register: each role's
rendering rules are *public* — every door states what it foregrounds and what it
systematically underweights (the `weighting_profile` table, rendered to readers).
Audience conflicts resolve per `audience-priority.md`, whose primary audiences are
designers *and disabled people* jointly.

**Cost.** The author sits in none of the five chairs: pre-launch solo synthesis,
declared (mission §Operational reality). Underweighting disclosure is itself authored
by the same solo process it discloses. *Path:* audience-priority's conflict-resolution
rules; post-launch consultation commitment; the advocacy-brief use-pattern carrying
the code-vs-evidence delta so the advocacy chair gets its native product.

**Instrument.** Door-epistemology disclosure rendered from `weighting_profile`;
role-switching preserved on every record (armature: same specification, different
register — including the plain-language translations of ●/○ in armature §4.7).

**Standing question.** *Which chair am I reading from, and what does this chair make
me unable to see?*

**Canon.** `audience-priority.md`; armature §4.5, §4.7; `weighting_profile`.

### T6 — Paternalism/prescription vs dignity/inclusion

**Poles.** To specify environments *for* people is to speak for them — and the
populations most specified-for are those whose own testimony institutions discount,
which is both why specification protects and how it silences. Dignity requires risk
and choice; protection requires constraint; "safety" is the historical alibi of the
institutions disability rights fought. The dementia loop-plan and the anti-ligature
ward are the same tension in plan and in hardware.

**Stance.** Prescribe **capability floors, not conduct** — what must be *possible*,
never what people must do. The mission already holds the load-bearing pieces: the
guidebook "teaches professional judgment, it does not substitute for it" (#7);
Person Mode's answer "may fall outside" the population range — "that population
neither bounds nor defines the person" (#4); the posture DR declines to bind.
Extended by this register: provisions that trade agency for safety are flagged as
exactly that trade, with who-decided named.

**Cost.** A specification corpus normalizes; even capability floors encode an
author's model of a good environment. *Bound:* the floor/aspiration split keeps
compliance and vision separate. *Path:* Co-1 governance where testimony diverges from
protection logic; the dignity-of-risk flag; OT co-design as the individual-case
resolver.

**Instrument.** Agency-safety trade flags; Person-Mode handoff notes naming the
functional parameter and assessment range (armature §4.6); "questions to raise with
your architect / your OT" generated per record (armature §4.7).

**Standing question.** *Does this provision expand what the person can choose, or
what others can prevent?*

**Canon.** mission #4, #7; posture DR; armature §4.6–4.7.

### T7 — Universal vs population-specific vs individual design

**Poles.** One-environment-for-all meets opposite-signed real specifications (the
corpus's own flagship: C-04 high LRV contrast for VIS vs C-05 low LRV differential
for DEM). Population-specific optimizes and segregates. Individual fit is perfect,
unscalable, and dies with the next occupant.

**Stance.** The three Design Modes (mission #4) as an **order of interrogation**, not
a resolution: Universal = the code floor; Population = where the evidence hierarchy
does its substantive ranking work; Person = OT resolution unbounded by population.
The residue — population-vs-population and individual-vs-population conflicts — is
displayed at parity with the strategies menu, never silently resolved.

**Cost.** Parity display transfers the resolution burden to the reader; some readers
want an answer. *Bound:* the strategies menu and worked examples teach resolution
method. *Path:* conflict-domain research (population-taxonomy §3.5: 12 conflict
domains, not 110 pairs); the empty `conflicts` table is the named backlog.

**Instrument.** Parity display; conflict-domain index; mode chips on every value.

**Standing question.** *Who is the "everyone" this design claims, and who becomes the
exception it manages?*

**Canon.** mission #4, #5; Part 5; population-taxonomy §3.5.

### T8 — Best practice as-exists vs as-possible

**Poles.** "Highest held standard in practice" is shippable, precedented, and
ratifies the ceiling of the least-ambitious compliant building. "Highest possible per
research/advocacy" is evidence-led, unbuilt, and dismissible as utopian.

**Stance.** Publish **both numbers and the delta**. The machinery already exists and
is hereby named as this tension's instrument set: code consensus anchors only at the
weak band, flagged (mission #2, Option A amendment — "the jurisdictions may be wrong
together"); PMP walks measure the signed gap between stated thresholds and empirical
ceilings (`pmp_gap_signed`); the advocacy use-pattern foregrounds the delta as its
native product. The delta is not an embarrassment to be minimized; it is the
innovation and advocacy frontier, stated per item.

**Cost.** Demonstrated-in-practice values depend on POE evidence the corpus holds
thinly; the "possible" number can outrun costing. *Path:* case-study compendium
grounds "done"; economics volume grounds feasibility; strength bands grade "shown."

**Instrument.** Practice-value / possible-value / delta rendering; exemplar links;
band markers with plain-language translation.

**Standing question.** *Is this number the best anyone has done, or the best anyone
has shown — and what lives in the gap?*

**Canon.** mission #2, #5; DR-2026-07-20; DR-2026-07-21 Option A; PMP; economics Part 11.

## §4 Governance bindings (if ratified)

1. **No session may resolve a held tension.** Collapsing either pole — in prose,
   schema, or rendering — requires a DR amending this register. The two 2026-07-21
   adversarial passes stand as the audit form for detecting silent capture.
2. **The anti-fig-leaf rule is enforceable.** A disclosure without bound, path, or
   question is a finding at doctrine-recheck.
3. **Instruments, not essays, on content pages.** Grounds prose renders only in the
   grounds section; content pages carry the instruments (§0.3).
4. **The standing questions are the questions-led entry surface's seed content**
   (mission #6; B3 navigation), instantiated per item and per situation.
5. **This register is evaluated under the mission Test** — all eight Test questions
   apply to the register itself, and the register fails its own standard if it ever
   functions as a license rather than a bind.

## §5 What this document does not do

It does not amend the mission, the audience priority, the population taxonomy, or the
armature — it cites them as authorities and surfaces divergences for ratification. It
does not resolve the three-representation taxonomy divergence (§3a) — PR #52 proposes,
the owner ratifies. It does not create the public page — `grounds.md` is the render
source, Phase E renders it. It does not substitute for lived-experience review — it
records (T3, T5) why that review is structurally absent pre-launch and what its
arrival would change.
