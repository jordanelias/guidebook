learned_rules:
  - The formal/informal divide is the meta-barrier to accessible housing globally. The guidebook cannot resolve this through specification — it is a policy, land tenure, and infrastructure problem. The guidebook must name it explicitly.
  - ISO wayfinding specifications (23599, 21542) are physically determined and transfer universally. This is distinct from all other Phase 1 topics where specifications require adaptation. The wayfinding gap is implementation and maintenance, not design.
  - PAS 6463 sensory space physical principles (acoustic, lighting, visual complexity) are not culturally variable. The spatial model (private individual retreat) is culturally variable. These must be separated in the guidebook — specify the physics, flag the spatial model for co-design.
  - The Sejong Toilet (Indonesia) is the only documented accessible squat toilet innovation globally. It is Co-1/Tier 1 but unpublished and cannot serve as a specification source. This is the most significant single-topic design gap identified in the entire Global South evidence programme.
  - CRPD treaty-body evidence operates at a different level than design evidence. It does not specify — it mandates. The guidebook occupies the specification gap that treaty bodies leave unfilled. This relationship should be stated in Part 1.
