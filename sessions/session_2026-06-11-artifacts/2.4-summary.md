## What changed
`scripts/validate_evidence_state.py` previously validated only YAML files in `data/evidence-states/` (which don't exist → it returned 0) and scraped gaps from `gap_register.md`. 2.4 wires it to the **real DB tables** from 2.3 (the canonical source under DB-as-truth):

- **pending⇒gap-link** enforced against the **`gaps` table** (not the markdown scrape).
- **Scale-aware:** `design_scale ∈ {universal, population, person}`.
- **Directness-aware (§1.7):** a `discounted` source cannot also anchor the claim; `convergent` requires ≥2 evidence axes, `single_axis` ≤1; `divergent` requires rationale + synthesis_approach.
- State-field rules mirror `EvidenceStateRecord` at the data layer (provisional⇒confidence flag, not_applicable⇒rationale, stated/provisional⇒convergence).
- The empty-table case now reports a clean PASS instead of "no data files."

## Why these checks matter
They are exactly the conditional, cross-field rules SQLite CHECK/FK **cannot** express. CHECK covers the enum/boolean/single-column constraints; FK covers item/population/gap existence; the validator covers the state-dependent requirements and the directness coherence.
