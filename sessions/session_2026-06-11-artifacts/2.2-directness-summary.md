## What it does
`schemas/directness.py` implements the consolidation §1.7 names by reference. Directness is a **conditioning layer over the single ladder** (`tier_derivation.py`), not a per-scale hierarchy. Three structured dimensions replace the scattered primitives + free-text notes:

| Dimension | Consolidates | Grades |
|---|---|---|
| population-directness | `evidence_population_match.match_grade` | EXACT / PARTIAL / PROXY / MISMATCH |
| value-directness | `reasoning_doc_citations.value_match` (+ `spec_value_probes.passes_strict` corroboration) | EXACT / WITHIN-TOLERANCE / NOT-FOUND / CONTRADICTED (PAYWALL→ungradeable) |
| **scale-directness (NEW)** | grain-match between evidence grain and claim scale | DIRECT / ADJACENT / DOWN-WEIGHTED / NON-ANCHORING |

**Scale-directness** is the genuinely new axis — §1.7 bidirectional grain-match, §1.6 mode-asymmetric:
- AGGREGATE (SR/meta) × population = DIRECT; × person = DOWN-WEIGHTED (too coarse for the individual); × universal = ADJACENT.
- SPECIFIC (T1 study) × person = DIRECT; × population = DIRECT unless it generalizes beyond what it measured; × universal = ADJACENT.
- CODE × universal = DIRECT (the floor); × population/person = **NON-ANCHORING** (convergence-not-evidence, §1.6/§3).

`consolidate(...)` combines the three into one **categorical** conditioning grade (DIRECT / DOWN-WEIGHTED / DISCOUNTED / NON-ANCHORING) — not a number, per the no-false-precision stance. `directness_from_primitives(...)` is the single entry point from the raw DB primitives; `check_directness_record(...)` is the audit/vocabulary check (mirrors `check_tier_consistency`).
