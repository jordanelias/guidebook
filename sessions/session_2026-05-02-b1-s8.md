### Selection rationale (4-step Rule 3 walk)

**Step 1: A eliminated.** A scores 2/2/2 on the three weight-3 doctrinal criteria. Adaptation cost (19–29 sessions) closes the quantitative gap but leaves the corpus structure as markdown+YAML with custom Python query code — accumulating project-specific technical debt over 12+ year horizon. Doctrine should be embodied by the form, not adapted to.

**Step 2: B and C both natively satisfy doctrine.** Selection becomes operational, not doctrinal.

**Step 3: B-vs-C Cluster IX gap is structural and recurring.** B's 48% vs C's 33% reflects three distinct recurring costs: (a) CI tractability (SQLite build step vs JVM+Jena+SHACL toolchain); (b) solo-author operability (SQL broadly known vs SPARQL+SHACL+ontology specialized); (c) A10/A12/A13 integration (mechanical SQL migration vs ontology-design methodological work). These costs recur every working session, every CI run, every contributor onboarding for 12+ years.

**Step 4: Recurring cost vs structural advantage tradeoff.** C's structural advantages on Clusters I/VIII/X (named graphs, RDFS subclass, W3C standardization; +6 weighted points) are real but lower-frequency. The 3-weighted-point C-over-B gap is technically correct under the framework's weighting but understates the recurring-cost reality. D-03's "solo-only-permanent is a possible end-state" intensifies the recurring-cost argument: a 2038 solo maintainer is more likely to know SQL than SPARQL with no other priors.
