### A11 Session 1 — Legal and regulatory framework (governance-only)

**governance/legal-regulatory.md** (CANONICAL with §3.3 provisional pending counsel, NEW): 8 sections + change log. Operationalises mission L137–143 ("not a substitute for professional judgment", "not a legal authority", "does not pursue universal jurisdictional adoption", "pre-launch single-author synthesis") into legal/regulatory posture. Pattern is governance-only per roadmap §4 A11; counsel review is external and has its own clock.

**§1 teaches-not-prescribes posture** (final): the guidebook teaches accessible-design judgment; it does not prescribe legal conformance. Operative meaning: a reader following a guidebook recommendation is exercising informed judgment, not relieving themselves of code-conformance duty. Distinguishes from "just opinions" (the synthesis carries epistemic weight via A6 evidence-state machine and A5 Co-1 priority) and from "nothing is mandatory" (statutory codes remain mandatory regardless of guidebook framing).

**§2 liability framing** (final): four risk vectors named with framework mitigations — misuse-of-recommendation, adversarial-citation (V-08), currency, mis-jurisdiction. Three vectors named as outside-framework — deliberate fraud, third-party misappropriation (handled by licensing), off-label classification use (handled by V-03). Author posture: best-effort synthesis with declared limits; reader use constructed-as-informed.

**§3 front-matter disclaimer structure** (provisional pending counsel): five required elements (not legal authority; not professional advice; synthesis-state transparency; currency notice; jurisdictional scope). §3.3 ships full provisional disclaimer wording covering all five elements explicitly. §3.4 declares the wording subject to counsel review and specifies that releases through counsel-completion ship with the provisional notice.

**§4 licensing model** (final): **CC BY-SA 4.0** selected with reasoning. Permits derivatives, translations, adaptations, and commercial use; requires attribution and share-alike (derivatives must use the same licence — prevents proprietary repackaging). Carve-outs documented for cited Co-1 sources (retain original licensing), standards/codes (cited under fair dealing/fair use, not relicensed), and per-asset image licensing.

**§5 trajectory positioning** (final): **decline-pursuit / accept-invitation**. Rules out active pursuit of jurisdictional adoption and any certification/credentialing system. Permits responsive engagement with regulators who initiate contact. Permits independent referencing by code-writing bodies. Reasoning: active pursuit would create stake in formal enactment that would distort synthesis; the project's pre-launch resource state cannot conduct sustained organisational engagement credibly. Affects voice (no positioning as "the standard"), CS coverage (no new regulator-engagement thread), A12 decision-capture scope (no proactive decisions to record).

**§6 counsel review queue** (open): 10 questions L-Q1 through L-Q10. Severity tiers: 4 blocking (L-Q1 V-08 defence, L-Q2 disclaimer-element completeness, L-Q7 author-vs-project naming, L-Q8 jurisdiction-specific publishing requirements); 4 major (L-Q3 liability-formulation alternatives, L-Q4 CC BY-SA 4.0 enforceability, L-Q5 fair-use/dealing framing for citations, L-Q6 trajectory-disclosure phrasing); 2 minor (L-Q9 data-protection for cited authors, L-Q10 review cadence). Counsel response is filed as a separate document (`governance/legal-regulatory-counsel-review-{YYYY-MM-DD}.md`).

**§7 cross-stage dependencies + review cadence** (final): §7.1 closes A10 V-08 mitigation pathway — V-08 release-gate review status transitions from `escalate` (no disclaimer) to `cleared_with_notes` (disclaimer present, counsel pending), and on counsel completion to `cleared`. §7.4 review-cadence triggers: major release; jurisdictional accessibility-law change in any of the 24 covered jurisdictions; material methodology change; adversarial-use catalogue extension touching legal/regulatory matter. §7.5 status table records the seven decisions and their next-action state.
