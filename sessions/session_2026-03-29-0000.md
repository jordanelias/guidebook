## Session Narrative

This session produced CO-0004, the most significant structural change order to date. Starting from a review of CO-0003 and the Part 8 structural proposal, the author identified three cascading insights:

1. **Parts 3 and 4 are two sides of the same methodology** — co-occurrence framework and synthesis/sequencing both teach "how to combine provisions." Merged into new Part 3.

2. **Volumes I and II are an artificial split** — the theory/practice divide doesn't reflect how architects use the guidebook. Merged into single Volume I.

3. **Reading order must match reference order** — matrices reference items, so items precede matrices. Building-wide conflicts reference items and rooms, so they follow both. Room-level conflicts resolve inline in matrices.

4. **Most connections are synergies, not conflicts** — building-wide system conflicts (spanning rooms, whole-building systems) are the only co-occurrence content that justifies a standalone Part. Room-level conflicts belong inline in matrix annotations.
