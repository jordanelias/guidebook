## 1. CODE-VALUE GENEALOGY CHAIN

**The only genealogy claim in the corpus** is in `references/bpc/frameworks-and-methodology/manoeuvring-footprint-vs-turning-radius-methodology.md` §3 ("The convergence-not-evidence trap"):

> "The 1500 mm 'turning circle' appears across the majority of accessibility codes worldwide. The convergence is not evidence — it is downstream from a small set of 1970s anthropometric studies, principally the US Veterans Administration HUD studies that produced ANSI A117.1-1980 and its derivatives." … "The 1500 mm value is **the regulatory floor in 24 jurisdictions and the empirical adequate value in none of them** for the contemporary user population."

## 2. SWEPT-PATH vs ABSTRACT TURNING RADIUS

Core file: `references/bpc/frameworks-and-methodology/manoeuvring-footprint-vs-turning-radius-methodology.md` (OPERATIVE, closes GAP-272, owner directive "turning radius is disingenuous compared to swept path/turning manoeuvres").

Four grounds: (1) **No pivot-in-place** — REF-00736 Chaikhot et al. 2023 (T1, VERIFIED): 97% spin turns (axis at inner wheel, asymmetric footprint, outer wheel ~1.4m arc); 3% roll turns. Caveat: participants were 10 able-bodied novices. Authorship-correction history (mis-recorded "Vergara 2023", corrected 2026-05-25; GAP-272 closure text still says "Vergara"). (2) **Forward-swept geometry excluded** — footrests project 250–400mm (manual) / 500mm+ (power, elevating legrests); patent US 7,182,166. (3) **Drive-system heterogeneity** — MWD ≈ pivot; RWD front sweeps wide; FWD fishtails rearward (BS 8300-2:2018 annex + Steinfeld 2010). (4) **Manoeuvre type matters** — median daily turn 39°, IQR 24–67° (Sonenblum 2012, PMID 22377684); 180° enclosed bay **2400mm** entire-sample (Steinfeld 2006 RESNA, REF-00737 T3) vs open-ended bay **1925mm** UDI sub-sample (source's own caveat, audit L83: "ends of turn open, allowing participants to lengthen out their turn and reduce its width").

QAS value-walk (audit L60–75): stepped searches 2000→2700mm; only Tier 3+ hit = 2400mm; verbatim: *"IDEA and BS8300 results demonstrated that turning area clearances would have to be increased to 2400 mm (94.5 in.) compared to the current 1500-1525 mm (60 in.) to accommodate their entire samples."* Corpus previously selectively cited the smaller 1925mm figure (GAP-266, OPEN).

Audit F-X4 (L428–456): scooters are Ackermann-steered — no pivot, multi-point turn always required; T-shaped space (ADA §304.3.2) never surfaced in BPCs; Steinfeld values are "empirical swept-path measurements (3D digital probe + video, n=275 IDEA + n=50 UDI)… not idealized turning circles" (note: n=50 UDI is the 1925mm cohort).

Biomechanical externality (BPC §6): inner-hand braking force 15.3±15.7× straight-line; impulse 4.5±1.7×; ~900 turns/day; manual-user upper-limb injury prevalence 55–72% (Boninger). "A 1525 mm corridor isn't merely cramped; it forces the inferior turning manoeuvre repeatedly… for the user's entire wheelchair-using lifetime."

Parameter deprecation (BPC §5): "turning circle/radius/space" deprecated → "manoeuvring footprint"/"swept envelope" qualified by manoeuvre type + drive system; "1500mm turning circle" citable only as "code minimum (Tier 6 — regulatory floor…)".

Other anchors: REF-00073 Ringaert et al. 2001 (T1, VERIFIED; supersession: "24-year-old foundational… current best by absence of replacement"); D'Souza et al. 2011 (T3) via `references/bpc/population-general/upper-limb-impairment-built-environment.md` L41: turning ≥2100mm standard / ≥2500mm new-build primary circulation. Koontz manoeuvring-envelope work NOT in corpus (only propulsion papers). GAP-295 (OPEN, P2): Steinfeld 2010 base 15 years stale.
