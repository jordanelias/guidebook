## Context

A 2026-07-21 session interrogated the research taxonomy and was then audited
adversarially twice (once generally, once from lived-experience and global
non-English standpoints). The critiques and the canon converged on one finding: the
project's foundational tensions — universality vs situatedness, disability vs
function, frameworks vs testimony, categorization, the stakeholder chain, protection
vs dignity, universal vs individual design, practice vs possibility — are not
resolvable, and every attempt to resolve one silently (including this session's own
taxonomy draft) produced a documented failure. Meanwhile the canon already *holds*
tensions deliberately in at least eight mechanisms (parity conflicts, strength bands,
design modes, role-relative rendering, Co-1 co-primacy, code-as-floor-plus-delta,
declared partial Art. 4.3, adjudicate-not-bind). The owner directed that this pattern
be named, generalized, and made public.

## Decision (proposed)

1. Adopt `governance/held-tensions.md` as doctrine: **nine held tensions** (T1–T8
   pole-vs-pole; **T9 — generality vs. irreducible individuality — the meta-tension**
   that conditions T2/T3/T4/T7 and governs how the entire functional taxonomy is
   read: no two people are the same, function is a spectrum along a spectrum, and
   measured function is never independent of selfhood — the shared claim of the
   project's own Kawa, ICF capacity/performance, and Capability frameworks), the four
   operations (declare / disclose / navigate / interrogate), the anti-fig-leaf rule
   (every disclosure carries a bound, correction path, or standing question), the
   unification map, and the governance bindings — including: no session may resolve
   a held tension without a DR; instruments-not-essays on content pages; the standing
   questions seed the questions-led entry surface (mission commitment #6).
2. Adopt `governance/grounds.md` as the public-facing render source ("How This
   Project Thinks"), rendering at Phase E with the placement and per-page
   instruments specified in its §R — the "for whom" half of radical transparency:
   per-role rendering rules disclosed to readers, from `weighting_profile`.
3. Record, without resolving, the three-representation taxonomy divergence
   (DB `populations` 22 flat codes / `population-taxonomy.md` 11+sub codes /
   PR #52 axes×profiles) as an item the amended PR #52 reconciliation must close.

## Alternatives rejected

- **Resolve each tension** — every resolution collapses to a pole whose failure mode
  is documented (universalism → colonial neutrality; situatedness → unshippable
  relativism; function-only → erasure; disability-only → diagnostic gatekeeping; etc.).
- **Philosophy essay without bindings** — decoration; fails the mission Test #1 and
  the anti-displacement discipline (governance work must bind sessions or not exist).
- **Do nothing** — the session record itself demonstrates the cost: repeated
  oscillation between critique and counter-critique with no standing answer, the
  precise failure mode the posture DR was written to end at the product level.

## Consequences if ratified

Sessions gain a standing answer to foundational challenges (end of re-litigation);
the public site gains a grounds section that converts the project's structural
limitations (solo, AI-leveraged, English-built, pre-launch) from discreditable
secrets into disclosed, bounded, correctable properties; Door 2 / questions-led
navigation gains its seed content model; the sixteen taxonomy amendments (PR #52)
acquire their principled basis — each maps to a register tension rather than to an
ad-hoc critique.
