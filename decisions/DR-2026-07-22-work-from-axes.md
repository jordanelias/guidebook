## Context — the failure this closes

Across session 2026-07-21/22, asked to interrogate and improve the population codes
"from best practices in ethics and disability advocacy," the agent repeatedly did the
opposite of what the two-layer taxonomy exists to do: it reached for broad, "inclusive-
sounding" **umbrellas** that in fact **erase specific communities and collapse opposed
design demands** — the pre-taxonomy category-thinking §1.3 and the irreducibility
principle were written to end. Three self-caught instances, each owner-flagged:

1. **`OFS`→"energy-limiting chronic illness" (ELCI).** Erased the *orthostatic/autonomic*
   mechanism entirely and lumped three distinct conditions/communities — POTS
   (orthostatic), ME/CFS (exertional/PEM), MCAS (chemical/immune) — under one fatigue
   umbrella. The axis layer already distinguished them (POTS→STA+THR+BAL, CFS→STA,
   MCAS→CHM). The "improvement" was *worse* than the status quo, which at least kept
   `OFS/POTS`, `OFS/ME`, `OFS/MCAS`.
2. **Respiratory/pulmonary dismissed as "decomposed"** into fatigue + air-quality —
   but dyspnea ≠ fatigue and respiratory reactivity ≠ MCAS chemical sensitivity; the
   armature (§5, R8) had flagged it as a genuine axis candidate with concrete spatial
   consequences.
3. **`MOB`→"physically disabled / wheelchair users."** Lumped opposed demands
   (powered-chair vs ambulatory-with-pain vs little person vs limb-difference), used
   the wheelchair-symbol exemplar that erases the *majority* (ambulatory disabled) and
   the *part-time wheelchair user* (the very dynamic-disability population erased in
   instance 1 — erased twice), and buried the orthogonal upper-limb/reach axis.

The structural cause: treating the profile layer as *buckets of people* (category-
thinking) rather than *specific self-identified communities*, and generating those
buckets **from an armchair rather than with the communities** (a co-production failure;
T3/T9). In every instance the axis layer already held the distinctions the umbrella
destroyed.

## Decision — the discipline

**Work from axes.** Population/profile curation, demand-mapping, and specification
proceed FROM the functional axes (Layer 1) — specific, architect-actionable,
diagnosis-neutral, co-occurrence-native — never FROM broad population/profile
umbrellas. Concretely:

- The **profile layer is populated only with specific self-identified communities on
  their own terms** (co-produced). It is never a place to coin lumped coalition codes.
- **Broad umbrellas are forbidden as profile codes.** "Physically disabled" is a valid
  self-chosen coalition identity and a useless design code; "energy-limiting chronic
  illness" is a valid advocacy umbrella and a destructive replacement for POTS/ME/MCAS.
  Umbrellas may exist only as **additive cross-cutting tags** over specifics, never as
  replacement codes.
- A population is a **mapping-confidence-weighted vector over axes** (§3.2), not a
  bucket.
- **The impulse to reach for a population umbrella is the signal to STOP and decompose
  to axes** — and, where the question is *which* communities/terms are right, to defer
  to community self-definition rather than decide it from an armchair.

## Consequences

Future population work (adding CP, splitting Deaf-cultural, reopening the orthostatic
and respiratory axis questions, the reconciliation in `DR-2026-07-22-population-
representation-reconciliation`) proceeds axis-first; the guardrail is a session-start
trip-hazard (`CLAUDE.md` §10) and an operative rule (`project-standards.md`) so it
re-surfaces where it bites. No existing data changes.

## Not decided here

Which specific communities/terms populate the profile layer (co-production, later);
whether orthostatic and respiratory warrant their own axes/facets (reopened as
separate axis questions — the honest correction to instances 1–2, to be worked
axis-first, not umbrella-first).
