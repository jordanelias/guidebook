## Scope Clarification

This slug covers the **broader hearing loss population** — hard of hearing (non-signing), age-related hearing loss (presbycusis), hearing aid and CI users in general built environment, acoustic design for speech intelligibility across typologies, visual and vibrotactile alerting systems, and environmental noise reduction as accessibility intervention. It complements but does not duplicate `deaf-spatial-design` (signing space, sightlines, corridor widths) and `deaf-acoustic-built-environment` (hearing loops, RT60, STI, Auracast). Cross-references to those slugs are noted where overlap exists.

## Key Findings So Far

### Consensus (cross-jurisdiction)

1. **Hearing loop / assistive listening provision** is mandated or recommended in all searched jurisdictions for assembly spaces, service counters, and reception areas. IEC 60118-4 is the universal performance standard. (Already in deaf-acoustic BPC — CROSS-REF)
2. **Visual fire alerting** (strobe) is required in all searched jurisdictions. ADA/NFPA 72 sets flash rate 1–3 Hz; strobe <5 Hz; hardwired with battery backup. Vibrotactile (bed shaker) is more effective than strobe for sleeping individuals.
3. **Acoustic treatment** for speech intelligibility is specified in BS 8300-2:2018 (UK) and DIN 18041:2016 (DE). DIN 18041 provides the most granular targets: RT60 reduced by 20% for hearing-impaired users; background noise ≤30–45 dB by room type.
4. **Social model framing** is explicit in BS 8300-2:2018 revision: "the environment is the disabling factor, not the individual."
5. **Loop maintenance** is a mandated requirement in BS 8300-2:2018 — reactive and preventative.
6. **Auracast / Bluetooth LE Audio** is emerging as a DAR complement to hearing loops (not a replacement). HLAA ICAAT monitoring. (Already in deaf-acoustic BPC — CROSS-REF)

### Divergent / Not Yet Resolved

1. **Residential alerting systems** — no cross-jurisdiction consensus on mandatory visual/vibrotactile alerting in dwellings (as distinct from public buildings). ADA covers public; residential varies by jurisdiction.
2. **Workplace acoustic standards** — DIN 18041 + VDI 2569 (DE) is the most developed framework; UK has BS 8300 guidance but less granular room-type targets.
3. **Age-related hearing loss** — WHO estimates 1/3 of over-65s have debilitating hearing loss; design implications for ageing-in-place housing not yet systematically retrieved.

## Concept Boundary Warnings

- **DE:** DIN 18041 uses *Hörsamkeit* (a concept without direct EN equivalent — "audibility quality of a room for speech communication"). Do not flatten to "acoustics" — preserve native concept.
- **ZH/JA:** Hearing loss built environment evidence is primarily in rehabilitation medicine journals, not architecture/building science. Search strategy must cross disciplines.
- **NO:** TEK17 *universell utforming* bundles spatial + acoustic provisions — search as whole, log under both deaf-acoustic and hearing-impairment slugs.
