## 4. Hardware Type Requirements (universal across jurisdictions)

All 24 jurisdictions require or recommend lever handles or equivalent. Round doorknobs are non-compliant in every jurisdiction surveyed. Key requirements across all:

- Operable with one hand
- No tight grasping, pinching, or wrist twisting required
- Operable with closed fist (US/UK/AU best practice)
- Push-type, lever, or U-shaped handles acceptable

FR adds: "laissant tomber la main" (falling hand operation) — handles actuated by downward hand pressure only.

SG adds: "Door knob is not recommended as it does not provide adequate grip for persons with impaired hand functions."

## 7. Best-Practice Synthesis (Draft)

### Most inclusive provision
**Zero-threshold (Nullschwelle) doctrine** as practised in Germany (DIN 18040) and Singapore (BCA Code). All doorways level. No threshold height permitted. Where technically unavoidable (weather sealing, fire rating): maximum 20mm with full-width bevel, automatically closing weather seal, or flush sill with drainage channel behind.

### Most targeted provision
**Three-parameter specification:**
1. **Threshold:** 0mm (flush); where unavoidable ≤15mm beveled full-width
2. **Handle height:** 850–1100mm (DE lower + AU upper bounds)
3. **Opening force:** ≤20N (AU standard — most demanding globally)
4. **Hardware type:** Lever or equivalent; operable with closed fist; ≤28 in-lb rotational torque (ICC A117.1-2017)

### Conflict resolution
No genuine inter-population conflict identified for this slug. All populations (MOB, VIS, NEU, DEM, PAIN, OFS, NDV) benefit from: lower thresholds, lower operating force, lever handles, and consistent handle height. The direction of improvement is monotonic — lower is better for all parameters. The only tension is practical: weather sealing, fire rating, and acoustic isolation may require some threshold height. The specification should state this as a conditional exception, not a default.

### Highest-ambition actionable specification
- Threshold: 0mm. Where weather/fire requires: ≤15mm beveled, with automatic retractable seal.
- Handle: lever, 850–1100mm, ≤28 in-lb torque.
- Opening force: ≤20N. Where closer required: power-assist or automatic opener.
- Door opener controls: 800–1000mm, ≤5N activation.

## 11. Session Notes

- **Pattern confirmed:** Threshold/door hardware is dense Tier 5–6 with minimal Co-1. The clinical OT connection is indirect (grip force → standard adoption).
- **FR opening force anomaly:** France permits 50N — 2.5× the Australian best practice of 20N. This is a genuine regulatory gap, not a measurement difference.
- **US handle height anomaly:** Upper bound 1220mm (48") is unreachable for many wheelchair users and contradicts AU's evidence-based 1100mm cap.
